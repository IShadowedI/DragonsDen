Contemptor Kevin's Combat Patrol Issue 50 Review

May 4th, 2026 via Goonhammer
50 issues, not counting premium issues and character add-on issues? Wow, that's a lot of materials we've gone through and we're only now at the last issue of the regular Ork Combat Patrol, the second half of the Deff Dread.  (The first half, of course, was last week in Issue #49).  Does this issue have more than just a paint guide and game for the Deff Dread?  Do we get any relevant narrative articles?  Let's find out!

Once again, thank you to Tabletop Battles and Hachette for the opportunity to review these models, magazines, and materials.

Narrative Materials

We have a big selection of Ork narrative bits in this issue, which is absolutely perfect because all of the Ork narrative bits in this issue relate to the hottest topic of conversation here on TTB and in the 40K community: War Zone Armageddon and its Orks (1).

We start off with a look at Ork culture: While it appears on first glance all Orks do is fight with everything. While at first glance this is appears to make the Orks uncultured, they still have acomplex identity. This is because each Ork belongs to a warband, which is part of a tribe, and then part of a clan, all united in the banner of the WAAAGH!!! and the Mag Uruku Thracka, Ghazghkull. Orks also have a money system, based on teeth. The biggest toof is the most valuable toof. Orks never grow broke because even the puniest Ork can punch himself in the face to free up loose change.

Flash Gitz. Credit: 40khamslam.

Orks also have "strange science," in which Ork ramshackle technology works for them but not anyone else. Some in the Imperium suspect that Ork technology run on an Orky psychic field, but no one is sure. Orks also believe in the power of color: Red is for speed (da red ones go fasta), yellow is for wealth, blue is lucky, purple is sneaky ("Never seen a purple Ork, 'ave ya? Exactly."), and Green is da best because Orks are green.

The issue also highlights the largest and most feared Ork clan: the Goffs. They wear drab black clothes because anything more flamboyant is "muckin' around or showin' off", while Goffik horned helmets are not. They send massive waves of Boyz against the armies of The Emperor, and it doesn't tend to go too well for them. Goffs are the Orkiest of Orks, much in the same way the Luna Wolves (whose armor and color patterns the Goffs adopted during the formation in M32) were the best of the Space Marine Legions. They're the stompiest, fightiest, and bashiest of them all. Goffs also use all sorts of contraptions, like Stompas.

Ork Boyz Part 2. Credit: Kevin Stillman

Next, we have the leader of the Goffs and the Orks in general: Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thraka, da Prophet of Gork and Mork. We get an overview of Ghazghkull's history, in which they got bonked n the head, had visions of great conquest, and off to Armageddon they went. Ghazghkull loves to get stuck into the battle and rip stuff to shreds with Gork's Klaw or machine gun them into itty bitty pieces with Mork's Roar. Ghaz has a faithful grot named Makari, whom this magazine points out is the luckiest grot who has ever existed. Allegedly, "Ghazghkull is deeply fond of Makari, and as nobody has the nerve to give Ghazghkull bad news, every time Makari dies, [Makari] is quietly replaced by a new lucky grot before [the previous] loss is noticed."

Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thrakka

Ghaz has also managed to best multiple would-be enemies; of those, Ragnar Blackmane came closest to killing him on Krognar by decapitating Ghaz.

The last bit of fiction this week is a story, about....the Kin of the Leagues of Votann! A Kin engineer named Ymnir is dealing with unexpected problems aboard his pioneer ship, the Orgrave, when a large crash hits the ship. Ymnir realizes that it's an Ork warship that's smashed into them and is looking for a scrap. Ymnir and some of his compatriots set about trying to blow the Ork vessel out of the Orgrave, but then they cut a hole into the Ork ship. This bought time for the Kin Pioneer ship to get free, while Ymnir and two other Kin were stuck aboard an Ork warship.

Hobby Materials

The issue comes with the second half of the Deff Dread, and a painting guide for the Deff Dread. The Painting Guide for the issue recommends the hobbyist basecoat the model with Abaddon Black, pick out metallic areas with Leadbelcher, and paint other areas Corax White, Averland Sunset, Wraithbone (the cloth and bone) or Mephiston Red before washing them with shade paints, and highlighting the metallics with Stormhost Silver. This will get you a good looking Deff Dread.

My technique was to do it like I painted my Deffkoptas: I did all the metallics first. I then picked out the weapon casings I wanted to be red, and painted them up in red. For the main armor color, I used multiple coats of Black Templar, focusing more on the center with each successive layer to really bring out the model's highlights.

Deff Dread WIP. Credit: Contemptor Kevin Stillman

I then got distracted and decided to repaint my Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thracka, that I painted back in 2021 (and which still makes the occasional appearance here on TTB) alongside my Deff Dread and Deffkoptas. I'm of the belief that every army needs a centerpiece hero and the basic troops in order to start, and that while "elite mechs" are *third* on the list of things for a Contemptor Kevin army, they are very much that: third.

Gaming Materials

We get the list of the Gordrang's Gitstompas Combat Patrol, including a full description of the models' loadouts. The issue also comes with Scenario 31: Something Stompy this Way Comes. In this issue, the Deff Dread fights to murder it's way up escalating challenges. This scenario is a boss rush scenario, and during the enemy player's command phase they spawn in a new challenge from a table. The initial challenge at stage 1 is 3 Legionaries and 5 Termagants, with an opposition unit being replaced if the Deff Dread defeats it. If the Deff Dread kills both of the opposition units, then during the Opposition Player's Command Phase a new unit spawns in.

Deff Dread. Credit: Rockfish

The Final Verdict

This is a pretty good issue, if maddeningly incomplete. No, I'm not talking about the fact you only get half of the Deff Dread in this issue: The other half is back in Issue #49 and if you get both issues, you'll get a Deff Dread for more than 50% off MSRP and with a bunch of well written instructions on how to use it in Combat Patrol games. No, it's the story of Ghazghkull that I think is incomplete. I do not believe it's fully compatible with Ghazghkull Tharka: Prophet of the WAAAGH!, but I would need to go back and re-read it to see just how much of the story this issue slightly erred on.

But honestly, you should still get Issue #49 and #50 for your new or expanding Ork army. The Deff Dread has enough pieces that you can loot an Imperium model into your army using the leftover bits, and with the money you save by buying it in Combat Patrol you can go listen to an audiobook like Ghazghkull Thrakka: Warlord of Warlords while you build and paint them up.

Until then, Combat Patrolers (and Lore Dorks)!

-Contemptor Kevin Stillman

(1) As opposed to the Space Marines on Armageddon, which most people were comparatively unenthused by. That being said, I'm as excited for the new Intercessors as I am for the new Ork Boyz. It's just that the Intercessors and new Librarian were the only fun things in the Marine part of the box while everything the Ork side of the box is awesome, except for Grot sculpt #3. That one is kinda meh.
