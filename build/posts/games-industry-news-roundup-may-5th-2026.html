Games Industry News Roundup- May 5th, 2026

May 5th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Here at Tabletop Battles, we know that it’s hard to keep track of all the news happening all the time in the games industry. So much is always going on, all over the place, all the time. With games of all sorts, and their related media, being an informed gamer can feel impossible some days. 

That's why every week, we help you stay in the know by rounding up five of the biggest recent stories in the gaming and gaming-related pop culture sphere in the Games Industry News Roundup. Our trusty news boy, Dan “Swiftblade” Richardson, is right here at the very real, cool, and newly refurbished TTB newsdesk with the scoop.

Magic’s New One Ring Card Art Causes Firestorm of Controversy

Credit: Wizards of the Coast

Wizards of the Coast is in the hot seat this week following the reveal of the One Ring card for the upcoming Hobbit set. 

Wizards revealed the card as part of the larger showcase for The Hobbit set, which is scheduled to release this August. The rules are the same as the version first printed with the 2023 Lord of the Rings Magic set, and the new borderless art card is intended to be a throwback nod to its original print. As it turns out, the art is less of a throwback and more of a case of outright plagiarism of the original artist's work.

The version of the card printed for The Hobbit set is almost identical to the version printed with the Lord of the Rings set, save a few minor cosmetic differences like the letters being erased for The Hobbit version. The Hobbit printing of the One Ring also appears to be mirrored from the Lord of the Rings version, giving it a slightly different orientation. Key features of the new art, such as the colors, shadows, and reflections, are one to one with the 2023 original, putting to bed any claims of accidental similarities.  

Wizards of the Coast has since put out a statement, alongside the artist of the new One Ring card, Dan Frazier.

“I made a mistake, and I feel awful,” Frazier writes. “In trying to create an iconic version of The One Ring, while looking at references online, I ended up using Marta’s Ring as a reference and painted over it to try to depict the item fans hold dear to their hearts. In doing so, I didn’t make it my own."

Wizards comes to Frazier’s defense in the statement, writing, “None of this is intentional, but we are going to make it right as much as we can.” Wizards plans to credit both artists in the digital version of the Hobbit One Ring Card. Wizards doesn’t address in the statement if it plans to continue to work with Frazier in the future, nor does it plan to pull the card from the set, but has reached out to the original artists, Marta Nael, to apologize.

Donato Giancola, a former illustrator for Wizards, took to Facebook following the controversy to accuse Wizards of mistreating its artist teams, and reusing and recycling older artwork rather than commissioning a new piece. Frazier’s agent supports this claim that Wizards reused old art rather than working with something Frazier drew, writing, “After several changes requested we never saw this version.”

Wizards hasn’t commented on these accusations at time of writing. 

GameStop Preparing Offer to Buy eBay

Credit: Gamestop

Yes, you read that correctly. No, it isn’t supposed to be the other way around.

Last week, Gamestop announced that the company is planning to put out a bid to purchase eBay at $125 a share, for a total of nearly $56 billion.

GameStop is making this move in an attempt to become a “legitimate competitor” to online retail supergiant Amazon, alongside his wider attempt to reinvigorate GameStop following the company's declining performance over the past several years. 

In a letter to eBay leadership, GameStop proposes a plan for the acquisition that includes multiple ways that GameStop plans to cut costs at eBay to increase profits, as well as utilizing the physical storefront presence GameStop already has to drive eBay’s presence and sales. GameStop CEO Ryan Cohen also claims that they’ve already secured capital for the purchase. 

“GameStop had ~$9.4 billion in cash and liquid investments as of January 31, 2026,” Cohen writes. “The cash consideration will be funded from cash and liquid investments on GameStop’s balance sheet and third-party equity and debt financing, which will be fully committed at execution of definitive documentation. A highly-confident letter from TD Securities for up to $20 billion is attached.” 

This confidence on GameStop's part hasn’t changed the minds of nervous investors, however, as most of them are extremely dubious of GameStop’s ability to acquire a company far more valuable than their own in eBay. Stocks in GameStop also sharply dropped following the news, with their value dipping 10% and erasing the 8% jump when the news first broke.

eBay responded tepidly to the unsolicited offer from GameStop on Monday, writing, “The Board will review this proposal with a focus on the value to be delivered to eBay shareholders, including the value of the GameStop stock consideration and the ability of GameStop to deliver a binding, actionable proposal.” eBay plans to make no further comment on the offer at this time. 

Wizards of the Coast Declines to Voluntarily Recognize UWOTC

Credit: United Wizards of the Coast

We’ve got a double feature for Wizards of the Coast in this week’s roundup, just for you, dear reader. 

Last week, the development team for Magic the Gathering: Arena announced that they had passed a vote to unionize under the CWA, and had reached out to leadership at Wizards and Hasbro to begin negotiations. In their letter to leadership, the new union, the UWOTC, invited Wizards and Hasbro leadership to voluntarily acknowledge the union, and gave them until May 1st to do so before filing a claim with the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB).

The UWOTC released a statement on Monday that leadership at Wizards and Hasbro declined to voluntarily recognize the union by the stated deadline, and that leadership has made no attempt to contact the UWOTC leaders.

Wizards did release a public statement regarding the new union, which has been accused of using union busting language. Wizards leadership states, “We have received the filing and are reviewing it carefully. Our employees are the lifeblood of what makes us great, and we are committed to fostering a workplace where every person feels heard, valued, and supported. We believe we have a strong connection with everyone at Wizards of the Coast and that direct relationship with our employees is essential to how we work together to capture the imagination of our fans and players, inspiring a lifetime love of our games. We appreciate hearing about the needs and interests of our employees through this filing, and will respond through the appropriate process.”

“Management claims to be committed to ensuring every person is heard, valued, and supported,” The UWOTC wrote on Monday. “However, when we spoke up about our desire to unionize, they responded only to the press.”

The UWOTC elaborates further that while it is disappointed that corporate leadership has failed to recognize the union by the deadline, that the UWOTC has no plans to back down, writing, “We still maintain that the success of our union is inevitable.” 

The UWOTC received widely positive popular support when it was announced, and a public letter for fans to demand Wizards recognize the union has received 30,000 signatures as of Monday. 

“While we're disappointed in this delay, together we remain strong in our convictions,” UWOTC writes in the closing of their statement. “We look forward to sitting down at the bargaining table so we can make Arena better for workers, for Wizards, and for our players.”

Space Marine 2 Set for Third Year of Live Service Support

Credit: Focus Interactive/Games Workshop

On to less frustrating news, the development team behind Space Marine 2, Focus Interactive, is continuing live service support and development for the game for a third year.

“Space Marine 2’s live support has officially been extended into a third year, bringing even more free updates and DLC content,” Focus writes in a blog post. “Lately, our updates have been very well received by the community, which really motivates us to keep up the momentum for the third year.”

While Focus hasn’t yet announced what exactly will be a part of this third year roadmap, they’ve already noted that anything in the current release schedule that isn’t out in time will be rolled over into year three. Focus also makes it clear that the content released in year three will be free for all players outside of cosmetic upgrade packs.

Focus has been rightfully praised for its continued support for Space Marine 2 following its 2024 launch. The game has reached over 12 million copies sold worldwide, and was nominated for multiple continued support awards, including the “Labor of Love” Steam community award in 2025 and, most recently, a nomination at the 2026 BAFTA awards for “Best Evolving Game”. 

“We’re really excited about what’s coming,” Focus writes on their future plans for Space Marine 2, “and we think you will be too!”

Modiphius Announces XCOM Tabletop Game with Cryptic Website

Credit: Take 2 Interactive/Firaxis

Modiphius is expanding into yet another popular Video Game IP with the strategy series XCOM, creating a website for a tabletop version of the series as a part of Firaxis’s 30 year anniversary.

The website itself is spartan, and gives very little clues as to what exactly lies in store for the tabletop adaptation. As of this writing, all we currently have is a logo, as well as email newsletter signups for UK/EU and NA regions.

While we are largely in the dark as to what this announcement means, tabletop news site Wargamer does note that the game is currently listed under the “Five X” series of games. These games, which include titles such as Five Leagues from the Borderlands and Five Parsecs from Home, all have an emphasis on cooperative play against ever increasing threats to the players, which does track with XCOM’s gameplay loop of pitting a team of elite soldiers against increasingly dangerous alien threats.

Aside from this information, we have no additional information about the XCOM miniatures adaptation. Modiphius is also staying tight lipped, and has not commented on Wargamer’s observations or made any statements or announcements regarding the new project.
