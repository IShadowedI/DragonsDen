MTG banlist updates and announcements - May 2026

May 1st, 2026 via Wargamer

To keep the game fair and fun, Wizards of the Coast regularly updates its MTG banlist, a compilation of cards that're banned (or restricted to a limited number of copies) in a constructed deck. Read on for a summary of the latest Magic banlist update; the complete, up-to-date banlists for each format; and all the rules information you need to know on banned cards.

We also have a separate guide to the MTG Commander banlist, if that's the only format you're interested in. Check out our guide to the MTG release schedule to keep track of the big events likely to shake up the format, too.

Here's the latest MTG banlist news:

  • Latest MTG banlist update
  • Next MTG banlist update
  • MTG banlist for every format
  • Universally banned MTG cards
  • MTG banlist FAQs

Latest MTG banlist update

On March 23, 2026, Wizards of the Coast added Food Chain to the Historic banlist. That's it. One measly card for now!

Next MTG banlist update

The date of the next MTG banlist has not been announced, but we'd expect it around June or July 2026. That's because Wizards  promises one banlist a year, and the last big one was June 2025.

That said, predicting the next Magic bans is a tricky business these days. WotC is still theoretically running the 'once a year' plan - but we have also had some of those 'emergency bans' pop up since the firm adopted it. The best steer we have is that Wizards now tries to place bans in the first few weeks after a new set releases - so checking the release dates can provide a good guide.

Historic banlist

The MTG Arena format Historic also has a unique approach to MTG card bans. Sometimes, a card is 'suspended' instead of banned.

This is sort of a holding cell for potential bans. While you can't use a suspended card, Wizards says it plans to make use of the flexibility of a digital format to shuffle cards on and off the suspended list on a regular, and somewhat experimental basis. In contrast, full-blown bans are intended to be more permanent and considered.

Then there's a list of nine cards that refer to 'ante'. This was a way of playing Magic, back in MTG's earliest days, where you would essentially 'gamble' your cards on the outcome of a game. It was largely unpopular and soon scrapped, with the following cards banned across all formats:

  • Amulet of Quoz
  • Bronze Tablet
  • Contract from Below
  • Darkpact
  • Demonic Attorney
  • Jewelled Bird
  • Rebirth
  • Tempest Efreet
  • Timmerian Fiends

The MTG banlist also includes a number of cards that have been deemed racially or culturally insensitive. This is a universal ban that applies to sanctioned tournaments in all formats, and the cards' images were removed from Wizards of the Coast's database in 2020. MTG banlist FAQs

Are MTG Arena bans different?

Card bans affect MTG Arena just as they do paper Magic - so the above MTG banlists apply to Arena too. The main difference is, when your rare cards get banned in MTG Arena, you get something back for it.

When cards you own on MTG Arena are banned, you get wildcards back of an equal rarity, which you can then spend on new cards. It's a pretty good deal - in fact, in a funny way you could say that card bans are one of the main free sources of wildcards on the platform.

Why are Magic cards banned?

Wizards of the Coast's official reason for having a banlist is "to help maintain the diversity and health of the Magic tournament environment". In short, if some cards are more obviously powerful, everyone will play them. This pushes out players who don't enjoy playing that particular kind of deck, and games will quickly become same-y. 

There's a second reason for the banlist hidden in the first. The fact is that Magic: The Gathering is one of the world's most complicated games, and even the designers can't keep track of how every single card interacts with one another. Despite rigorous playtesting, cards that threaten the balance of a format can still slip through. Bans keep Magic playable. 

When are Magic cards banned?

Since August 2024, Wizards of the Coast has said MTG banlists should happen once a year, except in rare, particularly urgent circumstances.

However, in June 2025 we got an unexpected wave of bans in what WotC called its "mid-year ban announcement" - followed by a single card 'emergency' ban in March 2026. So it's hard to know whether we should be expecting one new banlist a year, or more. We're sort of hoping for a clarifying update from Wizards on the ban policy - and we'll update this guide if and when that happens!

Who decides which Magic cards get banned?

The MTG banlist for all sanctioned formats are managed internally by Wizards of the Coast. For most formats, there's not much public information available on the individuals who make these decisions, but we do know that MTG's Play Design team has some say in what cards are banned.

It's also worth noting that, while bans for the Commander format are ultimately decided by Wizards of the Coast, there is an external Commander panel that collaborates with Wizards on ban decisions. This was founded after Wizards took over management of the format from the Commander Rules Committee, who had been the target of harassment after one particularly controversial banlist decision.
