Preparing for the Taurus Cup in Umamusume: Pretty Derby

May 11th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Hard to believe that it was only nine months ago that we published our very first article about PVP and the original Taurus cup, but here we are at the beginning of a “new” year in Umamusume Pretty Derby with the rerunning of the Taurus Cup and a revamp of the Champions Meeting ranking systems. If you’ve been competing from the start, then you know what to expect from Champions Meetings, but if you happen to be a first timer, the Taurus Cup is both well trodden ground and also a new frontier in numerous ways. We’ll talk about the changes to the ranking systems before diving into the race itself, and you can decide if you’d like to challenge Open or Graded this time around on the green!

If you’re very new to Umamusume: Pretty Derby, the current scenario, Trackblazer, provides some unique challenges to how Champions Meetings work if you don’t have a premium deck and well established set of trainees and parents to pull from. But that’s okay, there’s no time like the present to start when it comes to Champions Meetings, and why not get a fresh start with the very first cup all over again! If you’ve got questions, we have a guide about Trackblazer, the original Taurus Cup and PVP, as well as numerous other guides that can hopefully give you a hand when it comes to getting more out of your time with Umamusume.

The New Graded and Open Leagues

Before we dive in to the second running of the Taurus Cup itself, a little update about Open and Graded Leagues. Originally, Open League allowed trainees up to B, and now, A+ is the cut off. This means that the entire Open League meta is going to shift heavily, but it also means that you may be able to consider competing in it with a few caveats if you are unable to regularly meet the S and more than likely S+ or higher that Graded League is going to start fielding. One of the problems with Open League is that while it has a “lower” cap than Graded, actually training an effective “low rank” trainee meant having a very strong knowledge of min-maxing the game. Now that players have had a lot more time to play the game and also with the change of stats and the easier time of hitting various stat goals, making an A+ trainee isn’t actually as restrictive as an effective B rank Trainee was.

Graded Rank will remain the land of whales, so don’t expect a lot to change there. In fact, you probably will see almost exactly the same types of trainees and set ups there for most CMs, with very little change or shifting of the meta from the original Japanese runnings of the races; if anything, the Global version of the game really hasn’t managed to distance itself that much from the spectre of the Japanese meta, even if we were more hopeful that the early balance patch in November would have done so. While it did shift things a bit (Christmas Oguri isn’t QUITE as dominant as she used to be), it really didn’t move the needle that much. So, if you think Graded is out of your league (sorry), try Open! You may find that it can be an easier and more accessible time and hitting A+ with a trainee is far easier now than it used to be. For that reason, I’ll be including some tips on stats for Open league from here on out; in most cases, Skill choices are the same in either Open or Graded, so really it is more the stats that matter.

The Return of the Taurus Cup: Second Verse, Same as the First



The Taurus Cup is a 2400m Medium Race run from the Left during Spring in Sunny Weather on Firm Conditions, functionally identical to the original race in almost every way. While the stats of the race haven’t changed, many other things have, from the types of competitors that are able to run it and the skills that are now available, to the huge gap in stats from the original race till now (Feeling nostalgic? We suggested 1000 speed, 700 power and 800 stamina!). The keywords of very helpful Green skills remain the same too, with Firm Conditions, Tokyo Racecourse, Left-Handed, Spring Runner, Standard Distance and Sunny Days making up the list of usual suspects.

Firm Conditions is still one of the most widely available Green skills in the game, and with the release of Nishino Flower as a trainee/parent option, you have a lot of ways to potentially obtain the skill either through cards or inheritances. Firm Conditions is probably one of the skills you should always shoot for on your trainee when looking at Greens, especially for Front Runners, simply because it is so easily available that you can consider a pretty sure bet. If you’re training a Front Runner, you could even consider trying to snag Firm Course Menace from Narita Top Road’s SSR events.

Tokyo Racecourse on the other hand remains pretty restrictive, only appearing on Vodka’s SR and SSR, as well as inherited from Ines Fujin, Special Week, Eishin Flash, or Winning Ticket. Spring Runner is another oddball, only available from Inari One Power, Sakura Chiyono O Stamina, and innately on Mejiro McQueen, Sakura Chiyono O, Cheerleader King Halo, Ballroom Seiun Sky, Anime Teio and Kitasan Black. These two are a lot harder to obtain if you aren’t specifically going to try inheriting them from your various parents, so if you’re trying for Groundwork you may want to secure your other greens before looking for these. Spring Runner also has a Gold version you can inherit from Cheerleader King Halo and Sakura Chiyono O called Spring Spectacle, by the way.

Left-Handed Is another skill that hasn’t changed much in terms of availability, although it is a bit more widely available than it was the first time Taurus Cup ran. You’re still looking to a specific set of cards, being Agnes Digital Power, Mejiro Ardan Wit, any Silence Suzuka card (including the Team Sirius card), or Zenno Rob Roy Speed. You can also try to inherit it off of a Mejiro Ardan or Silence Suzuka parent/grand-parent, which may be easier as none of those cards are particularly meta (although Team Sirius can be good if you aren’t training any of them).

Standard Distance is another fairly common skill, and primarily comes from Nishino Flower’s cards, as well as Kawakami Princess Power, El Condor Pasa Power, Eishin Flash Speed, Bamboo Memory Power, and Ines Fujin Wit. Sadly the cards that provide this aren’t great, and Nishino Flower Power is probably the best card in this pool. Finally, Sunny Days can be obtained from El Condor Pasa Power, Satono Diamond Stamina, Mejiro Dober Wit, and Hishi Akebono Guts; if you’re trying to get numerous Greens, you could look to run El Condor Pasa and target both Standard Distance and Sunny Days for maximum efficiency.

Trainees to Consider

One of the biggest changes to Umamusume: Pretty Derby since we first spoke of Taurus Cup is the amount of trainees that are viable and available. When we first ran our coverage, the field was extremely narrow, and in most cases, heavily dominated by Gold Ship. Now, the meta has changed quite a lot, and while Gold Ship remains a very strong competitor, there’s been quite a lot of shifts in who you can or should consider training. There’s also been some big changes in Debuff strategies, particularly with the skill Dominator now being available. We’ll list some of the best options here depending on your playstyle and availability.

Although she just released, Mejiro Palmer is a great option if you have her, especially as she can do Runaway strat.

Free to Play: The good news is that Gold Ship remains one of the best trainees you can bring to Taurus Cup, and remains a pretty solid trainee for most Medium and Long Distance races. The others are sadly vastly outclassed this time around except for Daiwa Scarlet, and unless you want to heavily invest in Sakura Bakushin O or Haru Urara, you are probably better off putting your money on Gold Ship as your ace.

Some Pulls: Some of the 2-stars available remain very solid choices across the board, with the best ones being . In fact, Mejiro Ryan is considered a top competitor in this race, so if you have her at 3 Star or above, she’s worth considering as a trainee in the Late Surger position; her innate skill, Let’s Pump Some Iron, is an amazing last distance unique. You could also look into Gass wonder, Nice Nature, or Agnes Tachyon for either Debuffers or aces, if you like. Since there are a lot of options here, I’d say only consider El Condor Pasa, Super Creek or Winning Ticket if you had no other options and are in need of a third, but a team of Ryan, Gold Ship, and one of the other 3 as a Debuffer would go over well.

Three Stars: We’re approaching an overload of 3 Stars that are viable, and one of the key tenets of Umamusume is that anyone you like is generally pretty viable to use, so you should just go with whoever is the strongest competitor you own that you really want to see do well. That said, Christmas Oguri, Maruzensky (both versions), Valentine Bourbon, OG Seiun Sky, and Mejiro Dober were the originally dominant 3 Stars, and you can expect to see them here again. If we talk more about positions, expect to see those and Ines Fujin and Silence Suzuka as Front Runners, along with the possibility of Runaway Suzuka and Mejiro Palmer. Pace Chasers don’t really do well in this race but you can expect to see Christmas Oguri there sometimes. The real competition is in the Late Surger category where you’ll see Mejiro Dober, Tamamo Cross, Kawakami Princess, Oguri Cap, and a few others.



A special mention is Cheerleader Nice Nature, who recently debuted in Global. Although she’s quite strong as a competitor in her own right, Cheerleader Nice Nature is the Upgrade to Nasty Nature, making her even stronger as a Debuffer and giving her more access and viability to skills, especially with Dominator. You may want to consider building a Debuffer if you have Cheerleader Nice Nature, and if you don’t, you may just want to consider trainees that can recover from a very likely to be heavy Debuffer field; Global Umamusume fans have shown that they really like Debuffing.



Christmas Oguri Cap is an interesting trainee. In the Japanese meta, she was absolutely dominant for most of her runs in Champions Meetings, but in Global Meta, she hasn’t been quite as dominant as one might expect; some of that may just be due to players already knowing what to expect or perhaps the Balance Patch back in November of 2025, but whatever the cause, this particular CM is Christmas Oguri Cap’s to take. Her Unique Skill is very easy to activate in this race, and she can overcome the Dominator Debuffers easily due to her multiple recovery skills and various other benefits. That said, while she is likely to be very strong, it really is hard to call as to whether or not she’s going to dominate like she did in Japan, so don’t feel like you don’t have a chance without her; Regular Oguri Cap can work really well too, and both are easy to train in Trackblazer, making them great choices for that reason.

Stats and Skills

The Stats you’ll want to shoot for in Graded League are probably what you can expect of CMs of late, which is very high and almost approaching stat caps in numerous categories. Most very competitive Aces are going to want 1200 Speed, 1000 Power and 1000 Stamina, 600 Guts and 900-1000 Wit, a fairly brutal mixture that requires excessively good rolls during Trackblazer runs and very specifically chosen decks (and luck. Lots of luck and patience). If you’re considering trying Open League, you’ll find that the stats are… actually a bit more comparable than you expect, but can be a little forgiving: 1200 Speed, 900+ Stamina, 900 Power, 500 Guts, and 600 Wit, the last two of which are pretty easily obtainable. The biggest challenge with Open League is riding the line of getting to these numbers and NOT exceeding them, because moving your trainee from A+ to S makes the unable to compete and yet also not good enough for Graded either.

You’re going to want at least 1 recovery skill, preferably a Gold skill that fits your build or Ace well. Swinging Maestro remains the best overall recovery skill you can aim for, but you can also try to make use of Miraculous Step or Trackblazer (for Front Runners), Gourmand or Race Planner for Pace, Go-Home Specialist or Sleeping Lion for End, and Lie in Wait or Indomitable for Late. If possible and it fits your budget, consider at least 1 gold and 1 White that can compliment it, especially if you feel you are going to have trouble hitting the Stamina cap. While anything can happen, you should probably expect a Debuffer to be in your race, if not 2, and their damage to your Stamina overall can really mess up your plans if you can’t recover enough to overcome it.

Since there’s a decent amount of Stamina and Power required here, you may also want to consider a deck that consists of 2 Speed cards (one being Kitasan Black MLB), a Power card, 2 Wit, and the shocking return of Riko Kashimoto! While Riko doesn’t provide a scenario benefit, her passive Guts and Stamina growth and skills are what make her valuable, but if you’re worried about Swinging Maestro and think you need it, take Super Creek instead, especially if you have one that is LB3 or higher. The other skills you’ll want to keep an eye out for are the same as usual, such as Groundwork for Front Runners, Professor of Curvature, It’s On, Beeline Burst, Playtime’s Over, and many of the other solid gold skills from previous CMs. Triumphant Pulse from Oguri Cap, Let’s Pump some Iron from Mejiro Ryan, and Lights of Vaudeville from Fuji Kiseki are all great uniques to look to inherit as well, especially Oguri and Ryan if you’re running a Late or End Ace.

Reset the Calendar

Taurus Cup is back, and with it the new season of CMs are upon us. The next one following this is a rerun of Gemini Cup in name only, as that race is actually a Mile challenge this time around, meaning you’ll need a whole new strategy for it if you never tried it the first time! Until then, If you have any questions, please feel free to shoot them over to me at marcy@goonhammer.com, or drop by the Discord and ask me there!
