Mayday, Miss Marcy! Much Ado About Sprue, Salt Levels, and Gaming Attire

May 14th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome back once again to Mayday, Miss Marcy, in which by now you likely know the drill: I take reader submitted questions, spin up an answer, and then see if they pass muster with the very same readers. Sometimes, I even hear back from them! The march of time continues ever onward into the Summer months, and as we get hotter (physically and metaphorically), the questions remain an ever even temperature. But how do you get your questions answered on this very website? Well, by a somewhat simple process of choosing one of three convenient options!

Have a question you want answered on just about anything you’d need advice on in this grand hobby space of ours? Board games, 40k, AOS, TTRPGs, MTG, anything and everything that we cover, we can help with!

For some examples, take a look at previous editions of our column at this link with a handy tag to see all previous questions and answers!

If you want to submit questions, we have a few ways you can do it. Patrons can use the Discord bot command to ask questions, which will be received anonymously!

If you’re not a Patron, or just don’t want extra steps, you can comment on this article, or e-mail me directly at marcy@tabletopbattles.com!

Last week, we answered questions about whether or not the gamers that don't talk to one another were a couple and how to manage anxiety over the ever changing tides of time, and this week, I have another set of three questions for you. Curious to see what they are? Well, let's get down to business then!

What's To Do With Sprue

Miss Marcy,
Due to a series of strange events, I have ended up with a few extra spare sprues, most of a (single) large Warhammer model. Specifically, I have extras of 2 of the 3 sprues needed to build it, but I don't expect to use any part of them. These sprues make up most of the model's structure, and I'm sure someone could make a nice kitbash using them, but that isn't something I have time or a desire for. Ideally, I'd give them to somebody who will use them or at least trade them for more useful bitz, but I'm not sure where I can find someone who can use them.
The Discord server run by my FLGS is probably the easiest place to find people who could put the extras to use, but I'm worried it would rub the lovely people at the FLGS the wrong way If I offered to give away 2/3 of a kit, especially given that they have it the kit in stock and I didn't get the original kit from them (I have bought a fair share of other stuff from them, though). I could also periodically go in and try to see if anyone there in person wants them, but I think that might also be a faux pas. Their store is wonderful and I don't want to do anything that would impede their business, but I don't know how else I could find someone who has a good use for the sprues. Would putting it up for grabs on their Discord be a bad idea? And do you have any suggestions on how else I could find them a good home?
-Doesn't want to Sprue anything up
Dear Sprue,

I think you should just ask in the Discord server, and if anything, ask the mods of said Discord first, but that seems like a better way of offloading things than not. They might even realize that they could easily set up a trading / looking for channel for people to trade things and perhaps make some rules surrounding it. A few stores I frequent have rooms like this, often with the caveat that things have to be given away for free, non-shrinkwrapped, or similar. It sounds like you wouldn't be directly 'competing' with the LGS since you are giving away unfinished and incomplete items, rather than trying to undercut their new product. If they have no way of rehoming it themselves for Store Credit or something, I don't see why they would be unhappy with their customers engaging in trading or otherwise. There are of course exceptions and a lot of times stores do not like patrons doing under the table sales, but if your plan is to simply give them away, I would just ask 'hey can I post this?' If they say no, I would then suggest you check something like Facebook Groups in your local area to see if anyone would want to take them off your hands.

Ideally the LGS will be happy to play ball, and as I said, maybe it can start people trading or swapping sprues or bits that are otherwise harder to get or purchase on their own. Yes, as we have had a few times in this article, LGS are businesses that need to make money, but if they make their business so unfriendly to their customers that a person giving another customer a sprue of miniatures for free is a major breach, you probably didn't want to frequent that LGS anyway, as all they see you as is a wallet with legs. I don't think it really matters where you bought the kit from, either; unless the shopkeepers have an exacting memory of every single thing you and everyone else has bought, what matters is that you are going "hey, I would like to offload some of these things, would anyone like them? I can bring them to game night". If you happen to have a group chat of players or people in the Discord that are that local group, you could also just DM them directly like "hey, I have 2/3 of a box of Marines I don't need, would you like them"? Ask for permission if you're feeling polite (or looking for money),  but if you're just handing it away for free, it really isn't their business.

Low Sodium Diet

Anyway, on to the question.
I've come to the end of my first competitive event, a Kill Team league over the past few months, and to put it bluntly I did terribly. I'm no stranger to the game, but even though I learned a lot more about Kill Team in the process and enjoyed almost all my games played, I always ended up either barely losing or massively losing, sometimes losing harder as the league went on and my own play improved. The one game I did win was by accident because my opponent forgot which objective they were prioritising.
I've handled losing each time in good humor, making sure to be a good & fun player to play against and learning why I lost & trying to build on it so my opponents aren't stuck waiting while I go "Uh... hold on... I think I can do this?" but it's been tough. Either it turns out my opponent can do something that throws my plans out of whack, or their side of the board has much better cover than mine (I think I won the "choose which side you deploy on" roll once all league), or my luck turns sour at just the wrong time, or in plenty of cases I just make a bad play and get punished for it. Sometimes I've done everything I can and the only thing I've been able to take away from a game has been "don't roll badly next time".
My last game, however, I felt myself getting salty for the first time, and I didn't like it. I was playing against a strong team run by a great player, on an official map layout that even my opponent remarked on how terrible it was for me, and the more frustrated I got the more mis-plays and bad rolls I made. I spent the game getting wiped off the board by enemies I couldn't shoot back at, beefing the rolls I needed to make and having to run across open ground to get to objectives my opponent already had snipers pointing at by end of Turn 1. Thankfully I didn't blow my lid or anything, and I made sure to initiate the "good game" handshake afterwards, but I did feel myself locking up, going quiet and was probably visibly seething. Utterances like "Hmm. Yeah, that's... yeah." and "Right, well I guess all I can do is" started cropping up, and I felt like going and taking a breather because I'm getting tilted and not handling it well would have made me more of an ass than just taking the latest and biggest in a long series of Ls like a grownup. I'm autistic, and even seeming like I'm having a meltdown while playing with someone would be a nightmare scenario for me.
As far as I can tell the only person mad at me is me, even my opponent was sympathetic at my situation, and it's not like I'm going to stop playing Kill Team or anything, but I still feel like an ass. Is there a better way I can digest my salt next time without being so frustrated, wasting my opponent's time by going off for a breather when I'm losing, or making my opponent feel bad for kicking my ass so thoroughly?
Dead Sea Rolls
Dear Dead Sea Rolls,
I think the way to relieve yourself of fear of being over salted and a miserable opponent is that you signaled the fact that you were playing in a league in which everything went badly from the start. That mostly means that every single loss was a knock-on effect that kept getting passed down the line, and when you reached this last game, it all came calling because there would be no further games to stress about: you realized that you were miserable and would end the league miserable, and every loss and bump in the road you faced along the way came back to you in the moment to signal that nothing you did here mattered. This was further magnified by, as you and your opponent surmised, you were going to lose and have a bad time by the way the game was set up and designed. To use the familiar saying, "it was rigged from the start", and the fact that you still tried your best to be a good sport proves that you are not the type of person to rage out or get super morose and unpleasant.
One of the unfortunate sides of our hobbies (miniature, TCG, etc.) is that sometimes you are just going to lose a lot and you are going to have to learn to accept it. I think the best thing you can do when you are on a downward spiral of losses is to try and focus or compartmentalize about what types of things you could do better. There is a limit what you can learn from losing, though, that doesn't end up at a totality of "I should stop playing". I think it was valuable that you finished your league, even on the downside, and you shouldn't worry about being an ass. You don't sound like you were a jerk, and frankly, if you were feeling down during the game due to what was before you, it sounds like your opponent was at least sympathetic.
I do always think people who worry about being bad sports or miserable opponents are rarely that; you can have bad games and react badly to them within a spectrum of respectable responses. What people are often wary of more are people who are ALWAYS miserable, no matter what is going on. No one is going to be able to "have fun" losing all the time, or most of the time, but a person who is miserable regardless of winning or losing is the one you need to avoid and be wary of. Sounds to me like you are not that, just someone who had a bad league experience with Kill Team, and perhaps your next League will be a lot better!

Which Graphic Tee Today?

Marcy,
The letter you got a few weeks ago about the person with the hawaiian shirt and the naked girl markers made me take a moment to consider my own wardrobe. i don't really dress that badly, i think, but i primarily wear graphic t-shirts and jeans or shorts to game nights.
I am not looking to date, nor am I looking to win any sorts of fashion awards, but i guess since i am now in my early 30s... I am wondering if maybe i should be dressing better?
Dressed to distress
Dear Dressed,
I know maybe people are hoping for me to become the Fashion Lady for our website here, and while I do think dressing nicely and dressing better is a thing you can do for yourself to feel better or comfortable, I am going to point to your own words: "I am not looking to date . . . or win awards". Okay, so are you comfortable? Do your shirts have upsetting things on them? Do they fit? Are they clean? If all of the answers to this are "yes", then no one cares and neither should you.
There's nothing wrong with a graphic tee and jeans for a gaming day. There's really nothing wrong with a graphic tee and jeans in almost any situation that does not require more formal attire, frankly, and your age actually has nothing to do with it. About the only time that should matter is if you're wearing graphic tees of something you think people might look at you weirdly for, in which case you either own it and wear it proudly, or wear something different, but that has nothing to do with the clothes and more to do with your own comfort. If YOU like your shirts, wear them! They're YOUR shirts. You aren't trying to impress anyone.
The biggest thing I will say to avoid sounding like it is body shaming or similar is that you should really just focus on wearing shirts that are long enough and loose enough to not expose skin you don't want exposed or look like it will burst at the seams if you move wrong, don't have foul stains or are not distressed on purpose, and most importantly don't have things on them that would make people upset or question why you're wearing that in a public place. To the question above, the guy wearing Hawaiian shirts wasn't the problem, it was everything else. A Uniqlo anime t-shirt or a Hot Topic graphic tee of Helluva Boss is fine, no one cares. Just wear what you want, don't smell, don't look gross, and you're fine.
