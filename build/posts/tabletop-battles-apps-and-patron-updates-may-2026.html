Tabletop Battles Apps and Patron Updates: May, 2026

May 12th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Hey there, Dear Reader, it's me again - Robert "TheChirurgeon" Jones, CEO of Tabletop Battles. It's been a wild month, but I'm back with another update on all of the cool things we've been working on here with our apps - specifically updates in the Tabletop Battles app, our Edge competitive dashboard, and Administratum.

If you missed our updates last month - which included 500 worlds support in Administratum - you can find that review here.

Some - but not all - of the stuff we're going to be talking about here are updates for Tabletop Battles Patrons, some available at the $5 level, and the rest at the $10 level. If you're interested in supporting the site and making more updates like these possible - and more common - head over to Patreon and support us. And if you already support us, thanks - we appreciate it and love making new cool stuff for you.

There's a ton to cover here, from new apps and content to updates to Administratum, so keep scrolling if you want the full download.

Tabletop Battles App: Support for Spearhead Sand and Bone and City of Ash

One of the features I've had the most requests for was updates to Spearhead for Age of Sigmar. I'm happy to say that not only have we updated the app to support Spearhead Sand and Bone, we've gone ahead and added City of Ash as well, so you won't have to wait months for support for that game.

When you pop into the Tabletop Battles App, you can pick which Spearhead you're doing when you set up the game.



From there you just pick your factions normally, select your layout, and then proceed with the game. We've put in all of the cards so you're good to go.

Loading...

Return of Yarrick Detachments and Battle Formations

A minor update but still worth noting: All of the Return of Yarrick Detachments have been added as well so you can play your games using those. We've also updated the Age of Sigmar Battle Formations to include the new releases.

Next Up: Heresy Missions Packs

It has been brought to my attention there are more mission packs in Heresy we haven't implemented. So those are next on our list, along with support for 11th edition.

Site Updates

Something that's a lot less flashy but just as important to us is the website itself. When we rebranded to Tabletop Battles, we didn't just change URLs and site colors; we rebuilt the entire site frontend, making it faster and more responsive, and fixing image loads so they'd take less bandwidth.

One thing we added that we're particularly happy with are these buttons to change out the article feed on the front page. Clicking on one will show you articles for that specific game or group, without having to go sort through them elsewhere.



We also added that "Featured" Column as well to replace the old "Latest" column, which was just showing the same articles as the All Articles feed. Now it will show selected articles which aren't in the most recent articles, so there won't be any overlap, and if you missed a key article from the last week or two, you may find it there.

This is part of a big challenge for us - we publish 35 to 50 articles every week and it's easy for things to get lost in the shuffle. So we're always looking for ways to make things easier to discover and find after the week it was published.

Administratum: Imperial Agents

It's finally done - we finished adding support for every faction and Codex in Administratum last month with the addition of Codex: Imperial Agents. Now you can create threats, pull levers on them, roll to uncover or thwart them, roll plot twists, and there's a convenient little auto-decrement for Time Remaining when you want it.

[video width="2064" height="1826" mp4="https://assets.tabletopbattles.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/impagentsdemo-1.mp4"][/video]

We're pretty excited to have actually finished implementing every codex before the edition ended. We think we did a pretty great job this time around getting everything in there and while we haven't heard much on Crusade for 11th edition, rest assured those old rules will still work. We'll keep supporting these systems for as long as it makes sense, and we're not above doing some conversion work on our own end if need be so you can still use those old materials with 11th edition.

Subscriber-Only Content

We've continued to publish weekly content for our $10 Subscribers - high-quality, in-depth competitive content, written by some of the best 40k players in the world. Since then we've published a ton of content, going into detail on specific factions and units, strategic concepts, and as always, answering specific questions from our $10 patrons.

Here are a few of my favorite articles we've published in the last few weeks:
  • Using The Matrix Builder to Plan for Teams Events
  • Advanced 40k Tactics: Planning Your First Three Turns
  • Picking and Building Your Core Units for Chaos Space Marines
  • Playing From Behind: A Guide to Catching Up
  • Astra Militarum: How Many Tanks?
Again, this is just the tip of the iceberg and there's plenty more to dive into, both in terms of what we've already written, and what's coming up next.

Podcasts and Zines

Also for our $5 Patrons, we've got regular podcasts - the Tabletop Battles crew publish about a half dozen podcast episodes per month on our Patreon. This past month on our HAMS Radio podcast we talked about 40k reveals for 11th edition, how we'll plan narrative events for 11th, Trench Crusade, StarCraft, and what's next for the site.

And Just In Case You Needed More...

That's an insane amount of stuff to get for your $10 or even $5 subscription, and it's all added value. 

Not only do you get access to everything mentioned above - hell, you make it possible for us to build this stuff - you also get to remove ads here on Tabletop Battles (I seriously hate that we have to run these to keep the lights on), plus access to our Discord, early access to ticket sales for our events, and custom features in Administratum and the Tabletop Battles App like changing your avatar, pregame question checklists, and Forecast Mode, which lets you forecast the outcome of a game without changing it.



Tabletop Battles is a creator-owned business. We're a team of dedicated enthusiasts looking to take this ragtag operation from a fun blog to a real business making apps and content you can't find anywhere else. And we want to pay the people responsible for making these apps and content, so they can continue to do this and put their time and energy into it. So if you like what we do and you want to see more of it, smash that orange button and come support us.

Have any questions or feedback? Drop us a note in the comments below or email us at contact@tabletopbattles.com. Want articles like this linked in your inbox every Monday morning? Sign up for our newsletter. And don't forget that you can support us on Patreon for backer rewards like early video content, Administratum access, an ad-free experience on our website, and subscriber-only content covering competitive Warhammer 40K!
