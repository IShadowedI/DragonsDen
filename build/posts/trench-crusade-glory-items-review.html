Trench Crusade: Glory Items Review

May 15th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Good day, trenchers. Whether you're playing in campaigns, tournaments, or just pickup games, you'll be taking the time to consider what to spend your hard-earned ducats on. Beyond that however, you'll accumulate glory - most campaigns will start with little to none, and other events and pickup games will often give you a restricted amount. These can be spent on powerful Mercenaries to bolster your roster, Battlekit items directly from your armory, or items from your Glory Item table. While the access to this will vary from event to event, we'll be reviewing all of these Glory Items primarily from the perspective of picking them up in a typical campaign environment. We already covered Mercenaries here if you want to see details on them, and most Glory-costed Battlekit have been covered in their respective faction focuses, so we'll be specifically cracking down on what the specific Glory tables have to offer.

Jabirean Alchemist Credit: @Secretly_Paul on Instagram

In typical review style, I'll be giving everything a rating on the following criteria:

S: This item is unrivalled in power or value, and can easily be given priority over other options.

A: Generally a great pick for most scenarios, often can't go wrong with it, although might be outclassed by niche picks.

B: A solid choice for specific niche scenarios or a decent enough value pick, but often is outclassed by other options.

C: Really only good in a particular use-case and not highly regarded outside of it.

F: Overpriced, underperforming, and generally not recommended

For the sake of clarity, I'll be going over all of the items alphabetically in order, but noting what factions have access to them as many items are available to multiple warbands, even across both allegiances. It's worth noting that the value of an item can change in the context of different factions, so I'll note when that's the case. One other quick foreword - while we aren't specifically going to talk about the other things you can spend glory on here, I may reference them as they are often in competition with how you want to spend your Glory.

Glorious Loot

Grail Knight. Credit: MildNorman

Armour of Cobar - 8 Glory - Heretic Legion

ELITE only, -3 Injury Modifier makes this a pretty appealing option right off the bat, and it also grants a Blessing marker to the wearer any time a friendly unit is taken Out of Action. The drawback? You can't take a shield, and you get -1 DICE to Dash actions. This is still a solid pick for something that you'll be supporting with a musician or just don't care about dashing - a heavy hitting Chorister or even a really annoyingly entrenched Priest. It's not super cheap and has a notable drawback, but this will give some serious beef to anything that wants to hang on to it.

Rating: B, as the cost is still quite steep and you'll need to invest in support unless you want to move like a snail.

Armour of the Fly - 7 Glory - Black Grail

ELITE only, Reinforced Armor that also grants NEGATE SHRAPNEL and FLYING is already a decent package. In addition, it grants you 8" of movement instead of whatever your base movement is. The cherry on top? You can make an additional attack at +1 DICE after you fight, but this only hands out blood markers if you hit rather than making an injury roll.

This is a solid package, adding some great mobility to some models that can be terrifying with added speed and agility. A built in combat helmet and some extra attacks to boot make this a solid option. 7 Glory is still not on the cheaper side, but for making your Lord of Tumors a walking nightmare, it's not a bad deal.

Rating: A

Battlefield Title - 5 Glory - New Antioch, Heretic Legion, Court of the Seven, Black Grail

ELITE only, pick an item that has a LIMIT from your armory. Increase the limit by one, even when the model that has the title bites the dirt. The value of this depends on a few things - first and foremost, your faction and what you have access to will change how you rate this. Beezlebub's Axe, Sacrificial Blades, or any weapons of the flame variety are all welcome additions to have in the roster, but importantly you'll want to have a roster wide enough to carry these. This means that this will increasingly become important in the latter part of a campaign and can be easily worth it to double down on some key gear, but early off you're probably better buying a more proactive item.

Rating: B, as it pretty heavily depends on how you're kitting out your team and how wide your roster goes, but when it's needed it'll pay itself back in spades. Also, you don't get the item, just increasing the limit, so this can overall be an investment.

Beelzebub’s Embrace - 12 Glory - Black Grail

ELITE only, this one does...a lot of stuff. I'm just going to put the full description here:



Listen, this probably isn't the most efficient way to spend your glory. What it is, however, is absolutely rad. Make your character (likely a Lord of Tumors here) into an absolute melee meat grinder. While the obvious dream here is just one-shotting anything you touch that's on a 32mm base or smaller, the reality is that you're going to have a good chance to just down things beforehand.

Is this better than a normal build for a LoT? Personally, I don't think so, especially not for the cost, but I do think this is a hell of a lot of fun.

Rating: C in terms of seriousness, S in terms of fun and raw damage spiking potential.

Bestial Skin Cloak - 6 Glory - Court of the Seven

ELITE only, lets you take a bonus from a selection of options once per turn. You can add a DICE to either a melee attack roll, a melee attack's injury roll, or a DASH action. Notably, Hunters of the Left Hand Path can't use Oracle Beast Cloak (the goetic spell that negates an injury roll) and this in the same turn.

This is great, I think it's a fantastic pick on a Praetor or kitted out Hell Knight. These are going to be buffs that just make you better at what you want to be doing - movement shenanigans, and clobbering people in CQB.

Rating: S, I might overrate this slightly but I think this is absolutely great, especially in warbands that really want the Praetor to be the star of the show.

Book of Battle Prayers - 7 Glory - New Antioch

ELITE only, makes it so the user cannot dash but they can take an action (NOT a risky action) every turn, and on a success/critical success, you can put a blessing marker on anything within 12" of the model.

There are two use cases for this. First, you can use this as a supporting tool on a model you want to sit in the middle of your forces and spread buffs around (likely a Cleric) and accept that the mobility loss means you may have to then move your other pieces a little slower. The second is putting this on a frontline tank and just have them constantly put these on themself, maybe something like a Machine Armored Cleric. You'll be slow as hell, but a serious brick wall.

Rating: C, I think it can be a hefty price to play if you aren't playing a more stationary force, but in the right castle-focused setup this can give you a lot more staying power.

Cruel Helmet - 2 Glory - Court of the Seven

Wretched only, Limit 2. I'm going to jump the gun on this and say I'm not a fan. The idea is that you can use this to punch your Wretched and generate an extra blood token on the first turn, but they start down and it's going to make them painfully useless to move around the board with HEAVY. If your list really requires a functioning blood battery on turn 1, it's an option for you, but I think investing anything beyond the bare minimum in Wretched just feels a bit bad.

Rating: C, as much as I personally don't like this, I recognize the scenario in which you would want this. As long as people are hotboxing their wretched with gas grenades though, this is pretty rough to sell.

Cup of Filth - 4 Glory - Black Grail

Lord of Tumors. Credit: MildNorman

No restrictions besides the Limit of 1 that applies to most things here. Notably, aside from what the effect might lead you to believe, this is NOT a consumable.

Before the start of the game, pick one ELITE or 4 Thralls to drink from the cup. For the rest of the game, give them +1 DICE to dash actions. This is fantastic - the versatility of this not being tied to the model that has it equipped on top of being able to just give something a permanent instrument buff is great. Movement wins games, and dashing consistently can really make so many strategies come to life.

Rating: A, you may not need this in a more elite force that has good instrument coverage, but I think you'd be hard pressed to not find value here.

Damascus Armour - 5 Glory - Iron Sultanate

ELITE only, gives you IMPERVIOUS stapled on to Reinforced armor, while additionally letting you take an extra battle scar before becoming unfit for duty. Ignoring armor piercing can really just dumpster the anti-armor capabilities of some rosters, and the extra survivability can make it a safer investment into one of your characters you think might be taking a hit. Alchemist Armor is really good and in some scenarios might actually be better than this, but that being LIMIT 1 means you can make two super durable characters with ease.

Rating: A, only less of a priority due to it having slightly less value in earlier campaigns, both due to less armor piercing weapons as well as less necessity for the battle scar increase.

Demonic Aura Grenade - 3 Glory - Heretic Legion

Limit 1, but no restrictions besides that. This is effectively a gas-less gas grenade that can fully ignores line of sight when determining the blast, but deals double blood markers. I think this has some sleeper value in later campaign stages where gas masks will become more common and negate the power of gas grenades, but aside from that it's the same use-case as those - toss them in, then clean up with melee or other hard hitting support.

Rating: B, early campaign these are probably not as worth it as just buying gas grenades, but later on they're a great upgrade.

Donkey’s Jawbone - 4 Glory - Trench Pilgrims

Elite only. One handed. Melee. +2 Injury Rolls.

Yeah, this will just beat the crap out of basically anything. If you don't plan on putting an Anti-Tank Hammer on something, this is a viable substitute. Won't have as much raw killing power into things with armor 2+, but generally more consistent/less risky and frees up a hand. Absolutely worth the cost for one of your melee focused ELITES. While it's slightly wasted on a STRONG model like a Communicant or upgraded Castigator, they're both solid platforms if you don't want to get your Prophet's hands dirty - and if you do, you know what to equip them with.

Rating: S, in my opinion this is always worth grabbing as it's just miles better than pretty much any one handed weapon you can get otherwise. Spoiler - Pilgrims also have the smallest pool of Glory Items to pick from, so the competition is a little weaker.

Ducal Winged Armour - 8 Glory - New Antioch

Can only go on a model with Knighthood or a Battlefield Title, so is inherently ELITE locked from this. -3 Injury Modifier, grants FLYING and 10" of movement. In addition, treat Down results as Minor Hits on the injury table. This is unparalleled mobility for the faction, and frankly the fact that you aren't shield restricted with this is pretty bonkers. 8 Glory is a lot, but there really isn't an actual downside here aside from the hefty cost, as you also have to invest a minimum of 12 glory to get this.

Rating: A, it's very powerful but you're investing a deceptively high amount to access this.

Executioner’s Axe - 6 Glory - Heretic Legion

This is an interesting one, as this isn't ELITE restricted. +2 Injury DICE (note: not a flat value), CLEAVE 2, and CRITICAL. Not Heavy either, which basically amounts this to a lighter but better greatsword/axe. The real interesting thing here is that this is sometimes not always a pure upgrade to a Sacrificial Blade, which are very powerful in their own right. This can still be a great buy on something like a Chorister if you don't want to run a shield on them, but additionally is fantastic on Anointed Heavy Infantry kitted to get in close. It's a lot to invest in non-elites, so I don't know how often I'm reaching for this on non-promoted goobers, but it's a fantastic piece of kit all around.

Rating: A, it's a great item but is only losing some brownie points because it still does have to compete with the easier to access and very powerful Sacrificial Blades.

Field Hospital - 10 Glory - New Antioch, Iron Sultanate

Credit: Raf Cordero

This one's a weird one. This just is added straight to your arsenal rather than tied to a specific model, and can only be actively used if you have an Alchemist, Combat Medic, or Sister of Saint Cosmas. At the end of the Trauma step in the campaign, you can pick a model in your force with one or more battle scars and roll 2d6. On a 3-6, nothing happens. on a 7+, you remove a scar. If you roll snake eyes, they gain an extra scar.

In my eyes, this is much more of a very late campaign luxury. Keeping your veteran models in the fight can be great, but this requires a lot AND a dice roll. I think you're better off investing in things to try and help stay in the fight rather than recover from wounds, but this game is swingy enough that I can at least see a case for hanging on to one of these when your roster is really juiced up.

Rating: C

Great Banner of New Antioch - 12 Glory - New Antioch

HELD, while the holder is on the battlefield, choose to pass or fail morale checks. Listen, this could absolutely be my lack of experience with seeing this on the table, but I think this is bad. It's a huge investment of glory, and you either hinder a strong model or need to overinvest in a cheap model to make sure they don't get assassinated (which, against some factions, is a very real threat). Completely ignoring a core game mechanic like this has the potential to be really strong, but somewhat like above I'd often rather just invest in equipment to help win a game normally rather than this. It does make some silly infantry spamming lists viable though, which is a fun prospect.

Rating: F, I don't think this is worth the cost.

Holy Grenade - 2 Glory - Trench Pilgrims

How the average Pilgrim feels when reaching for one of these.

Limit: 3, a regular grenade that adds +1 Injury Dice against DEMONIC and BLACK GRAIL models. This will depend on the context of how you're playing - if you're just playing games regardless of intentionally matching faithful vs. fallen factions, this will likely carry a lot less weight. However, in an environment where you ARE doing that, this will help crack a lot of targets in 2/3 main factions you'll be fighting. It's cheap as well, so it's not too big of a loss even when it's less useful in some games.

Rating: C if you're not playing faithful vs. fallen settings, but a solid B if you are.

Horn of Joshua - 9 Glory - Trench Pilgrims

Limited to 1, but unrestricted otherwise. This is another one I think is easier not to paraphrase:



This is absolutely wild. Blow up a terrain piece and blast everything on it with an injury roll, with an additional one if they fall more than 3" from it getting leveled. This is just downright absurd; luckily the once per game only triggers once it's successful, so you can keep trying until you get it off. That being said, this actually has some decent value beyond hurting enemies hunkered down in a terrain feature. Pilgrims are an army that wants to close the gap and get scrappy in melee - completely removing a blocking point is a great way to clear a path. This definitely requires some finesse to make use of, but I love the insane plays this can bring to the table.

Rating: Objectively, B, as it's a little on the expensive side. That being said, the explosive plays with this are something you find in few other tabletop games. It's an S in my heart for that reason.

Kilij - 2 Glory - Iron Sultanate

ELITE only, Limit: 2. It's a sword with +1 Injury modifiers, great stuff. Yes, the limit does mean you can just dual wield them, which absolutely slaps. For such a cheap price, these are a staple of IS and really make up for the lack of strong melee options in their standard arsenal.

Rating: S, for the price, you'll always find someone to jam one of these onto.

Knighthood - 4 Glory - Everything but Trench Pilgrims

ELITE only, The investment item, grants 1 glory at the end of the battle for each model with this that's on the battlefield. Note that it's still LIMIT:1 as well. In theory it's not horrible, but it highly depends on two factors:

1. The length of your campaign, as the value is much higher if you have more games available to rack up Glory with this

2. If you have models that you can be confident in surviving games, as Out of Action will make this worthless.

I can see the case for grabbing one of these early on a dork who sits in the backfield anyway, but I still stand by that this probably isn't worth it as it takes 4 games just to break even assuming nobody else is running one.

Rating: F, unless you have the ability to get more somehow, then it's a C.

Koraktor, the Great Tome of Hell - 8 Glory - Court of the Seven

Sorcerer only. Lets you purchase any goetic power for them that you could normally buy, including for other units and sins. This is additional to the limit of three you already have, letting you keep a net total of 4 powers. This is pretty awesome, as it lets you break the internal balance around the different sins. There's too many great options to get into here, but if you're utilizing a Sorcerer this can be a pretty huge power bump.

Rating: A, particularly because you can use this to get passive powers that don't have to compete with your other spells you need to cast.

Herald of Beelzebub Credit - Mildnorman

Locust Spitter - 6 Glory - Black Grail

No restrictions aside from Limit: 1. An 18" Range, 2-Handed firearm with -1 Injury Dice and Ignore Armor. It has a real paragraph of rules text that boils down to the creepy crawlies that you shoot from the gun bouncing around to the nearest visible target, friendly or enemy. The range will get shorter each bounce after the first, and it must keep going until you don't have a valid target.

This might be the item I have the least bead on, as this probably isn't great with a faction that doesn't have stellar long range and thus can often end up hitting your own things with this, however it can do an insane amount of damage if your opponent clusters up against one of these. The real answer is that it probably depends on how tight your terrain layouts are, but this has a really high ceiling in the right circumstances.

Rating: B, for "be careful with this thing."

Lordship of This World - 9 Glory - Court of the Seven

Praetors and Sorcerers only. This one is pretty wild; at the start of the game, after everyone has deployed, you can select a terrain piece up to 8"/8" that doesn't have any friendly models on it. You can move it up to 6" in any direction as long as it ends more than 1" away from other terrain, and anything on it moves with it. If you move it on top of an enemy unit, the owner gets to immediately redeploy it.

This one's equally as whack and awesome as the Horn of Joshua, but will also heavily depend on the type of board you play on. If you have the right terrain layouts this can be insane, but if you have a lot of layouts that often don't have pieces that players deploy on/close to, this might not be as powerful as you'd expect.

Rating: C, the capability absolutely is there for this to be a powerhouse, but it's very circumstantial to a lot of factors that are hard to account for in a generic rating system like this.

Masterwork Jezzail - 4 Glory - Iron Sultanate

Elite only. 36" range, +1 Dice, and Critical puts this somewhere between a regular Jezzail with Alchemical Ammunition and a Siege Jezzail in terms of base stats. It also shoots crazy bullets like the people from Wanted - you don't need to roll to see if you hit an ally that's within 1" of the target, and if you roll more than a 7 on the injury roll, you can make another injury roll for the closest enemy unit within [whatever you rolled]"-7".

This is a solid alternative to a Sniper or Siege Jezzail - it's not going to hit as hard as the siege naturally, but it's consistent and lets you play much more aggressively around your frontline units and punish clustered up enemies.

Rating: B, I think it's more of a sidegrade to your other options than a raw upgrade, but it's a very good sidegrade. It's probably not as good in most cases as an alchemical-ammo Siege Jezzail, but it's also not Heavy.

Piece of Silver - 12 Glory - Court of the Seven

Elite only. Before the game starts, set aside 3 D6 dice with a value of "6." At any point in the game that you are rolling dice besides an Injury or Bloodbath roll, you can replace a die before you roll it with one of these 6's. If you roll multiple dice, you then roll any others, but this die is treated as having rolled a 6. You can only use one of these per roll, and you can use them on any roll regardless of if the model holding it is involved. If the model holding it is taken Out of Action, however, you lose any extra dice you had set aside.

Consistency and rigging rolls is historically one of the most crazy things you can do in a game that naturally has a lot of variance, and this is no exception. This gives you the ability to make things like surefire charges and even guarantee an attack hit into some targets. It's expensive for sure, but the power is pretty outstanding.

Rating: A, the price makes this more restrictive but it needs to be costed this way or else it would be a problem.

Restraining Muzzle - 1 Glory - Court of the Seven

Yoke Fiends only. You can have three of these that you purchase with Glory in addition to the three you can buy with Ducats. This is a weird one to analyze, as these are useful if you want to have Yoke Fiends that do something besides clobbering the nearest thing and taking silly charges. The item itself is very good, but you won't often need six total at once, so really the question of this comes down to if you value 1 Glory or 10 Ducats more when putting one of these on your roster. For my money, I'd more often go for the Glory ones if you aren't saving for a specific item.

Rating: I'm not really sure? It's hard to rate this item as it's the only thing you can also buy for Ducats here. Since it's a very useful item but isn't really groundbreaking I'm just going to say B for now.

Resurrection Engine - 11 Glory - New Antioch

Consumable, Limit: 1. Another item that doesn't go on a model, but just sits in your roster. Once during the campaign, swap any result on the trauma table for a full recovery. The item is then consumed on use.

A stop-gap to make sure one of your key models doesn't bite the dust is quite good. As much as I'm being a bit of a hypocrite saying this after saying that I think these reactive "saving" things aren't great, this one can be fantastic. It can salvage an unlucky roll that knocks off your best model later in a campaign, and that alone is pretty priceless.

Rating: A, it being not tied to a specific model really sells this for me. Definitely not something you're grabbing until the tail end of a campaign though.

Trench Pilgrims Warband - Credit Bair

Rocket-Propelled Grenade - 2 Glory - All Factions

Consumable. Notably, this is Limit: 2 for Iron Sultanate and New Antioch, but Limit: 1 for everyone else. 36" of range with +1 Injury Dice and Ignore Armor means this will hit like a truck, but no accuracy bonuses and a single use means you're really going to want to make it count. Frankly, I'm not huge on consumable weapons in campaign settings like this, but I do think they can be a great "oh crap" weapon to have in your back pocket. I'd be harsher on them if they weren't super cheap and readily accessible, but I do think these can be great on models that don't have as much of a ranged punch, but have decent shooting stats.

Also worth mentioning that this isn't held like a weapon, it's just an item you can use like a ranged weapon when you consume it. That means you can hang on to this and not have it take up any hand slots, which is a nice touch.

Rating: B, it's solid but being a one-time use means this can sometimes just be a really expensive paperweight, and consumables can turn early campaigns into a bit of an arms race.

Salvage Golem - 4 Glory - New Antioch

Not allocated to a model, this little guy generates D6*5 extra ducats at the end of every game for each one you have in your inventory. It's still Limit: 1 to actually get them though.

If you've played other campaign style games, it cannot be overstated how much extra long-term economic investments can pay dividends. This averages out to being a free dork with some basic gear for every three games you play, and that's not a bad investment if you pick one up from the get go.

Rating: S, I firmly believe the free money boost from this is invaluable but ONLY if you get it as soon as you can. In a shorter campaign setting, this likely drops to an A as it will be most valuable when you can get a decent amount of money off it, but 4 Glory means the barrier to entry is low.

Smokescreen - 5 Glory - New Antioch

Consumable. After deployment, you can declare you're dropping a smokescreen and consume this if you choose. If you do, pick a battlefield quarter and it becomes hotboxed until the end of the game. Any ranged attacks that have line of sight passing through the smoke have -1 Dice.

This is interesting as it's a bit of a meta call, but it can be really good if your campaign has some scary shooting-focused armies. You can deploy in that corner to give yourself an early reprieve from shooting while you get into a more comfortable board position. This can get weird with certain board layouts and deployment zones, but it can really swing the matchup into a longer ranged faction.

Rating: C, as it's not something you're always going to want and 5 glory for a consumable isn't stellar, but it might be backbreaking for a tough matchup.

Sniper Scope - 2 Glory - New Antioch, Iron Sultanate, Heretic Legion

Limit: 2. Pick a weapon with "Rifle" in the name, and give it IGNORE LONG RANGE. Contrary to what you might expect, Sniper Rifles don't naturally have that rule, so this can be a great upgrade if you have anyone with one of those. While there are other weapons that can still use this pretty well on midrange skirmishers, this really shines as a way to just make snipers better at what they already want to be doing. Can be somewhat funny on an Automatic Rifle if you have them blasting out in longer ranged fights.

Author's note - I don't think there's anything stopping you from putting this on an Anti-Materiel Rifle which is pretty bonkers, but I would check with whoever is organizing your event/campaign.

Rating: A on Snipers (and AMR's if you can), B on other rifles, although not as highly suggested.

Mobile Sultanate Grand Cannon - 10 Glory - Iron Sultanate

The Brazen Bull Credit: Trench Crusade/Mike Franchina

Brazen Bull only. The weapon of choice for eliminating your enemies from 3 different postal codes away. While the M.U.R.A.D. Bombard is best for blasting clusters of enemies and the Flame Cannon is best for close range armor/horde shredding, the Grand Cannon is what you bring when you really want one guy super dead. 48" Range, +2 Injury Dice, Ignore Armor. It also pushes models D6" away if they aren't turned to paste from the shot.

This is a fantastic choice for a supportive artillery focused Bull. While you lose the area of effect capabilities of the other weapons, you get the highest range and raw power of the bunch. It's expensive, but all of the bull weapons are pretty pricy in Ducats as well.

Rating: I'm going to rate this against the other options you can take with a Brazen Bull - and thus, I'm going to give it a A. I think it's a side grade to the other options, but it's powerful enough in it's own right and gives a different role to the other options you can typically buy from the roster.

The Mark of Cain - 4 Glory - Heretic Legion

Elite only. Verbatim: "If an ACTION taken by a model causes one or more BLOOD MARKERS to be placed next to a model with the Mark of Cain, then place an equal number of BLOOD MARKERS next to the model that took the ACTION."

This means you can effectively redirect damage back towards anything that hurts you. Obviously, this means you want this on a model you already expect to take a beating. I actually quite like this on a Chorister, as it means you have a debuff piece that your opponent really wants to remove, but is actively punished for doing so. It's not bad on your other options as well, but ideally you'd rather not get hit as much on your other ELITES to begin with.

Rating: A on a Chorister, B otherwise.

Tormentor Chain - 3 Glory - Heretic Legion

Limit: 2. Not restricted, but worth a shout that this and Silenced Pistols are the only ranged options Death Commandos can pick up. 1 Handed, 10" range weapon with a plethora of keywords: ASSAULT, IGNORE COVER, IGNORE LONG RANGE, and SHRAPNEL. Among these things, it also doesn't make injury rolls as normal, and instead places 2 blood markers (one for the initial hit, then one for SHRAPNEL) next to the target if it hits. Then, fling the target up to 12" in a straight line closer to the user. Lastly, enemies can't retreat if they're within 1" of a model equipped with these.

On Death Commandos, these are fantastic for picking off a target that's stuck in a cluster of enemies that you don't really want to fight. It's good on other models as well, but the mobility of the Commando is really where you'll make this shine. It can be fun on a Priest as well, but I think there are generally better options as this still will take up a 1-handed ranged weapon slot.

Rating: A on a Death Commando, C otherwise.

Knights of Avarice Anointed, Credit - Loxi

Trench Dog - 1 to 3 Glory - Any Warband Besides Trench Ghosts

Pet Semetary apparently didn't get too popular in this universe, as the spooky ghosts can't take the good boys. These have a crazy amount of rules, but to summarize, you can deploy them as part of your warband that doesn't count towards Morale. Sadly, the rules explicitly say they cannot be promoted. I think this is a shame. They are a relatively poor combat statline but pack 8" of movement and +1 Dice to Climb, Jump, and Dash actions.

They cost 1 Glory base, but you can make them a FIRETEAM for +1 Glory. In addition, they can take one of the following special abilities for +1 Glory cost:
  • Guard Dog (anything but Black Grail and Court): Infiltrators can't be deployed within 12" of this model
  • Hellhound (Fallen Only): All Hellhounds have NEGATE FIRE. Black Grail and Knights of Avarice Hellhounds have +1 Dice and GAS on their attacks, all other Hellhounds have +1 Dice and FIRE on their attacks.
  • Martyrdom Dog (Trench Pilgrim): Your dog has a Martyrdom Device and can activate it like an Ecclesiastic Prisoner.
  • Mercy Dog (New Antioch): These have and can use a Medi-Kit, and can drag Downed models within 1" of it when they move, but they can only move half the distance when doing so.
These all provide pretty unique utility and are pretty potent in their own right. For 2-3 Glory you end up with an extra activation that can sometimes do some cool stuff, and that's honestly not a bad shout.

Rating: I'm going to rate these individually, as they each are quite different.

Guard Dog: C, it's a bit of a meta call with how much infiltration you have.

Hellhound: B, worth investing in Fireteam as well for this.

Martyrdom Dog: B, can be a pretty crazy trading piece for matchups with tough nuts to crack.

Mercy Dog: A, the Medi-Kit is a super useful tool and having a cheap fast body to help get one where you need it is aces.

Standard Dog: F, I don't really think they're worth it for factions like Iron Sultanate that can't get any of the upgrades.

Trench Crusade, Trench Ghost - Credit: Bair
