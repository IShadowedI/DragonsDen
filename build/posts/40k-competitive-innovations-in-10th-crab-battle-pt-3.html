Thoughts

A theoretial build becomes practice here, with Deathwatch daring to leave Black Spear Task force behind and try Bastion Task Force. They clearly have the best Battleline special Datasheet outside of Templars, and packing a bunch of Veterans (plus one Hero Fortis Team) lets you hurl an extremely daunting force up the table, and put especially severe pressure on melee foes thanks to all those Judiciars.

Great theory, but this Astra Militarum build is very much not the right target for the Deathwatch. They're not interested in melee, and are also reasonably short on high value targets for the concentrated firepower Bastion offers to really sing. There's the Dorn, but the Deathwatch lack the numbers to really shoot for that at range, so it's going to sit there carving through their ranks for a while. The transports similarly create a bit of a headache - it's hard for the Deathwatch to properly get a punch on the juicy stuff inside when they initiate battle. However, they also can't really play it cagey - the Guard have enough plasma guns and supporting fire to comfortably clean up a couple of the big squads a turn, so parcelling out resources won't work.

That plus Hidden Supplies definitely feels like it leans this towards a full send for the Deathwatch, and I think it has a solid chance of working. There's lots going on from the Guard side, but the Deathwatch squads are still decently threatening with only 3-4 models left, so the Guard have to make sure they're fully taking down each one, which requires big commitment. If they're up against a wall of units and only kill two, they might get pushed in the next turn, and at the very least will be kept off scoring for a bit. I don't really think the Deathwatch have the tools for any other plan here, and from the Militarum side it's all about anticipating that the rush is probably coming, and ensuring there are layers of counterattack available to punish it when it does. In this instance, it appears that the counterattack went off relatively smoothly, handing victory to the Militarum.

Archetype

Librarius Fusillade Fun

Thoughts

Something completely different from our next winner - a Librarius Conclave build that leans hard into the volume firepower plan. Sternguard tooled up with Fusillade will kill almost anything stone dead, another attribute that goes up in value when 250pts centrepieces are the name of the game, and this build ensures the effective delivery of that blow by bringing both a Land Raider and Ventris. If the opponent has good screens, Land Raider, if they've gotten greedy, Deep Strike, easy. I also like some of the other combos set up here; a full Intecessor unit can actually do decent all-rounder damage if you just pop Prescient Precision on them, and taking Obfuscation on a second Librarian lets one of your units skulk on a flank quite effectively. Feels like you're maybe a little vulnerable to some of last month's top builds like a full Possessed/Chosen send, but May is crab time, and this list looks ready for it. Good stuff from Stepan.

Archetype

Ceramite Ultramarines

Thoughts

Rounding out this week's final major, we have Ceramite Sentinels Ultramarines. This new detachment has picked up a couple of showings, and it's not just because people are deathly bored of Gladius and Blade. Being able to stick re-roll 1s on Vindicators by toeing them back into terrain is genuinely excellent in the current metagame, and such easy access to good re-rolls helps the list fight on multiple fronts better than some other builds without having to invest in Guilliman. The Stratagems from Sentinels also help protect and enhance a small number of elite Infantry units, as you see from one squad wach of Stern and Victrix Guard, and access to full Wound re-rolls for the Victrix is also a genuine draw to the detachment. The only caveat I'd offer is that this build is unusually terrain pack dependant; specifically this rules on GW terrain, is decent on WTC, and is kind of garbage on UKTC, as only a small subset of the terrain pieces in the latter are easy to toe the back of a model into while projecting threat outwards. If your local maps support it, and you want a big of variety, this looks like a genuine breath of fresh air. Well done Maxim.
