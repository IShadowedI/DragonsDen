Thoughts

Elves vs. Dwarves, but in space. On the elf side of things, Cody returns with the Spectacle build that did very well for him last week, cutting out Reavers in favour of stuff that hits hard or denies points, and not much in between. Against him, a Votann build with plenty of go-wide power from Junior, able to go unit for unit with the Drukhari, and with a few nasty surprises up its sleeve.

In general, this setup favours the Drukhari - they love having lots of mid-weight stuff to pick off, they've got enough Dark Lances that they can trouble the Land Fortresses, and their speed is going to let them control the pace of engagement. The Votann do have a few assets to counter that, but probably not enough. The artillery is decent at picking up Wyches that hit the table, and the volkanite overwatch from the Steeljacks is very good, but the Spectacle side has a Periapt unit to force it to stay well at bay, and just in general the Votann are going to have to be super careful about spacing against this list. Putting that on standard Take and Hold where the Wyches have plenty of cheap, fast units with OC 10 for disrupting scores makes this a hard sell for the Votann side to pull off, and I'm not hugely surprised by the result. I wonder if, from that side, you just go super aggro with the Forts and see what happens - there should be enough Darklight to kill one, but T12 at least makes a bounce possible, and if you can flip the script on the Drukhari and put them on the back foot, maybe that helps.

Thoughts

Our final double Defiler dilemma is also on the brawler plan, taking slightly fewer units but making them meaner by adding the Huron/Masters/Chosen option. Well done Trae for sprinkling a little extra spice in with that one!

Thoughts

Guard have hit one of the biggest drop-offs from the rise of Defilers because it's too easy for the pure Pactbound builds to just outgun them, but they have a deep pool of options and this build showcases one of their more successful routes to adapting.
