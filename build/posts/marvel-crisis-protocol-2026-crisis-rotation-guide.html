Marvel Crisis Protocol 2026 Crisis Rotation Guide

April 30th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Another year brings a new crisis rotation. We recently went through the new missions from Operation Zero Tolerance. Now that the challenger document has been updated we can get the full picture of what the competitive mission pack will look like for the rest of the year.

Some Terminology

  • King of the Hill - These secure crisis require your characters to stand in a particular spot to score them. If both players contest the same objective it can cause neither player to score it which can in some cases slow the game.
  • Pay to Flip - These secures require you to pay to interact with them to give you a chance to control them. Usually once you control them you are free to leave until your opponent interacts to take them off you. Teams that have additional power early can take advantage of this as they don't have to choose between picking up an extract and flipping a point.
  • Max (x) VPs - The maximum number of victory points you can score in a round for a crisis. The available Victory points on the board ultimately dictates how quickly or slowly a game will go. If you consistently score 4 victory points per round you will get to 16 and win the game after 4 rounds. Scoring 1 less at 3 per round would cause the game to take a full 6 rounds to score out instead so even 1 more victory point available can dictate the pace of the game.
  • Asset or Civilian - The type of extract being used for the crisis. A small but significant number of rules interact with only one of these. Examples would be the Inhuman's card Terrigenesis and Namor's ability to force you to drop extracts.
  • Attrition - The playstyle of trying to daze and or KO your opponent to prevent them scoring in order to win the game in the long run.
  • Source Extracts - A new style of extract where there is a fixed source where you initially pick up extracts from. In the cleanup phase extracts you have picked up go back to the source and you have to pick them up all over again.
  • Secure Zone - A new style of secure where there is an arc on the secure affecting a specific part of the battlefield. When being placed the player placing the token can rotate it in any direction.
  • Limited - Characters can only hold one limited objective token.

Leaving

We say goodbye to the following crises in 2026. Of course in casual games you are free to play however you want, but for most people, even outside of tournaments, standard challenger is the most common way to play.

I've included the maximum threat value of each of these crisis in brackets as well as other relevant details below each one.

Extracts Leaving

  • Struggle for the Cube Continues (17) Max 5 VPs – Asset
  • Fear Grips World As "Worthy" Terrorize Cities (18) Max 4 VPs – Asset
  • The Montesi Formula Found (18) Max 3 VPs – Asset
  • Terrigen Canisters Fuel Doomsday Device (17) Max 5 VPs – Asset
  • Deadly Legacy Virus Cured? (19) Max 3 VPs (usually) – Asset
Some of these extracts have been around for years now, so much so that colloquially they have developed their own shorthand names, with Fear Grips most often being called "Hammers" despite the word hammer not being in the actual name of the crisis. Struggle for the Cube Continues has been around since the very first core box of the game.

Several strategies have evolved over the years for how best to play some of these crises, specifically ways of stacking the Legacy Virus with Kingpin's Criminal Syndicate or picking up multiple cubes and running into a corner. It's worth noting that with the removal of these two crises in particular there will no longer be any crisis that allow you to stack extracts on one character. The new keyword 'limited' denotes that each character can only hold one.

Personally I'll be sad to see Hammers gone as it always brought fun games; who doesn't like rolling more dice, after all? Having said that, there were definitely teams who could take more advantage of this crisis than others and it has had its time in the sun.

Secures Leaving

  • Cosmic Invasion! Black Order Descends on Earth (16) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Deadline to Destruction (16) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Mayor Fisk Vows To Find Missing Witnesses (16) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Riots Spark Over Extremis 3.0 (17) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • M'Kraan Crystal Gets Heroes Home! (19) Max 4 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • Intrusions Open Across City As Seals Collapse (19) Max 3 VPs – King of the Hill
Two of the crises leaving, Cosmic Invasion and Riots Spark, are on the 'D' map, the widest shape possible. This would give a big advantage to fast moving teams like Web Warriors who could pivot around the board faster than others could catch them. Riots could be particularly punishing for attrition teams.

At the other end of the fightiness spectrum, Intrusions and Mayor Fisk were two of the most brawly crisis in the previous rotation so the combination of these leaving will be a mixed bag.

Extracts Staying

  • Alien Ship Crashes in Downtown (17) Max 3 VPs – Asset
  • Unexpected Guests Crash Royal Wedding (17) Max 4 VPs – Civilian
  • Spider-Infected Invade Manhattan (17) Max 5 VPs – Civilian
  • Scientific Samples Found in Discovered Universe (17) Max 5 VPs – Asset
  • Inhumans Deploy Advanced Weaponry (18) Max 4 VPs – Asset
  • Mutant Extremists Target U.S. Senators! (19) Max 6 VPs – Civilian
  • Skrulls Infiltrate World Leadership (20) Max 4 VPs – Civilian
  • Jailbreak Leads to Mass Mutant Escape (20) Max 4 VPs – Civilian – Source
Looking at the extracts that remain in this rotation, there are a couple of surprises here. Mutant Extremists Target U.S. Senators is the highest scoring crisis in the current rotation with 6 extracts on the board. This can lead to some teams running away with scoring before the opponent has a chance to stop then.

Having said that, being only allowed one move action when holding a Senator does sometimes turn things to a shooting gallery and the combination of these two things and the Terrigenesis card made Inhumans a terror in the competitive scene for a while. As it is so polarizing, the new mission selection system means that teams who are not favored on it can just choose never to play it which does help.

Secures Staying

  • Deadly Meteors Mutate Civilians (17) Max 3 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • Infinity Formula Goes Missing! (17) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Guardians Save Shi'ar Empress in Style (17) Max 4 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • Mutant Madman Turns City into Lethal Amusement Park (18) Max 4 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • Wedding Party Targeted in Terrible Attack! (20) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Super-Powered Scoundrels Form Sinister Syndicate (20) Max 5 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Lockdown! Security Systems Stymie Breakout (18) Max 3 VPs (except round 1) – King of the Hill
Just as Senators was the highest scoring extract, Super-Powered Scoundrels is the highest scoring secure and is a little surprising to see on the remain list. It is a crisis that heavily favors scoring teams like Kingpin's Criminal Syndicate or the Web Warriors.

Infinity Formula is a fan favorite that I am happy to see stay, as the effect of giving your characters more power means that you get to do more on your turns.

Although Mayor Fisk is leaving the rotation, Wedding Party is a similar secure, so teams that were good at Fisk before can still take Wedding Party.

New Crises

That does it for the crises that were in the previous rotation; let's look at the crises entering this rotation. For a full overview of these missions take a look at our article here.

Extracts

  • Evidence of Experimental Soldiers Exposed (19) Max 3 VPs – Asset – Source
  • Sentinel Schematics Sabotaged! (17) Max 4 VPs – Asset – Source
  • Salvaged Supplies Fuel Resistance Efforts (18) Max 4 VPs – Asset
  • Surprise Assault! Mutant Homes Destroyed (16) Max 4 VPs – Civilian – Source

Secures

  • Strike Team Secures SHIELD Relay! (17) Max 4 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • X‑Men Infiltrate Secret Weapons Facility (17) Max 5 VPs – Pay to Flip
  • Power Overload! Factory Goes Up in Flames (19) Max 5 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Survivors Search for Safe Shelter (20) Max 3 VPs – King of the Hill
  • Assault Ships Make Sweeping Search! (16) Max 4 VPs – King of the Hill
Strike Team Secures SHIELD Relay! was a mission that was part of the War of Kings pack but didn't make it into the Challenger document. With Riots Spark and Cosmic Invasion both leaving, there is more room for another wide map to enter this rotation.

Overall Picture

After everything is said and done we've got 24 crises to work with this time around. A visual representation of the rotation is shown below. In red are the extracts, in blue the secures, and in purple, the places that have both.

When evaluating a board, even before tokens are placed it is worth looking at what terrain is in these areas. Board edge is chosen before tokens are placed, so if you can look at the board and make these evaluations by eye you can set yourself up for the rest of the game.

Operation Zero Tolerance Extracts and Secures Credit: Alfred_Pharius

 

Operation Zero Tolerance Extracts Credit: Alfred_Pharius

There are several missions with Extracts sat on the midline. Just as with the current rotation, characters that can pick up on the midline with one move such as Namor or Angela will continue to be useful.

Not every affiliation will have access to 'safe' grabs on the midline like this, so the ability to punish an opponent early on may be another route to take.

Source extracts work like secures in some ways as they are dropped each round and need to be moved to and picked up again. Other extract types are picked up once and then held until dropped. The need to pick them up each round is a 'tax' that affiliations that struggle with power might find difficult. It also means that picking up an extract and running to the edge of the board is no longer an option when source extracts are in play.

When it comes to Assets vs Civilians there are 6 of each type within the rotation. This only matters at the moment for a few abilities such as Terrigenesis for the Inhumans but it is worth bearing in mind.

All extracts in this rotation are limited meaning that only one character can hold them at a time. This might make things difficult for any strategy that involves going 'tall' with fewer characters as you still need enough characters to pick extracts up.

Operation Zero Tolerance Secures Credit: Alfred_Pharius

Out of these secures, five are Pay-to-Flips and the other seven are King of the Hill Style. With five Pay-to-Flips available it might be possible for a team like X-Men to take only Pay-to-Flip secures and do well. They have access to First Class, which means that they don't need to pay for the first time they interact, giving them strong scoring pressure early.

And the other end other end of things there are more than enough King of the Hill style secures for Kingpin to do well with this next rotation. Scoundrels and Infinity Formula both still being in this rotation mean that this might be Mayor Fisk's time to shine.

Whether a mission is King of the Hill or Pay-to-Flip can heavily dictate the speed of the game. Besides at the start of the game, Pay-to-Flip missions are controlled by one player or the other. Any time a point is flipped it is adding to one player's score and effectively removing from the other player's score.

King of the Hill secures can be scored by either player as well, but the big difference is that they can be contested and so scored by neither player. When this happens it can slow the game down considerably as it can force ties which wouldn't happen otherwise.

Secures tend to dictate where the fights happen. Looking at the locations of where these secures are shows that in most cases things will be more central this rotation. Characters like Rocket Raccoon that have traditionally sat on the back point shooting from long range won't be as useful this rotation as there aren't many back points to sit on!

Threat Values

Lets take a look at what threat values we can expect from this rotation.

Operation Zero Tolerance Threat Levels Credit: Alfred_Pharius

For secures we have:
  • 1 crisis at 16 threat
  • 5 crisis at 17 threat
  • 2 crisis at 18 threat
  • 1 crisis at 19 threat
  • 3 crisis at 20 threat
For extracts we have:
  • 1 extract at 16 threat
  • 5 extracts at 17 threat
  • 2 extracts at 18 threat
  • 2 extracts at 19 threat
  • 2 extracts at 20 threat
The most common threat value by far is 17 threat. When building your squads it is worthwhile thinking about the strongest team you can make at 17 threat as there is a high chance that you will have to play at that threat value.

We know from interviews that 3 and 4 threat models are likely to get health improvements as we've seen with Nick Fury and Hawkeye already. With this in mind at 17 threat we are likely to see a lot of teams with two 4 threat models and three 3 threat models.

On the flip side of the coin there is only one secure and extract at 16 threat. If you can't make a strong team at 16 with the new mission selection system, you should be able to avoid it by not picking it and not putting those missions in your own roster.

Eighteen, 19 and 20 threat games might become relatively rare given this spread. Teams with expensive leaders such as She-Hulk might have difficulty working in other high value threats and have to settle with multiple lower threat characters.

Victory Points and Game Speed

Operation Zero Tolerance Victory Points Credit: Alfred_Pharius

The maximum number of victory points on the table can dictate how long a game will last. If you can consistently score 4 per round and your opponent can't you will win after turn 4. If you only score 3 per round you won't hit 16 until turn 6.

The most common maximum victory points you can score for both the secures and the extracts is 4 per round. Assuming both the extract and secure are maximum 4 per round then there will be 8 available on the table in the majority of games in this crisis pack.

Let's make some assumptions.
  • Round 1, the score is even at 4 all.
  • Round 2, you take one off your opponent to make it 5 to 3. Your total score is now 9 to your opponent's 7.
  • If you want to score the game out on round 3, you would now need 7 of the available 8 points on the table to do so.
For most teams this would be difficult though not impossible. The likelihood is therefore that the average game will go to round 4 if not longer.

Assuming 8 maximum victory points and 17 threat for the 'average' game means that more often than not squads will be 5 wide. Taking only 4 characters will give you priority most of the time but you may struggle to keep up with an opponent's scoring potential.

Having said that, the missions being central might mean that priority is the more important factor. Your opponent can't out score you if you take away their activations in the first place! It's an exciting time to be playing Marvel: Crisis Protocol both competitively and for fun.
