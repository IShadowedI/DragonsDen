Trench Crusade Warband Focus: Defenders of the Iron Wall

May 8th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome everyone to the next Warband Focus! This time, we venture outside of the great Iron Wall and join the expeditionary forces of the Iron Sultanate, who, after centuries of defending the gate from atop its battlements, now venture out to reclaim territory once lost. Taking the fight to the heretics is, in and of itself, a defensive action. 

Iron Sultanate Warband Credit: @Secretly_Paul on Instagram

The Lore

Since the moment the gates of the Iron Wall closed, the Sultanate has focused on defending its new borders from relentless attacks by Hell’s forces. For centuries, the Grand Cannons have ringed their mighty tune atop the Iron Wall, and the soldiers stationed there have perfected the art of shooting from an advantageous position. Since Sultan Malik began his reign, the objective of the Sultanate has become something more than mere resistance. Now the objective is to retake old ground, for in the eyes of the Sultanate, it is land that has been lost, but not forgotten. The Sultan now sends warbands to recapture this land or recover lost treasures and artefacts, utilising the expertise of Sappers and marksmen who once stood atop the Iron Wall. 

The Variant

As always, we’ll take a quick look at a quick overview of what the Variant brings to the table.  
  • Far from the Sublime Gate: No Lions, Yüzbaşı or Assassins can be recruited, and no Cloak of Alamut or Wind Amulet can be taken by any model. 
  • Grand Cannons: You can include up to two Sultanate Grand Cannons in this Warband (with some restrictions). 
  • Janissary Officers: This warband may include up to two Janissary Officers, who also have the Elite Keyword. 
  • Marksmanship of the Iron Wall: When making a Ranged Attack, the Elevated Position modifier grants a +2 Dice, instead of the usual +1. 
  • Sapper Corps: You can bring up to four Sultanate Sappers. 
  • Siege Jezzails Teams: When making a Ranged Attack with a Siege Jezzail, add +1 Dice if a friendly model is within 1” of the wielder. 
  • Silahdar: Instead of a Yüzbaşı, your leader is a Silahdar, which has the Yüzbaşı profile. The Mubarizum ability is replaced by the Strong Keyword and may include Alaybozan, Anqā Guard, and Explosive Charges. 
  • Sipahi: You can recruit a Sipahi Automaton Cavalry, which uses the Mamluk Faris entry and cannot change the Battlekit. You may still take a Mamluk Faris Mercenary as well. 

Battlekit Options

So we've looked at the changes as a whole, but this is only half the picture. The Defenders do have a good selection of unique Battlekits that, in a broad sense, offer more control of the board than outright killing power (the big exception being the Grand Cannon). The problem is, with a lot of them, you just won't have the Ducats until later down in a campaign, unless you're getting lucky in the early post-game.
  • Anqā Guard (10 Ducats): Available only to your Sultanate Sappers, this battle kit is essentially a deployable terrain piece that can be placed during deployment or in-game.
  • Banner of Desert Wind (30 Ducats | Limit: 1): Grants a 24" debuff bubble to enemy movement, reducing it by 1". On the one hand, it's a nice wide bubble and a nice debuff. However, it can be taken only by your Leader and must be held at all times. Reducing your Silahdar's offensive or defensive potential for this is just not a wise investment. If it were Elite-only instead of Leader, it might have more play.
  • Explosive Charges (35 Ducats | Limit: 1): This consumable can only able to be taken by your Siahdar & Sappers. It allows you to place a charge on a terrain piece in secret (you write it down on a piece of paper) and then detonate it, forcing models on the terrain piece to take an Injury Roll with the Shrapnel Keyword. As your Silahdar can take it, and it offers some great psychological pressure for your opponent, it is certainly a piece of Battlekit worth taking.
  • Iron Wall Kalkan (30 Ducats | Limit: 1): This shield grants the Brazen Bull Cover and the benefits that keyword grants. It also allows the Bull to essentially push enemy models and potentially damage them. Whilst this is nice in practice, a whopping 30 Ducats is a lot to pay compared to the simple Trench shield at 10 Ducats. If this also granted -1 Armour, it would be a juicier pick.
  • Sultanate Grand Cannon (60 Ducats | Limit: 2): Saving the best until last, the Sultanate Grand Cannon is a defining part of the Variant. Granting +2 Injury Dice and Ignore Armour at a whopping 48" Range. It can be wielded by the Brazen Bull, but also can be deployed as a Gun Battery, with a model within 1" being able to use it (using the model's stats for the attack). Should anything survive the hit, it is pushed back d6".
When you put this altogether, you’ve got a great defensive gunline that expects you to work with careful positioning to make use of the elevation bonus, as well as working in power pairs for your Siege Jezzail users. The Silahdar and Janissaries can offer a capable melee threat if needed, but Melee is not going to be your strongest point. If anything, it’s one of the weaknesses of this Variant. That and your more elite nature, seeing as many of your stronger shooting weapons, whilst packing a punch, are expensive and will leave your pockets empty and your models few. Mistakes will be punished, especially against foes that can infiltrate or get up the board quickly and get behind your backlines. 

The Units

Silahdar

Iron Sultanate Yüzbaşı Captain. Credit Urr

The Silahdar replaces the Yüzbaşı Captain that we know. However, the Sildahar replaces the Mubarizun's ability with a simple one that grants it the Strong keyword for free. Whilst this is nice, the Yüzbaşı has the option to buy the Janissary Veteran upgrade, which gives it Strong and the Janissary Counter Charge ability for 10 Ducats. This is a bit of a nonbo, where you’re able to buy something you already have. Still, getting Strong for free is nothing to sneeze at, and so I’ll take it! Battlekit-wise, the Silahdar make amazing use of the Halberdgun, allowing them to fully utilise their +2 Melee and Ranged stat line. Throw in some Corrosive Ammunition and some Alchemist Armour, and you have a good, solid front liner. 

Recommended Battlekit Choices: 
  • Halberdgun
  • Alchemist Armour
  • Corrosive Ammunition

Janissary Officer

Janissary

Great Siege Jezzail users, the Janissary Officers act as mini Silahdars in almost all aspects. Whilst they hit a little less hard, they can still do solid work either in Melee or Ranged. Given the power of the Iron Sultanate’s ranged selection, the latter is certainly the most optimal. As an Elite, you will need to ensure that they stay safe and gain that valuable XP as soon as possible. In the early campaign games, you'll not be able to fit much in beyond a Siege Jezzail and some kind of Ammunition extra, so keeping them at safe is a priority. 

Recommended Battlekit Choices: 
  • Siege Jezzail
  • Alchemical / Corrosive Ammunition
  • Two-Handed Hammer
  • Armour
 

Jabbirean Alchemist

Iron Sultanate Jabirean Alchemist. Credit: Urr

Whilst the Alchemist shines in the House of Wisdom, their presence in the Defender variant is due to their fantastic shooting capabilities, where the +2 Ranged Attack stat and their Mastery of the Elements ability mean they are perfect users of the Sultanate Grand Cannon. Whilst expensive, it is worth every Ducat. As you're going to be strapped for ducats, getting Armour on your Alchemist is less of a priority. They should be positioned to keep them safe from harm without compromising their offensive capabilities. 

Recommended Battlekit Choices: 
  • Sultanate Grand Cannon

Brazen Bull 

the Finished Bull

The beefiest of Bovines, this absolute slab of artificial beef is right at home in the Defenders, able to make use of the Sultanate Grand Cannon as a mobile artillery piece. However, the best use since the release of the recent Beta rules preview has made the Flame Cannon a great pick! With no need to make an Attack Success Roll, you don’t need to worry about the lack of a Ranged Stat Attack bonus, and the cherry on top is the inclusion of the Assault Keyword. After that charge, you can beat them over the head with even a simple Two-Handed Hammer. 

Recommended Battlekit Choices: 
  • Flame Cannon
  • Two-Handed Hammer
  • Trench Shield
  • Standard Armour

The List: 

Cannon Go Boom.
 

  Faction: Defenders of the Iron Wall Rating: 700 Ducats | 0 Glory Patron: Sublime Gate

## Elites ##

**Silahdar** - 175D Battlekit: Halberd-Gun, Corrosive Ammunition, Alchemist Armour, Trench Shield

**Jabirean Alchemist** - 116D Battlekit: Sultanate Grand Cannon, Trench Knife

**Janissary Officer** - 88D Battlekit: Siege Jezzail, Alchemical Ammunition ## Troops ##

**Brazen Bull** - 210D Battlekit: Flame Cannon, Great Hammer/Maul, Standard Armour, Trench Shield

**Azeb** - 41D Battlekit: Trench Knife, Musical Instrument

**Azeb** - 35D Battlekit: Jezzail, Alchemical Ammunition

**Azeb** - 35D Battlekit: Jezzail, Alchemical Ammunition


For this list, I kept it as a classic example that showcases the army's strengths while also highlighting its weaknesses. You've got your flexible Sihaldar pic, ready to ping shots off at Ranged and able to get into the thick of it when needed. The Alchemist with the Grand Cannon because Boom = fun. I included the Bull here because it needs to be promoted as soon as possible; losing it at any point in the campaign would be a big Ducat loss. Also, in the early games, your opponents will have less of a chance to take it out. Finally, some basic Azebs for actions, sitting on objectives, and taking some semi-reliable potshots. Whilst this is all well and good, there is a big investment in the three core models of Sihaldar, Bull and Alchemist, each costing a hefty amount of Ducats, and not leaving much for your other troops. This is just the price you need to pay to field this force.

Final Thoughts

The Defenders of the Iron Wall is a Variant defined by its clarity of purpose. You pick this faction because you know what it does and it does it well. If you want a gunline that has more of a defensive and positional focus, rather than outright kill potential, then this is the variant for you. It may not be as deadly as something like Heretic Legion, but it rewards smart play more so than most. Plus Cannon go Boom! So what more can you really ask for?
