[40k] Tabletop Battles Hot Take: The Red Terror

May 4th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Launching a surprise Monday assault from beneath the earth, the Red Terror explodes back onto 40K battlefields with a lethal new Datasheet. This accompanies the new model that's going up for preorder on Saturday, and it looks like one that Tyranid players are going to want to get their hands on. Unusually for a pure Datasheet preview, Warhammer Community have also included the points cost for the model, so we have the full picture, which is pretty exciting for Tyranids (and Final Day fans in Genestealer Cults), and appropriately terrifying for everyone else. Let's take a look.



The Red Terror runs you 130pts and boy do you get a lot for that. The Terror is fast, zipping 10" around the Battlefield and ignoring walls despite being a Monster, and packs a great profile for sweeping up light and mid-weight Infantry. 12A is a lot, particularly as if you get to swing with it in Synapse Range you hit the key S8 breakpoint to really mangle foes, but even without that you can just about sweep a squad of Marines once you factor in the (extremely funny) gaping maw attack (though watch out for Armour of Contempt, which pushes you to needing buffs to make it happen). Several detachment rules also help it get over the line to reliably chomping a unit, and you do want to make sure it kills what it hits because of the Subterranean Hunter rule.

This allows the Red Terror to jump back into Reserves at the end of either Fight Phase as long as it's not in Engagement Range, meaning that if you successfully sweep the foe, or simply don't happen to be in a Fight right now, you can hop into Reserves to plan your future crimes. This rule is wildly powerful every time it shows up in its full strength form, and makes the Terror a nightmare to pin down and deal with, and makes it an exceptional scoring unit to boot. It also makes your opponent's early screening a nightmare, because the combination of this plus Deep Strike means if you start on the board and go first, turn one Rapid Ingress is online if you pull it at the end of your turn. The evasiveness is helpful because the one real weakness here is a lack of invulnerable save, so really big guns just splatter it. That does provide a bit of counterplay on Overwatch too - something like a Defiler with any sort of re-rolls has a decent chance of just sending it directly to the shadow realm.

Where do you use The Terror? Tyranid-wise, any of Invasion Fleet, Subterranean Assault and Vanguard Onslaught love it. The model is priced to move, so Fleet doesn't mind just jamming it in as a value piece, and lots of attacks synergises with the Detachment Rule to up the reliability. Subterranean's re-roll 1s also really help with that, and a Burrower that you can repeatedly reset helps the rest of the list function very smoothly. Finally, Vanguard Onslaught makes it extra speedy, and adds some extra tools to defend it when out of position between Unseen Lurkers and Hypersensory Scillia. At the extremely aggressive price the Terror has landed at, you should probably jam them into all of the above.

The same is probably true of Final Day - the all-round value here is high, and being able to repeatedly Ingress then place Catalyst wherever needed helps keep the army's buffs up. Doing that is also near completely risk free as stands - as long as you're willing to spend the CP, you can move in, tee up the buff for a turn, then vanish. Simples.

The Terror seems exceptionally good at the asking price, and should make plenty of lists. They do bounce off some foes, so if they went up too much in cost they'd drop off as an auto-take pretty quickly, but at 130pts the package is too good to pass up.

Rob: The Red Terror is unlikely to be the kind of attack piece that say, the Twin Lance are - you're likely to be severely disappointed with them the first time you go into Armour of Contempt - but that ability to go back up in either Fight phase makes them an incredibly versatile scoring piece with some great melee upside. At worst, it's like a kind of Warp Talons for Tyranids, and you'll have to choose its targets carefully. But what you get for 130 points is just so incredibly efficient that it's worth taking the Red Terror because you won't get that much value anywhere else for the same points.
