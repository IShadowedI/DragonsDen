[40k] Tabletop Battles Hot Take: May 6 Faction Pack Updates

May 6th, 2026 via Goonhammer
GW sprang some new faction packs on us this morning, with updates for Space Marines of both the Loyalist and Chaos varieties, the Astra Militarum, and the Tyranids. There's also an update to the Munitorum Field Manual, but no apparent changes - instead this seems to be incorporating the costs for the new units released on Saturday. Presumably an app update will shortly follow, which will be nice for me personally as it will mean I can add Wazdakka to lists instead of just leaving a 175pt gap.

Let's take a look at what changed for each faction in these updates.

Space Marines

The updates here are targeted directly at the Armoured Speartip detachment. In our Detachment Focus we highlighted the power of these rules, which added tons of extra mobility to Space Marine armies in ways that were likely to be troubling for anyone going up against them. GW clearly thought they'd overcooked it, with several changes being made to the Detachment rules.

Firstly, the Rapid Deployment detachment rule gets two key changes - FLY transports are now excluded from it, which means no more use of Stormravens. In addition, the detachment rule no longer triggers on a TRANSPORT which arrived from Strategic Reserves - this is how we thought it worked anyway, but clearly GW wanted to add some clarification. Further on in the keywords section, FLY is again exempted from the HEAVY TRANSPORTS rule.

The Tip of the Spear Enhancement gets a nerf too, reducing from Scouts 9" to Scouts 6".

Finally, the Machine Wrath Stratagem now functions more like Careen from War Horde in that it only triggers if its Deadly Demise ability actually goes off. This makes it much less reliable, as you can't simply throw the transport in there and bank on having it move away to safely drop its cargo off.

One notable Stratagem which did not change is Rapid Embarkation. We were expecting this to be updated to bring it in line with the Aeldari versions which require your unit to have been eligible to fight, but apparently not (and notably didn't change for Orks either in the Blitz Brigade).

Repulsor Executioner by Craig "MasterSlowPoke" Sniffen

Chaos Space Marines

A couple of keyword changes here to errata Rhinos and Land Raiders - Mutilators are now properly excluded from embarking in Chaos Rhinos, and take up two spaces in Chaos Land Raiders. Not massive, but a good correction of an oversight.

Credit: Thomas "Goatboy" Reidy

Astra Militarum

There's a host of changes to the Astra Militarum, with updates to both Commissars Yarrick and Graves plus the Hippogriff AFV. In addition, the Recon Squad datasheet is formally added to the pack - you can find our take on this new unit here.

The biggest changes here are for Yarrick, whose Counterstrategist ability gets a total overhaul. Firstly its trigger is now that an enemy unit ends a move within 9" of this model, rather than 12" of his unit, massively reducing the danger zone. Secondly, the friendly unit it targets cannot have made a Normal move or used Fire Overwatch already this phase - so no double-dipping or usage of reactive moves to get a unit into position for it.

Of the three options the rule gives you, only the first one (make a Normal move of up to D6") is unchanged. The second option, to shoot, now functions like Overwatch - you need to roll a 6 to hit. This is vastly less powerful than the original version. The third version is completely rewritten; instead of declaring a charge, you now make a Surge move. This has two significant impacts; firstly it obviously reduces the distance you can move (d6" rather than 2d6"), but also you still have to move towards the closest enemy unit, and can only get into Engagement Range with that one. That means that an opponent can effectively block or redirect the move.

Decisive Command also gets a change, now only functioning in your Command phase (so you can't double up on splashing orders around by triggering them in other phases).

Commissar Graves gets smaller changes, with keyword updates to add CHARACTER to her party bus version and remove COMMAND SQUAD from her on foot. The foot version's Brutal Disciplinarian ability is now changed to once per turn instead once per phase; one suspects this was a typo rather than the intended functioning, so good fix.

Finally, a wording tidy-up on the Hippogriff AFV, which now just has a lascannon rather than a heavy lascannon.

Credit: Bair

Tyranids

Tyranids get the Red Terror's datasheet added to their faction pack. You can find our hot take on the Terror here.

Credit: BuffaloChicken

Impacts

The significant changes here are to Space Marines and Astra Militarum.

The Armoured Speartip changes ought to sand the edges off that detachment, reducing some of the stupider plays that were available and making it more vulnerable to an opponent being able to punch back - if you throw a transport forwards you are now highly likely to lose both the boat itself and its contents, rather than being able to effectively guarantee you'd keep the latter safe. Taking Tip of the Spear down to 6" reduces the scope for those kind of big turn 1 plays as well. It's also good to see the potential silliness of making the disembark move after arriving from reserves cleaned up.

For the Guard, Yarrick takes an absolute hammering - his Counterstrategist ability is now easier to avoid and does much less when it does go off. It's the right call, because the former version was wild, but he's pretty expensive now for what he brings compared to what he did before. The nerf to Decisive Command is a sensible one, but again reduces his utility a bit. Astra Militarum have been strong for a long time now, so they didn't necessarily need another great datasheet, but the Old Man may find himself spending more time on the display shelf than the battlefield.
