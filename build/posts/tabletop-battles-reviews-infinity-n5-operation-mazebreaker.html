Tabletop Battles Reviews: Infinity N5 Operation Mazebreaker

May 12th, 2026 via Goonhammer

Introduction

This month will see pre-orders begin for Operation Mazebreaker, the newest in a series of 2-player starter boxes for Infinity. Thanks to Corvus Belli for providing us the preview information, images and rules for the models in the box. In fact, to be pedantic, this release is 2 boxes: Operation Mazebreaker, and Beyond Operation Mazebreaker. The latter is a 2-player expansion set. These offer new models, for both updated and entirely new units, for 2 Sectorials: The Operations Sub-Section, which is part of Aleph, and the Jurisdictional Command of Corregidor, which is part of Nomads.



This article will review the physical box contents - we have plenty of pictures, but we have not yet handled the models in person - and give our verdict on whether this box is a good idea for players starting Infinity, and/or either faction, and what we might recommend to expand on it. 

If you’re more interested in the rules for new & updated units, and what we know of the wider, forthcoming changes to the rules of the two Sectorials, you can read our articles about OSS here, and Corregidor here. 

What’s In the Box?

As is typical for these ‘Operation’ sets, there are two opposed sets of 7 models each in the main box, and two sets of 3 models in the Beyond box. There is also a pre-order bonus model, Ruby Monday, which is included in the bundle if you pre-order both. She is a sort of mercenary who can be employed by either Sectorial. The main box also includes a game mat, set of cardstock terrain, and a starter rules booklet. The aim is that two new players can learn the Infinity rules by following this booklet, which will explain the basic game mechanics, and offer a series of introductory missions, starting with 3 models per side and building up to using the full set.

Unfortunately, the preview information Corvus Belli sent out did not include a PDF of the rules booklet, but honestly, we don’t expect it to be vastly different in structure from the one included in previous boxes, like Operation Sandtrap, released when the game’s current N5 edition dropped. It’s a well-established system that effectively introduces players to Infinity’s unique system of Orders and Automatic Reaction Orders (AROs). 

Once players are ready for the full breadth of Infinity rules, building their lists to their own taste and using the standard missions (all of these resources are free online), the two collections of models are not necessarily exactly balanced to fight each other. It roughly works, but in our view the intent is more to launch players into the game, with the expectation they’ll want to buy at least a few more models to tailor their force. Buying both boxes will give you and a friend each 10 models, and 200-odd points, depending on which profiles (weapon and skill sets) you choose for your units. Standard games of Infinity are 300 points, and in practice, most lists use 15 models. It’s also reasonable to have a few more models in one's collection to allow variation. We’ll offer some recommendations at the end of this article for what new players might look to add.

The Models

Aleph Operations Sub-Section (Mazebreaker)



Aleph is the AI which underpins the societal, technological and political systems of the Human Sphere. Depending on your perspective, its troops are lapdogs of the capitalist status quo, sinister Macchiavellian forces, or benevolent guardians of humanity as it moves toward post-humanity. Whatever they are, they sure do make cool robots.

Asura



This is essentially the leader of the force in the box - Asuras are one of the most elite models in the faction, and indeed in the game. They are hackers and gunfighters of the highest calibre.

Genghis: Love the forearm blade. She has some nice details over the previous sculpts of this unit, and they’ve toned down the slightly ridiculous pneumatic bosoms. Musterkux: She traded Chest day for Legs Day and I’m 100% here for it. That wicked wrist-blade is a straight-up villain-coded weapon and if that’s bad, then I don’t want to be the Good Guys anymore.

Nagas



These are the sneaky infiltrating types, fit for a variety of roles on the tabletop, depending on their weapon option. Genghis: A nice pose for a skirmisher here. It doesn't hurt that the long Aleph-pattern boarding shotgun is one of the coolest looking guns in the range.

Musterkrux: That drum magazine is utterly menacing. That shotgun is gorgeous. Oh, the Naga? Yeah, she's a lovely sculpt too! My pick for the best Aleph sculpt in the box.

Dikpala Tacbots



Established players may know these as Dakini Tacbots, it appears they were renamed. They are the line troopers of Aleph; being Remotes, rather than conventional light infantry, gives them marked strengths and some weaknesses compared to other factions’ equivalents.

Musterkrux: I like the use of rounded geometry on these models. You've got enough straight lines to communicate the fact that these are Robbuts, artificial and mechanical, but also smooth curves and corners/vertices to sell them as high-tech as well as fit in with the rest of Aleph's 'organic-adjacent' aesthetic.

Genghis: These are considerably meaner-looking than the old Dakinis. I love the shape of their heads and shoulderpads.

Shukra Consultants



This sharp-looking person is a dedicated support and leadership unit. In-game, they can either be a Lieutenant, leading your force from the rear, or have the Chain of Command skill, letting them take over if your original Lieutenant (probably the Asura) gets taken out.

Musterkrux: I loved the original Shukra as he was selling the Adam “I never asked for this” Jensen from Deus Ex: Human Revolution vibes pretty hard but I also adore the new Shukra design. She’s got that blend of Business-wear and Spooky Cyber Helmet going. That pose, with the Multi-rifle half-hidden under the jacket, sells the idea that she’s a (well-prepared) negotiator on a deal gone wrong more than a jacked up Special Forces Monster. Love it when the game leans a bit more into the street-level business instead of eternal and total war against aliens. Genghis: I’m loving the blend of futuristic corporate style in the coat, and the crazy high tech masked helmet.

Sharvara Houndbots



An entirely new addition to the game, these are extremely fast-moving, forward-deployed assault units. They’re a big change to the faction, providing a unit archetype they were previously lacking.

Genghis: I wouldn’t have said I was necessarily a robo-animal sort of guy, but this might be the standout model of the box for my money - and I’m a Corregidor player! A good dog. Musterkrux: Su-Jian remains my favorite robo-animal but I do have a little bit of time for the Sharvara. I feel like, between Next Wave’s Cyber-Dinos and even O-12’s SiriusBots we’ve seen a lot of the generic ‘prowling predator’ sculpt that we’re seeing again in the Sharvara (which is very solid, let’s be fair, they ooze menace) but I’d be interested to see CB get a bit more dynamic with these quadrupedal sculpts, maybe some action-poses? Leaping, pouncing, maybe even stalking?

Aleph Operations Sub-Section (Beyond Mazebreaker)

Dasyus



These are the prestige sneaky beakies of Aleph, a notch above the Nagas. They have the powerful Mimetism(-6) skill - essentially they’re very difficult to shoot unless you have the right equipment - and got a lot of exciting new equipment options in their new rules.

Genghis: Honestly, this is another standout model for me. There’s just something about the helmet and the gun. I’m not playing OSS, but I’m considering what I could use him to proxy as. Musterkrux: I celebrate our differences, my friend. However, in regard to the Dasyus I’m actually not a fan. “Hacker wearing blank visor, foot resting on a Tactical Case, one hand doing the Hacker-Gesture” is a trope at this stage and I’m not sure CB has done anything innovative with that pose or sculpt here (Full disclosure, I am a known ‘Crouching Dasyus’ enjoyer, so my bias is real). I do like that pistol, that chunky suppressor with stylised baffles is very evocative.

Kinnara Scoutbots



An entirely new unit for OSS in this release, these are built for close-range and melee combat. They can infiltrate during deployment, or parachute onto the table edges mid-game, making them the advanced assault troops of the box OSS force. 

Genghis: These are like mini versions of the iconic Marut. OSS’ cornering of the cool robot market continues apace.

Musterkrux: That’s a very dangerous thing to say for someone on a penal ship full of anti-Aleph conspiracy theorists.

Corregidor Jurisdictional Command (Mazebreaker)



Corregidor is one of the three great motherships that form the Nomad Nation. Of those spacefaring misfits, Corregidor is usually characterised as 'the Hand'. It was originally a prison ship, and still has penal troops, but most of its population are rough, tough, highly skilled space workers, in a variety of heavy industries.

Territorial



This is a new unit, an elite Medium Infantry (MI) which can be a gunner, an engineer, and/or a Lieutenant.

Genghis: I’m actually not too keen on this model, as cool as its rules are. The helmet design and the sort of skirt don’t do it for me. Also, while it is of no practical consequence, I’d have preferred to see it modelled with one of the longer guns, instead of the SMG. Musterkux: I actually adore the helmet but, yeah, the rest of the sculpt is a bit of a miss for me. Also, from some angles those boots give me wild Astroboy-vibes. I’ve never been a gun-smith or armourer, and have no pretensions of ever being one, but I feel like the stock on that Combi-rifle is wildly too short (they call it the Length of Pull, apparently). My 2 minutes of googling seemed to indicate that the stock should sit in your elbow while still allowing you to bend your arm freely. Maybe it’s a dynamic stock, this is the plausible future, afterall?

Vigilante



Another new unit, a sneaky, camouflaged MI with a variety of specialist roles, and some fighting capacity.

Musterkrux: Mixed feelings. On one hand, the hood adds to the model’s profile/silhouette nicely, gives it a bit of a hunchback feel to the way it’s loping forwards but I also have a niggling question about the ergonomics of a solid hood and how it might impact on one’s peripheral vision and situational awareness. It does look very cool, though.

Genghis: Now this unit I really like the style of. There’s an unusual mix in the bulky armour which is shaped like a stealthy hood.

Bearcat



Yet another new unit, the only Heavy Infantry in the box for Corregidor, this is an aggressive, airborne deployment unit. It is definitely the tip of the spear, giving them a super-mobile option to smash through enemy lines. 

Genghis: my favourite from the CJC side of the main box. I love the details of the armour and I think it looks menacing. I’m seriously considering how to get my hands on additional copies.

Musterkrux: I see a HI doing a 'pirouette-landing'? I'm gonna smash that Like button. The weird geometric patterns and shape of those shoulder-pads/thursters is really unique and eye-catching. I'm also a sucker for the Spider-eye/colander faceplate.

Bandit



These are Irregular, melee-capable skirmishers, perfect for aggressively infiltrating near enemy positions. They’re an infamous unit for their ability to start the game outside the enemy Deployment Zone, with bits of deployable equipment already laid down. 

Genghis: I love the details on the torso and the bare arms, I like the posture, it really conveys that this is a close combat threat with the classic Nomads machete design. But I think I will shave down the broccoli-top hair and leave the model with an enclosed helmet. It’s funky, and I’m sure better painters than me will make it look very interesting, but I prefer a more clipped look. Musterkrux: Very happy to see another Bandit sculpt. I do love the original but this one is very nice, as well.

Alguaciles



Line infantry troopers for Corregidor, and reputedly the base statline profile from which other units’ costs are extrapolated, these Alguaciles appear with SMGs (and gasmasks!) for the first time ever. 

Genghis: I like a lot of the design elements, but I wish I liked the Nomad SMG design more. These are fine, but like many long term Nomad players who were around for the 3 Alguaciles in Op Icestorm and the 3 Alguaciles in Op Crimson Stone, not to mention the N2 sculpts, I don’t really need more Alguaciles in my life. Double digits would be…Alguasilly. (I refuse to apologise).

Musterkrux: Genghis has it right, this will be too many Alguaciles for some players. However, I do appreciate that even though Operation: Icestorm was released back in 2014, the designs for Alguaciles remain consistent enough that nearly 12 years later you can still recognise that these models are all related.

Corregidor Jurisdictional Command (Beyond Mazebreaker)

Shifta Insertion Group



Awesome name, awesome dude. That terrific gun design is a Thunderbolt, the first Nomad version of the weapon we’ve seen sculpted. Despite their aura of tough charisma, these are actually light supporting troopers.

Genghis: I think this is a superb model. The gun and the body armour are cool, but the stance sells it. I do wonder if I should try shaving the cat ears off his mask. I prefer the luchadore look to Black Panther. Musterkrux: OMG, thank you! I thought I was the only one. Yup, great model once you ‘manage’ the Black Panther factor.

Coyote



These are highly skilled Medium Infantry, built for gunfighting, so it’s nice to see the model sculpted with its versatile Multi-Marksman Rifle. The shield on its back seems to represent its new Dodge (+3ARM) skill - a new rule for Infinity. Musterkrux: Poncho, Facemask, Beret. These are the three schools of fashion you must master if you want to crush it. Coyote is my pick for best Nomad design in Mazebreaker.

Genghis: Another standout for me. Love the pose, love the face mask, love the poncho and tactical combat shield thing. I am not such a fan of the beret (is this a trend? Am I just incredibly picky about headgear?). I may have a go at either filing it down and sculpting a smooth Nomad helmet-top, or sculpting the poncho neck up into a hood. 

Bambadroid



These Remotes provide low-cost support and defense to the more expensive Nomads. They are intrinsically linked to Coyotes; they can be fielded as Bambabots, Peripheral units synchronised to a Coyote controller, or they can be Bambadroids, which are independent, Order-generating Troopers. The latter can only be taken 1 per Coyote in your list, but they can easily slot into Fireteams. 

Genghis: No notes, this is a cool, chunky industrial robot. Take that Aleph, Corregidor has cool robo-buddies too! I bet painters are going to do some insane things with those flat surfaces, not least the screens on the ‘face’ of the model.

The Terrain



The main Mazebreaker box comes with some cardstock terrain and a custom mat to place it on. This is 32”x24”, which is appropriate for starter games as found in the booklet, and small (150pt) games - full size Infinity games are played on a 48”x48” table, which is obviously a very different proposition. While we haven’t handled the terrain, it looks to be CB’s recent style of very robust cardstock, which is quite nicely colour printed. The overall aesthetic is meant to evoke the underground, xeno-technological labyrinth which forms the backdrop for the campaign’s setting in the lore.

One thing new players are always told, quite rightly, is that Infinity needs a LOT of terrain to function properly. The 4 small building/container pieces, 3 stalagmite lines (which should basically fill the role of barricades), and 1 stepped objective centrepiece are a nice lot of scatter, but as players move into freeform games with the full Infinity rules, they will need some larger pieces to break up Line of Fire, and a good double handful of scatter terrain.

Musterkrux: I like the white buildings/structures, they look unique and more interesting than your standard 'science fiction' box. They'll fit in quite nicely with earlier iterations of Infinte cardstock terrain. Perfect. I don't like the Stalagmites (remember kids, when it comes to Stalagmites and Stalactites it's G for ground, C for ceiling) but appreciate that trying to use cardstock to design a natural terrain feature was never going to be easy but I suppose they help sell the idea that this battle is happenign underground and any terrain is better than no terrain for a new player. Finally, that Altar to Glowing Green Evil is Warhammer-coded as hell and I'm not sure if I'm Yuck or Yum on that. It's cool but is it Infinity-Cool?

Is Mazebreaker a Good Entry Point?

The quick answer: Yes, it is very good, provided you are interested in the 2 factions contained in the box. There are ~45 factions in Infinity, with a variety of aesthetics. This is a good way to start the game, particularly with a friend, but if you want to have a long look around first to identify the faction you’re most enamoured of, you might as well buy straight into that instead. If you’re unsure, open to any faction, or especially if you like the look of these ones, it’s great, since you will get some starter terrain and a play area to get cracking with immediately.

The forces contained in each box give the potential to create workable army lists for each faction, up to 200pts. Both fall a bit short of the 300pt size of full games (OSS is ~260 and Corregidor ~220, depending on profiles chosen), so players would naturally look to expand them slightly quite soon.

We should explain Infinity’s modelling approach to weapon loadouts. Basically, there is no need for what most wargamers call WYSIWYG. The models in the box represent units. Each unit has a selection of profiles - it might have one with a submachine gun and one with a sniper rifle, or one which is a Hacker. Lots of the depth of Infinity list-building comes from the great number of profiles one can select. Because it’s unrealistic for CB to produce models for all the profiles a unit has, no one expects WYSIWYG for weaponry. The Naga in the box can represent any of that unit’s 6 profiles, even though only 1 carries the boarding shotgun it is modelled with. No one will bat an eyelid at representing unit profiles in this way. The models in the Mazebreaker box don’t necessarily represent the weapons you’d want across an army list - 6 of 7 Corregidor models are armed with short-ranged SMGs - but this doesn’t really restrict us when drawing up army lists. 

Suggested 200 Point Army Lists

OSS Mazebreaker List

  • Shukra Lieutenant - Multi Rifle - 24
  • Sharvara Houndbot - light shotgun - 17
  • Naga - Multi Sniper Rifle - 29/1.5
  • Kinnara Scoutbot - Infiltration, Minelayer - 25/0.5
  • Dikpala - SMG, panzerfaust - 13
  • Dikpala - Paramedic, combi rifle - 15
  • Dikpala - HMG - 21/1
  • Dasyu - Minelayer(2), AP SMG - 30/0.5
  • Kinnara Scoutbot - Parachutist, SMG, E/M Carbine - 24
Total: 9 models, 198pts, 3.5SWC

This list is fine for learning games. It was impractical to include the Asura without going down to 8 models (cutting the Dasyu and one of the Scoutbots, while paring down some points on the Dikpala). As written, the list has some long range cover from the Naga sniper and Dikpala HMG, three close range brawlers in the Houndbot and Scoutbots, and a powerful Hidden piece in the Dasyu.

Corregidor (Surface Division) Mazebreaker List

  • Territorial Lieutenant - AP Red Fury - 30/1.5
  • Shifta - Chain of Command - 21
  • Coyote FTO - Multi Marksman Rifle - 26
  • Bambadroid - AP Mine Dispenser - 13
  • Bandit - Forward Observer - 24
  • Bearcat - Killer Hacker, Boarding Shotgun - 30
  • Alguacil Gatta - SMG, Contender - 10
  • Alguacil Gatta - SMG, E/Mitter - 10
  • Alguacil Gatta - SMG, Adhesive Launcher Rifle, Para Mines - 10
  • Vigilante - Hacker, Sensor, AP SMG - 25/0.5
Total: 10 models, 199pts, 2SWC.

This is a functional list for learning with 200pt games. It lacks real firepower past 24”, but with a Discoballer model (the Shifta), a midfield camouflaged model (the Vigilante) and a drop trooper (the Bearcat) it has some tools to get inside long ranged AROs. 6 of the 10 models are Specialists, so it should have plenty of tools to achieve objectives. 

Those lists will be usable against each other, but we’d encourage any paired new players, using this box to learn the game together, to experiment with tweaking them. Once you’ve tried swapping around different profiles for the units in the box, expand to proxying models as other units in the Sectorial (or you may already have bought more models by that point). When you’re ready, have a think about building your own lists from the full roster on the Army app. We have a guide to the principles of building a good list in N5 here. 

What Should I Buy Next?

A good rule of thumb for Infinity lists is that your cheaper units (in terms of points cost) are the ones that appear most often in lists, with the expensive models substituting for each other. This is driven by the deire to fit 15 models, or close to it, into 300pts, so a selection of cheap models is very helpful. Accordingly, for both factions, we heavily advise getting a box of ‘utility Remotes’: these are the Zonds Remotes Pack for Corregidor/Nomads, and the Rebots Remotes Pack for OSS/Aleph. Those boxes contain 2 Remote bodies each, which can be assembled with various options to serve as 4 different types, all of which are fairly cheap supporting units. Honestly, most experienced players of either faction can use 4 of those bodies (so 2 boxes) in their lists regularly.

For OSS, the Aleph Operations Pack is a 10-model box - big by Infinity standards - which offers some good value and useful units, but be aware that it contains 3 Dakini which are essentially replaced by Dikpala, so there’s unwanted redundancy. Because of those resculpts, it probably won’t be around for ever, so might be worth snapping up. The Reinforcements Aleph Pack Alpha contains 6 models that gel very nicely with the Mazebreaker set - there’s an Artalis engineer to support your various bots, and Riksha/Dawon Tacbots which are other types of low-medium cost, flexible troops. If you’re a full on OSS sicko, you might consider buying either of their TAGs, the fearsome Marut or redoubtable Maximus. 

For Corregidor, more of those cheaper, broadly useful troopers would be Corregidor Jaguars - which have their own box of 4, but are relatively old and might be updated soon - and Diablos, which are in the Corregidor Fireteam Beta box. Our other obvious shout out is the set of two Moran Masai. Those are almost signature troopers for Corregidor, we don’t leave home without them. Similarly, special characters Jazz and McMurrough are rightly famous units. Note that within Corregidor, Mazebreaker introduces a distinction between two ‘Divisions’ (see our rules article) so beware of boxes that include EVAders, Sombra, the Gator TAG, Tomcats (and their special character Carlota Kowalsky) and Hellcats. Those units probably won’t be usable in the same list as the new Mazebreaker units, although they are things you might want in your collection eventually, as most of Corregidor is still units that can play in either Division. The Iguana Squadron TAG is a great pick for a big, nasty centrepiece model. 

Conclusion - Final Thoughts and Verdict on the Box

Genghis: I have been yearning for some tweaks to Corregidor for a long time; this is a wave of fresh content, and I still have plenty of questions about how it will all fit together. But it hammers home the style (both aesthetically and in-game) of why they are my original & forever faction. Their iconic models are highly skilled, well equipped Medium Infantry, while still only being mid-cost models in the wider scheme of the game. When they tackle enemy TAGs, I feel like a giant killer. Musterkrux: I’m also a big fan of the CJC Underdog aesthetic, even if they’re not my favorite Nomad faction (Bless you, Tunguska.). However, I differ in as much as I think that with CJC getting featured in Operation: Crimson Stone they shouldn’t have gotten this release slot. That said, the models, both profiles and sculpts, are lovely and I’m happy to have them even if the circumstances of their arrival doesn’t agree with me.

Genghis: Personally, I am planning on picking up 2 of the Beyond boxes. Shiftas, Coyotes and their Bambabot/Bambadroid buddies are all right up my street. I like their look and I will definitely want to throw that many into some of my lists. Musterkrux: Full disclosure, this box was not designed for me specifically. I have a relatively established Aleph force, including the contents of the Operation: Coldfront box, whose contents mapp very neatly with what we’re getting here in Mazebreaker. To be sure, this translates as: ‘The Coldfront contents were so good as a starter for Aleph players that they did it again’, so consider that an endorsement of the Aleph contents for new players (but less so for established Aleph Enjoyer). The CJC half is a good starter for someone new to Nomads but is also a solid pickup for a long-time collector. Overall, I rate the box for players who might be new to one of both of the factions but less so for people who already have both factions. 

Genghis: That’s very true, I think this is a box which is even more of a ‘wow factor’ for new players, than it is for existing players to pad their collections. What I was not expecting was to be overall impressed by the look of the OSS models, even more than by the Corregidor stuff. I’m not necessarily going to jump into playing them - death to the Aleph surveillance state! - but they will make wonderful antagonists for my Nomads. Those creepy robot faces. 

Top Model for Each Side?

Operations Sub-Section

Musterkrux: I think my winner is the Naga, even though the Boarding Shotgun profile is the least compelling loadout available for Nagas. I love the way she brandishes that Shotgun. The sculpt on the face is well-rendered and departs from the generic anime-vision of catwalk-strutting super-model aesthetics Infinity often leans into. Brava. Her pose communicates a degree of balance while in motion, throwing her hip in one direction while the shotgun counterbalances.

Genghis: I’m torn between the Houndbot and the Dasyu. Robot dog or robot face? Hmm, I’ll say the Dasyu. I wonder if I could proxy it as something in one of my other factions…

Corregidor

Genghis: Another tough one, but I’m going to say the Bearcat. He’s a chunky boy. 

Musterkrux: The Bearcat is sweet, not gonna lie. However, he scores zero out of three for Berets, Ponchos and Facemasks. So, my vote is for Coyote not-so-Ugly. 

That’s all folks - pre-orders for Mazebreaker should start at the end of May, so we hope you’re ready. We are on the edge of our seats waiting for the final details of rules changes to the factions involved.
