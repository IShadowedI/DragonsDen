BattleTech: Brawl in the Bluegrass Classic Tournament or 'My Bear Clock is Broken, What Time is It?'

May 8th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Howdy Mechwarriors and welcome back to another Goonha- I mean Tabletop Battles tournament Report! With me, your favorite writer: Liberty! Myself, Peri, Lynn, and our buddies Connor and Brenndan went all the way on out to Kentucky to go to the annual Brawl in the Bluegrass tournament! Peri, Connor, and my journey out, and subsequent Bloodright shenanigans, were covered previously! That means that this week we’re getting straight into the meat and potatoes of why we’re here!

Killin’ Folk.

Brawl in the Bluegrass 2026

Brawl has been going on for a while now and has grown to be, currently, the biggest Classic Battletech Tournament on the East Coast, if not in the US overall! As I said in the other article this event is set in the beautiful state of Kentucky, though more specifically the city of Lexington!

I’ll be honest the entire time I was there I was humming the theme of the show ‘Justified’ to myself but no one in the car actually got the reference, unfortunately. Leaving our, honestly quite nice and ‘real people live here most of the time’ themed Air BnB we got under way to the hotel the tournament was in and along the way stopped.

For the Tradition.

Waffle House, staple that it is, provided the nourishment needed for the day, at least until Lunchtime. As always I am a slut for reasonably priced breakfast foods. The All Star, blessed be its potatoes, eggs, toast, meats, and Waffle, continues to be my dish of choice. What else would I get? Just a Waffle?

Don’t be ridiculous.

With breakfast ingested, coffee injected, bills paid, and a day of games ahead of us we made our way up the road with our mini boxes and badges in tow! We got there on time again, hopefully setting a new trend, and got to hang out and chat with some other players about the lists we had all brought! So, what did I bring?

—

The List

So, as you may remember from my traipse on down to Southern Assault V, and bringing the Bear Necessities to South Carolina Classic: I love the Kodiak. Pair this with the fact that Peri had challenged me to run Kodiaks until it stopped working and we’ve got an easy path forward. Pair this once again with the fact that the tournament is allowing duplicate variants and has a 10K BV cap and you have QUITE the easy path forward:

Two Bears Highfiving.

What is Two Bears Highfiving, exactly? Well, that’s simple, and not one of the ‘Mechs in the list need a real introduction:
  • Two(2) Kodiak Standards at 4/5
  • Two(2) Phantom Cs at 4/5
  • Two(2) 5 Man Squads of Salamanders at 4/2
This list is nasty. It’s hateful. It’s… well it’s perfect.

So perfect in fact that during testing and practice games against my friends I felt it necessary to go make something a bit more reasonable for them to test into. This list is me. It’s heavy and driving, it wants for nothing more than to catch a line and hammer down that line into the enemy formation, breaking them clean in half. It’s fun, it scratches my itches for big guns and SRMs, it’s fast, it’s threatening, and it leaves questions, puzzles, and dead ‘Mechs all across the battlefield.

And I don’t end up bringing it.

See, I’ve played a lot of Kodiaks at this point. Enough that even I, Big Bear’s No.1 Fan, started to get… bored…

And that practice list is lookin’ really fun. So what is that practice list? Well, we gave it the operating name ‘Task Force Dancer’ because it was all about testing if the opponent could deal with having to handle ‘everything around you has 3 or more TMM, is low cost BA, and is probably in woods, or is a damnable Gauss truck up on the far hill at the back of the map. This… is fun. It’s new. It’s fresh. It’s…

It’s the Canon operation standards of the Colonial Marshals.

—

Task Force Dancer

This list is born, as I said, from my boredom of just beating people to death with Big Bears. My soul craved Cowboy shenanigans. Craved weird shit and thematic applications. So it did it. As a mental blow off valve I made this weird, annoying ass, cannon style Colonial Marshals list.

And I loved every second that I played with it. It’s incredibly mobile. Makes the opponent think about keeping their head down. Is on the objective whenever it wants to be and no where near it when it doesn’t. It’s just so stupid that most lists don’t have a real answer to it and that starts making me have bad thoughts. Makes me grow attached to these little blorbos that have developed a little personality as I’ve played them.

Makes me… sentimental.

So, what do these idiots drive? Well, let’s see!

Task Force Dancer, Protect the Burger Town! Credit, Liberty

Command Marshal Bert ‘Innocent’ Chilton: a 2/4 Pilot, rides out in his King Crab KGC-001 ‘Pipe Bomb’. This is a volatile shitbucket of a ‘Mech but in a world where it gets to just vibe at the back of the map it is hitting you on 6+ your defensives at long range. It routinely pulls 8s on things that can’t even retort, and it only gets better the closer you get. Unfortunately this does mean it will cost 3,420 fucking BV. As well it was required that someone be named the CO of the force, and of course Marshal Bert here took that honor.

Marshal Jane ‘Shovel Head’ Doe: a 3/3 Pilot, soars onto the battlefield in her Taurian built Warhammer WHM-10T ‘Screaming Eagle’. I’ll be honest, Warhammers shouldn’t be allowed to move like this. I’ll also be honest, I picked this thing for a few reasons and one of them is the fact that it is the most efficient way to get a 20+ damage DFA that can reach out 7 or more hexes that isn’t some weirdo Solaris ‘Mech. Weird requirement I know but having the ability to just say ‘look you need to be shooting at this thing this turn or things are gonna go real fucking bad for the target’ is something I’ve been realizing is incredibly powerful. Would I trade those ERMLs for an extra IJJ? Yes. Do I control that? … Well, no.

Marshal Rayland ‘Crunch’ Samson: A 3/5 Pilot, seeks to be the biggest nuisance this side of the periphery in his beloved Marshal MHL-6FR ‘Beetle Back’. After all, it’s not a Colonial Marshals list without a 6FR around.

Marshal Anna ‘Zip’ Marden: A 5/4 Pilot screaming across the battlefield in her advanced tech Cadaver CVR-T1 ‘Ditch-Digger’. Anna’s the newest member of the Task Force but an indispensable part of the unit as she works to spot targets and suppress enemy electronics to let the big hitters do their work!

Marshal Posses Harvest and Sow: A pair of 4/4 Tinstar squads that ride into the fight aboard Screaming Eagle and Beetle Back. Ready to exploit the holes rent open by Pipe Bomb’s heavy handed fire.

What a way to spend 10,000 BV. Well, 9,999 BV, to be precise. Also, of note, god I am incredibly proud of these miniatures. They came out so good.

—

The Fit

Starting off we’ve got the familiar pair of boots, handmade leather belt, a good pair of gray canvas-y jeans, and properly fitted black Stetson Bozeman, top it off with a slick handlebar mustache and my white bloused button up with the sleeves rolled up and you’ve got a good look! But what of a coat? Well…

I finally did it! I got a poncho! A real nice one! It’s alpaca wool from Ecuador! It’s comfy, breathable, and definitely not the exact same pattern as the one Marty McFly wore in Back to the Future III. That’d be ridiculous.

—

Round 1: Dig, Gravedigger, Dig.

Fill your hands you son of a bitch.

Round 1 is a scenario named ‘Half a League Onward’ on a map that my notes only referred to as ‘Desert Hell’. My opponent this round is the winner of Brawl in the Bluegrass for the last two years running, one Will ‘Vapor’ Donte. Our, relatively short, pre-game chat was nice, going over each other’s list and laughing at the madness inherent to them both.

For his part Vapor decided to pack in a Clan Wolf Star, from the 'Play Doh Galaxy' as he put it, comprising of a 4/4 Alpha Wolf B, a 4/2 Wulfen D, a 3/4 Linebacker F, a 4/4 Valkyrie C, a 4/4 Fenris J, and a 4/3 squad of Elementals with Flamers! This is a nasty force that I'm gonna have to be kinda careful around. As long as the mission doesn't force me to bring the King in close though I think the odds should be in my favor.

Play Doh Galaxy stands ready upon their drop disk, Credit: Liberty

This mission is… well it’s something. It’s got a lot of modifiers but the core aim of the mission is to get your guys across the center of the board to score points. As well if the commander, and the entire force alongside it, gets across the centerline of the board then you get bonus points. There are also points for kills and the first to 100 primary OBJ points locks the opponent out from scoring.



On top of this there were some things we didn’t notice, namely the fact that when you crossed the center line the first time you get a free BSP strike. Any crits taken on your side of the board were at +2, and any melees done on your opponents side of the board was at a -2.

But there’s one big thing I failed to clock: There were still kill points, and you could get the points for killing someone more than once. See, my Tinstars, Warhammer, Marshal, and Cadaver were all well fast enough to get on his side of the field and start scoring for their lives. But Marshal Chilton in the KGC certainly wasn’t. This, if I was operating at full mental capacity and could read- or hadn’t been handed the full A4 sheet that contained the scenario rules with the round timer already ticking -would actually be fine. They’d be scoring quite rapidly and would hopefully work in such a manner as to close the inevitable primary lock. All I need to do is not drive the King Crab forward.

So here’s a picture of the King Crab definitely not doing that.

No, Wait, Don't do that, Command Marshal! Credit, Liberty

Fuck.

SO, this goes terribly. The Linebacker, which I entirely forgot had SRM-Infernos after it had just thrown them at my Tinstars, runs up and super heats the King Crab after it goes ham into the rear arc of the Alpha Wolf. It savages the Alpha Wolf, popping the gyro and putting it to the floor, something that, had I remembered the fact that DFAs always hit rears against prone targets I would’ve just started stepping on him with the Warhammer until it died so other stuff could’ve gotten some work. Really this whole match has been a comedy of errors on my part so far.

It avoids the shutdown and the ammo explosion this round but next it unfortunately takes an Ice Ferret to the back. An Ice Ferret that immediately rips the rear side torso open, gets two crits and finds the ammo bin, sending Pipebomb into the stratosphere to follow its name.

And here I was hoping painting the name on it would act as a good luck charm.

This minor issue turns into a genuine fade run as the Warhammer falls to a triple crit in the side torso from the Ice Ferret that only hits the engine, thus putting it down, the Marshal gets crippled by the Ice Ferret clubbing it to pieces, and both squads of Tinstars are also dead… to the Ice Ferret. By the end of this game the only thing I had left was the Cadaver that was running around being a nuisance.

I killed an Alpha Wolf and a Wulfen for my trouble.

I lose the round 125(+however kill points work out)-60(+however kill points work out), finally tasting a defeat that I’ve not found in a tournament since the 2024 Richmond Open. I did not like this taste, but there is little I can do for it now but remember to read the fucking scenario in full in the future. Can’t believe I forgot my fuckin’ ADHD meds.

Now, down to the loss bracket for me!

Round 2: Work that Shovel with a Vigor, Gravedigger

Two paint jobs? Is that allowed?

Round 2 is on a city map stacked up against an opponent who, going by my lack of a name in my notes, I believe asked to not be named. They’re running a pretty fun concept, though that they said worked better in the Alpha Strike list, with a primary force of Federated suns ‘Mechs backed up by some Republic of the Sphere remnants! The paint schemes are quite striking and look astounding on the table.

Two Forces Clash, Probably a Training Exercise... Right? Credit, Liberty

Now, I don’t remember exactly what the variants were as it seems I didn’t actually write them down? I’ll be honest my note taking on this one was lacking, I was still futzing with bits from the MREs we had brought along for lunch.

I’m fairly certain they had a Templar O, a Scourge WD1, an Ostsol 8D, a Jackalope BD, and a Nightstalker of… some variety?

Fed Suns AND Republic Soldiers? Greedy Greedy! Credit: Liberty

Scenario wise we’ve got a mode that everyone knows, and many love: Karnov Down. Between our lists we’re both quite certain I’ve got the movement to get there on both points before he does, and as such I fully intend to leverage this to my advantage. In rolling off I got to choose my side and chose the one I did for one reason: a level 5 building within my 3 hex deployment zone.

I see no God Up here, other than ME, Credit: Liberty

Now, what I meant by ‘I had the movement’ is the fact that the Cadaver zipped off the board with one of the points on turn 2, having been hit by one cERML on the approach and suffering no following on the way back out. By turn 5 the Marshal and Warhammer had made off with the other one, having left their payloads of Tinstars behind to guard the point.

The Jackalope attempts to pursue the Marshal and Warhammer, getting into the rear arc of the Marshal and landing some good hits that pop one of its Heat Sinks. This is inconvenient but certainly not the end of the world.

The Templar’s entrance to the fight was rude, rude in that for about 3 turns it was only allowed to exchange fire with the King Crab, shots that were on about 4 better than his. The Templar falls on its ass in 3 consecutive turns before getting safe from the Crab as the Scourge gets there to stand in as the bait to keep his guns off him while a squad of Tinstars decides they really want his ass.

The King Crab begins exchanging close in and concerted fire with the scourge, a fight in which it rarely misses, staring down with disgust at the offending ground-bound heavy ‘Mech for its impudence to stand to the King on High. In this long drawn out fight the KGC takes a TAC to the side torso and I nearly shit myself, luckily the LRM-15 eats the hit and I simply lose a gun that I was too close to employ effectively anyhow. By this point in the game we’ve been in the thick of it and the Warhammer and the King Crab are down all of their head armor with internal damage from 5 point hits.

YOU DARE STAND AGAINST ME?!?! Credit, Liberty

In the final round of the game the Warhammer flies up onto the roof of a building, sights her guns, decides to go up the heat scale knowing it’s about to be time to bail and hammers three hits into the Templar’s Right Torso.

And pulls 6 Crits.

The Templar O has 7 Crits in that location, 3 of them are engine and one of them is SRM ammo. There is no surviving this. Interestingly what I miss is the ammo so the ‘Mech is fully salvageable! At some point I’ll have to paint a Templar up in Marshal colors for the meme I suppose!

I win in the last turn as the Ostsol eats the floor and cripples itself while the KGC falls on his back and hits armor, failing to meaningfully harm himself any further, something that very well could’ve happened. Also I forgot to get the points…

Round 3: That big old hole just keeps gettin' bigger

Who bombed this city? Was it me? Was it you? Was it… them? I bet it was them, Steiner bastards.

Round 3 is a scenario named ‘They Call Him Baba-Yaga’ on a ruined city map! The goal here is to headhunt the opponents CO and keep yours alive and unidentified. Now, unfortunately, this has a hiccup: There’s only one way to actually force this identification: having an active probe. The opponent can reveal their CO in exchange for a one time action but they didn’t really amount to much and seemed like something you’d do if you really kinda had to to hopefully save their skin.

Out for a good ol' sunday jaunt in the city ruins! Credit, Liberty

I… Well I don’t have one, the Reaches get 3 mechs with them and one’s a fuckin’ Urbie, and I don’t remember the packet telling me it was something I should try and have so…  Well, we’re working on intuition!

And intuit I shall! There’s a great big ‘Mech over there and I’m gonna go get its ass!

My opponent this round is Matt ‘The Northman’ Ethington who is playing a Steiner force! In this force we’ve got a Cyclops CP-11-G at 4/5, a Gladiator GLD-5R at 4/4, an Incubus at 4/4, a Hellspawn HSN-10G at 3/5, a Night Stalker NSR-K1 at 4/4, and a Hellhound II at 4/4! Of worry here is that Night Stalker as it has a probe so it is a definite public enemy no.1 if I can get a good hold of it.

Now, friendly reader, take a moment and think. Which of these ‘Mechs do you think is the CO?

Got it in your head? Good. Now, let’s say it together.

Ready?

The Cyclops. Ok, now that we’re both working on the same assumption let’s get to it!

Look at them over there, thinking they're SAFE, Credit, Liberty

The game starts with the Marshal and Whammer pushing up to drop the Tinstars in screening positions for the King Crab before them and the Cadaver moved up to do a skirmish line against the opposing force, hoping to get the Cyclops in a good spot to beat to death. The King Crab has ambled up onto the roof of a ruined building and is setting about ‘The Work™’ as it slings Gauss Rifle slugs at everything that moves in its eyeline.

Before long the Night Stalker makes a break for it at the King Crab, obviously intent on making the ID check on him. The Cadaver breaks in return to intercept and get the big man under their ECM cover to turn off the probe and keep the secret safe. This… well it isn’t necessary as the King Crab turns, sights up, and rams a gauss slug squarely into the Night Stalker’s Hip, making it into something not at all threatening while the Incubus and Hellspawn make their break for the backline as well.

It’s at about this point that the Warhammer DFA’s the Cyclops, knocking it to the ground while staying upright. The Cyclops beefs it and goes unconscious which results in it taking aimed shots from the Marshal, and Warhammer, squarely into the CT, finally putting it down.

THAT'S RIGHT, GET DROPKICKED, Credit, Liberty

And revealing that it is NOT the commander.

Shit.

With this we’re nearly out of time and my attention shifts to the Hellhound for the two turns we have remaining. It’s not going to be enough but if I can threaten it right then maybe I can bait something out. Unfortunately I don’t manage it.

Meanwhile the King Crab is still being bullied to no end by the Incubus, Hellhound, and dying Night Stalker. Well, until he turns and slams both Gauss Slugs and the LPL into the Incubus’ right leg, shearing it and the torso clean off, sending it to the ground. Where it takes the first head hit… and passes out. Next turn he stops, sights up, and aimed shots the same salvo squarely into the CT, obliterating the ‘Mech and ignoring the Hellspawn that’s attempting to bother it.

I win on time and points, hitting the 100 point lockout in the last round with a final score of 125-40… well that plus the Kill points from killing his Cyclops, Night Stalker, and Incubus.

That’s a 2/1 W/L ratio for today, interestingly the same set I had when I was just starting out in 2024 at Richmond Open!

Awards

I, obviously, did not win. That was Vapor, my first round opponent and 3 year champion of Brawl in the Bluegrass! Honestly if I was gonna lose to anyone on this one I’m happy it’s the man who won overall and someone with a history here! Nor did I go home empty handed! I got 2nd best painted because I did a lot of conversion work to make the Warhammer into a WYSIWYG 10T and to build my Cadaver! At some point I intend to do some articles about my kitbashing which should be fun!

So what did I take home for my 2nd place painting? Well it felt only natural to take home the Periphery offering on the table so I left with a framed plaque and a copy of Field Manual Periphery! It’s been an interesting read and a fun time seeing what the state of the Periphery was leading into the Jihad!

This tournament was a blast with another great few days of fun competition, conversation, and friends new and old! If you’re thinking of going to a tournament near you honestly just do it. As long as you go in with fun expectations you’re liable to have a good time and meet new people you obviously share an interest with which is a great thing that everyone needs more of in this world of ours!

Now, none of us actually won, but the Steak Dinners were never really about the winning, they were about spending time with your friends after a good day of gaming and fun! So, let’s get to it!

Steak Time!

Dinner

Fuck Winnin’ I got a damned good Steak.

This time around we got to go to a real local place named Columbia Steakhouse, and it was astounding. I got the Nighthawk Special, a beef tenderloin broiled in garlic butter and served with a heaping helping of monstrous steak fries and a big old bowl of the day’s vegetables.

Sweet Delicious STEAK! And BEER! I swear it wasn't actually Mich or Bud, I'm better than that, I swear. Credit, Liberty

God, look at it, that might be one of the best steaks I’ve ever had. Shit I might go back to Brawl next year just to get it again.

After this we got ice cream at another local place which was equally great. Afterwards we got on home to the ABnB, settled in, got cleaned up, and went to bed to drive on home in the morn.

Final Thoughts

So, I’ve got a, somewhat minor, complaint this time. For TOs, when it comes to your missions I beg, beseech, and plead with you: Either release them early, or give time prior to the start of the clock for the players to review the scenario in full with their opponent to ensure both of them fully understand what they are doing. Every game I played I found myself and my opponent, and just about every table around us, calling over one of the TOs constantly to attempt to get clarifications and ask questions about intent. Alternatively, if you want them secret to players so they can’t list build around them just do what Hoovie of the South Carolina Classic did:

Release them after your mandatory submittal date. Then, on top of that, block out time to allow yourself to audibly review the scenario with the entire field so that any questions they may have are given such that everyone can hear them and the answer.

That said, this was a great time. Between the drive out, the tournament itself, the trial of bloodright, the run out to IWM, the time spent with friends I’ve met and cultivated at tournaments over the last year and new ones I look forward to meeting once again! If dates and times line-up I will absolutely be back again next year!

Remember, even with all of our copious talk of going hard at tournaments, my adoration of competition, and gunning for a win, what matters is having a good time and doing it all with a smile on your face. All the ‘mech reviews, the pretty plastic and the shiny mathrocks aren’t worth a damn if you don’t use them to have fun with friends! So go grab a drink, a good pack of snacks, your favorite game partner(s), a box of funny ‘mechs, a cozy spot, and a fun map then get to making some good memories, at home or at a tournament!

Until next time! Fair weather, good seas and happy gamin’! Go tear shit up out there and have fun!
