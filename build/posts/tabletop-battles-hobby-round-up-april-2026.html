Tabletop Battles Hobby Round-Up: April 2026

May 1st, 2026 via Goonhammer

Welcome to the Tabletop Battles Hobby Round-Up for April of 2026!  See all the cool things we painted this month and got inspired by after the twin punches of Adepticon and Salute!  So let's dig in!



"Contemptor" Kevin Stillman

After Adepticon, I was ready to paint paint paint.  My suitcases were filled with models and new paints to try out.  And so try out I did!

My first minis for April were Sergeant Metarus and a squad of Bladeguard Veterans.  I got started on these in March, but didn't finish them up until April.

Veteran Sergeant Metaurus. Credit: Kevin Stillman

Bladeguard Veterans. Credit: Kevin Stillman

Then, I went all in on StarCraft Terrans at Adepticon.  My goal was to get the entire Terran starter minis done before the end of April, and so I spent my birthday weekend at the paint desk.

Credit: Contemptor Kevin Stillman

Credit: Contemptor Kevin Stillman

Credit: Contemptor Kevin Stillman

Credit: Contemptor Kevin Stillman

Then it was onto Orks from our Combat Patrol Reviews.  This wound up being a bit more of a challenge because of an unexpected trip halfway across the United States for funeral reasons, but I was able to finish up 10 Ork Boyz.

Ork Boyz Part 2. Credit: Kevin Stillman

I had one heck of a productive April, but that's nothin' compared to May.

 

Campbell "SRM" McLaughlin

Somehow it's been only a couple weeks since Adepticon; I wrote up an absolute boatload of words about that experience if you're in need of yet more reading material while slacking off at work.

Ultramarines Ballistus Dreadnought. Credit SRM

I meant to have this guy done for Adepticon, but a confluence of crummy situations conspired to keep me away from my painting desk, as I'll get to in a moment. Once I could really focus on painting though, this was a blast to knock out - Dreadnoughts are seemingly the only vehicle models I actually enjoy painting. I went hard with my 2nd edition-style hazard stripes and hot rod flames, and even got to use a bunch of the recent Ultramarines upgrade bits and transfers. In the two games I've used him he's almost been useful, which is about as good as I can say for a Ballistus.

Blazco, from Animal Adventures Cats and Catacombs. Credit: SRM

On St. Patrick's Day last month, our sweet orange lad Blazco sadly passed away from heart failure. I had been given a set of these Animal Adventures minis, and painted up a little tribute to my fallen friend. It was a simple mini, easy to knock out in an afternoon, but one that captured his gormless expression, assumed class of bard, and copious amounts of orange fluff. It helps to have literal thousands of reference photos. His basing also has some rocks from our neighborhood, tying him in with the place he lived.
 


Lyran Commonwealth Caesar, Griffin, and Savannah Masters. Credit: SRM

I wanted some momentum before the new Cities of Sigmar stuff dropped, so I grabbed a couple of the Battletech salvage boxes I had lying around and knocked out this little bunch of mechs and vehicles. I already had a Griffin (being the first mech I'd painted after getting back into the game) but the salvage box gave me this one with the boombox missile pods so into my Lyran collection it goes. The Savannah Masters were also fun little vehicles to paint, though if they were any smaller they'd just look like little lumps of PVC.

Next month I'm likely gonna be going hard on Cities of Sigmar stuff; I was also hoping to have the Freeguild Gallants and other models from City of Ash ready to go here, but losing a second pet in just over a month hit my household hard, and a bunch of travel has also kept me away from my painting desk. Here's to a better May, with more models to show for it.

Jefferson Powers

I got quite a bit of painting done this month. First off we have some civilians for Guards of Traitor's Toll, along with the last two guards.

This first batch were put together from the sprues that come with the starter set.

Traitor's Toll civilians. Photo: Jefferson Powers

This second batch of civilians are from Midlam Miniatures. I needed a few models from them for a different project and added these to pad out my order. I think they're perfect for Traitor's Toll, and the scale lines up perfectly.

Townsfolk from Midlam Miniatures. Photo: Jefferson Powers

On the left below are the last two guards that I didn't get done last month. On the right we have two random models that should fit in great with the other civilians. The guy on the left was a promo miniature from Footsore. I don't know where the Llama came from, it was in a bargain bin at a local game shop. He sure looks shifty though doesn't he?

Traitor's Toll guards and a few random civilians. Photo: Jefferson Powers

I did these two Reaper models with the idea of using them in Traitor's Toll as well, but the scale doesn't match for the presumably human-sized grave digger (he's quite a bit bigger). Maybe once he's viewed at table distance with a bunch of buildings and other figures it won't be so noticeable. Scale doesn't really matter for the ogre or troll or whatever he is, so he might still make it into the game.

Reaper gravedigger and assistant. Photo: Jefferson Powers

I also painted this set of Spartoi from Crooked Dice. I have no idea what I'll use them for, they just caught my eye and were a quick paint job, something to do while waiting for other stuff to dry.

Are they robots or skeletons? Maybe both. Spartoi by Crooked Dice. Photo: Jefferson Powers

Finally, we have the best name for a set of miniatures ever: A Fistful of Clints, from Great Escape Games. I had a lot of fun with these -- Dead Man's Hand is one of my favorite games that I never play, so maybe this will get me closer to actually getting it to the table again.

A Fistful of Clints. Photo: Jefferson Powers

Rockfish

I'm well into the depths of pre-edition backlog clearing, and that makes for a wild mix of things to end up in here! I'll open with the assortment of different preview models that I worked on, unfortunately a couple of these units were competing for time before the reviews dropped so didn't make it in.

Thulia Ghuld. Credit: Rockfish

Hastarii Fusiliers. Credit: Rockfish

Tyranid Prime with Lash Whip. Credit: Rockfish

A fun assortment of different goobers, unfortunately for me painting admech kinda makes me want to get some more to populate my army with some of the more recent models that have joined the faction since I was last working on it.

Chimera. Credit: Rockfish

Kasrkin. Credit: Rockfish

Guard as ever is one of my favourite armies to work on and I had some fun things lurking around so I bashed these out fairly quick.

Vespid. Credit: Rockfish

Crisis Battlesuits - Sunforge. Credit: Rockfish

When I was writing this I got held up on the varnishing of some crisis suits so they may or may not manage to get in here, but these vespid are in safely regardless! (Follow up, I managed it evidently!)



As always I worked on some battletech models as little breaks from other stuff scattered in there throughout the month, I'm making really good progress on clearing my backlog as I only have like five or so distinct mechs for this scheme hanging around! There's some assorted duplicates of stuff I need to figure out what exactly I'll do with, but that's ok.

Sam Isaacson (not Josh)

The game that my hobby orbits around is Conquest, which for the most part is a large-scale, rank-and-flank game. I'm therefore currently painting 26 of the same model while waiting for another 30 to arrive - the verbatim message I sent to the co-hosts of the Joshing Around in Eä podcast earlier this week was "There's nothing like spending an hour painting a single colour and knowing you're barely over halfway through".

To simply focus on one, nice model is therefore a special treat for me, and this month brought with it the opportunity for an extra special treat.

2024 included a birthday with a zero on the end of it, and I used some of my birthday money to find a model that would feel brilliant to paint. The one I chose was Gardus Steel Soul, a character from Age of Sigmar's Stormcast Eternals. I don't play that game so have no idea about his role on the battlefield, but what I can say is that it's difficult to find many reference images for this online, which makes me think that people feel like (a) it's not a competitive model to add to an army, and/or (b) the model looks derpy.

Regardless, I love him.

Gardus Steel Soul painted by Sam Isaacson (not Josh)

Gardus Steel Soul painted by Sam Isaacson (not Josh)

Gardus Steel Soul painted by Sam Isaacson (not Josh)

Gardus Steel Soul painted by Sam Isaacson (not Josh)

I'm always keen on models that stand out as different, and I'm a bit fed up of seeing minis  described as "dynamic" because their foot is resting on a rock. Gardus Steel Soul's dynamism comes from his superhero landing pose, which defines the shape of the base and causes his coat tails to fly into the air, throwing his hair upwards. It has a silhouette that's recognisable from 40 feet away and makes it a challenge and delight in equal measure.

I'm not the most skilled painter - by a long shot - but I'm really happy with this little man that is now sitting in pride of place on my bookshelf. The armour uses my current favourite paint - Vallejo Xpress Velvet Red, which has a gorgeous matte finish and feels like it paints itself - alongside a base of the ever-reliable Retributor Armour. I used Coelia Greenshade as a wash for the gold, to add some nice contrast against the red, and then did some freehand lightning and drybrushing with the classic Vallejo Goblin Green, which I'm pretty happy with.

Mike Bettle-Shaffer

A slightly slower hobby month for me this time around, with a huge amount of assembly and a bit more progress on my Adeptus Custodes project. The Dystopian Wars boats were definitely trickier to build than I'd perhaps planned for, and I had some long sessions at the spray booth laying down the gold base coats on my Custodes. I did manage to get another bust to a point where I was happy with it, this time a Sister of Silence. I may still return to this one, as I'd like to push some more life into the rendering but I haven't decided on the how yet.



My local gaming group have recently started a Crusade to see off 10th edition, and it was only once we were underway that we realised none of us actually had any Boarding Actions terrain. I used this somewhat flimsy excuse to grab myself a set of the Bandua Boarding Actions scenery, and tried to get it together as fast as I could.



Rounding out the month for me, I had my first go at painting some tartan. After experimenting with various fine tipped pens, I honestly ended up preferring just using a decent brush. It felt easier to control, and allowed for some additional options when selecting colours. I did like how much easier it was to pop pupils in with the pens, so the experiment wasn't a total loss.

Scott "Brushwizard" Ferguson

It's been a hot minute since I've been able to join in on one of these!

I've been working on a few different projects this month, the main one being a new terrain set for Marvel: Crisis Protocol. This new table is currently being dubbed Neon Tokyo, and the idea is that it will be painted to simulate a night time vibe, complete with led lit interiors and fluorescent paints doing the heavy lifting to create a neon sign effect. Most of the terrain is from Corvus Games Terrain, with a few other pieces that I've randomly found too.

credit: Brushwizard

So far, I've been busy doing tests and proof of concept projects. I've painted a ramen shop, a couple digital billboards a few arcade cabinets for the interior of a larger building and wiring said large building. Most of my time has been spent tweaking digital files to accommodate wiring, leds and battery packs. But it's slowly coming together and soon I'll be making steady progress on it.

I've learned a lot about running and powering leds and working with clear filaments. I might redo the large billboard too, I'm still not 100% happy with the way the light transfers through the graphics. It's currently on revision 3, and I think with 1 more try I'll get it right. But I need to print a few other clear things first before I return to it.

credit: Brushwizard

Another thing I've been focused on lately is Bushido: Risen Sun from GCT Studios. While I was at Adepticon, I picked up an absolute pile of models so I could start playing that on days that we didn't feel like playing MCP. I picked up nearly all of the Shiho faction models, and was given a few extras as well.

So far, I've painted Shiho Hiroto, Shiho Kieta, Shiho Eishin, Somabo the Blue Crane and a couple of terrain pieces. These also allowed me to play around with some new basing effects as well, I bought a few packs of Gamer's Grass for this project while at Adepticon and I'm completely sold on them. The tall grass especially and those lotus flowers are a nice additional touch for Somabo.

It's been a nice break from super hero painting, and I'll be getting back to that as soon as I have my main team painted.

Josh 'Moogly' Boyes

WOW. It's been a while. Do you like Necromunda? I love Necromunda. Nearly all of my hobby over the past few months has been dedicated to getting my gang up and running, as well as playing in a great two-day campaign in Leeds.

I posted my plastics already months ago, but I've touched them up since then and gotten way better pictures of them.

'The Refused' - credit to Josh Boyes

These freaks are my pride and joy, and I love to loathe them. I've recently got them some Redemptionist backup on the way too.

The genestealer cult won't mind their grenade launcher being borrowed. Credit - Josh Boyes

All of these have been a challenge to try and not use metallics for anything, and I think I've succeeded on vibe so far if not technical execution. I'm happy either way I judge it, and I'm sticking my toes in customising my guys bit-by-bit too.

Loading...


This sucker was a joy to do up, got a lot of use out of my airbrush to do it, and sticking the stupid huge candles on his hunchback turned out way cooler than I figured it would.

I've added some classics to the mix as well. I've done up a few metal juves and gangers to join my lot in the Underhive.

WIP - Credit to Josh Boyes

It's so cool seeing them next to the modern plastics, they really hold up. The scared juve with a stub pistol in both hands is my absolute favourite, it's ridiculously grim.

What's he doing? His best. Get 'em, Rust munch. Credit - Josh Boyes

Aging less gracefully are the original plastics. A friend generously gifted me a few of the plastic Goliath & Orlock from the original starter set, and they were a lot of fun to paint up, by man. They are shallow when it comes to detail.

Models older than me, and yet somehow they look better. - Josh Boyes

Look at all that space for details! - Credit to Josh Boyes

No gang in the Underhive survives without a few hangers on, and the new hive-watcher from the new plastic sprue has been great so far for keeping pesky Delaque from webbing me from the word 'go'.

What's a primaris psyker doing down in the Underhive anyway? Credit - Josh Boyes

Any old mini is usually good enough to end up representing something in the hive, so my weirdo collection is always expanding with some borrowed legends from the guard.

Not directly related to my gang, but a neat little addition is this backdrop I got printed for me by a friend. I'm really chuffed with the result a bit of drybrushing and a sepia wash got me.

Expect to see this a lot! Credit - Josh Boyes

As for what's next? Well, I've got some Ridgewalkers that need some blunderpoles, and you can't go into the wastes without a car... and then there's all the terrain on top of that.

Loading...


Necromunda's going to be keeping me busy until 11th edition drops, and then my Black Templars will be back in action, hopefully alongside a brand new Genestealer army.

Carter "Saffgor" Kachmarik

April was a big month for me, and the Admech as a whole, because we've received the first release beyond pity heroes & Kill Teams in 6 years! Thulia Ghuld and the Hastarii might be a bit overcosted at present, but boy are they wonderful models.

Thulia Ghuld. Credit: Carter Kachmarik

Jokes have been made of Thulia's prodigious posterior, and I wanted to lean into a grotesque version of that, with her riding an arachnid matriarch into the fray. This entire kitbash was made possible via the plastic Shelob kit, which conveniently sized up perfectly for this use case. Thulia here is actually just atop the techno-organic spider, and you can see her little legs and shrimp-like posture if you look closely.

Archaeopter. Credit: Carter Kachmarik

I'm also working on adding some air support to my Admech, complete with additional buggy bits and some work-in-progress painting.

Hastarii Fusileers. Credit: Carter Kachmarik

Finally, I've got some Hastarii Fusileers in the works, based off the Sha'dar Hunter kit, as dedicated monster hunters aiming to add more tallies to their legend. Ultimately, given I've played Data-Psalm Conclave at every non-teams event since the codex, I'm mostly excited for Thulia swinging at S11, but Fusileers ignoring cover seems prescient for 11th Edition.
