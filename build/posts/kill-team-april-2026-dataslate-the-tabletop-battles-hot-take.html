Kill Team April 2026 Dataslate: The Tabletop Battles Hot Take

April 30th, 2026 via Goonhammer
It’s that magical time once again, Kill Team players. After a quick patch to Murderwing in March (which you can hear our take on here), Games Workshop has released a full balance dataslate, with some pretty substantial balance changes across a wide spread of teams. As always, we’ve got quite a bit to cover here, so we’re going to hop into this without further ado. 

Video Review

If you would like to watch the video review of the April Dataslate, our friend Can You Roll a Crit? has one available you can watch below.

Core Rules Updates

No major rules changes here, but there are two errata to speak of. The first is a minor clarification about ignoring changes to weapons stats and how it interacts with damage reduction. Ignoring weapon stat changes does not ignore damage reduction, but it does ignore abilities that would change the damage profile of a weapon. Pretty straightforward.

The other errata here is about climbing, which continues to be a rules bugbear for Kill Team. Here, the design team clarifies how climbing over multiple terrain features or different parts of terrain in one action. There's some charts here to help make it clearer, but the short of it is you can continue to climb as long as the second terrain feature is within 1” horizontally and 3” vertically of the first. If you visualise stairs for this, you’re on the right track. 

Approved Ops Updates



Some big buffs here for two Seek and Destroy ops, and a minor bump for Martyrs. First, it’s been made clear that Martyrs does still work even if the damage that incapacitated the operative isn't from an enemy operative, which is nice.

As for Seek and Destroy, both Dominate and Sweep and Clear picked up some big buffs. Firstly, scoring for Sweep and Clear has been reworked. Now, you get 1VP performing a clear action on an objective as long as no enemy operatives contest it at the end of the turning point, which becomes 2 VP if that objective also has a swept token on it. Additionally, you can score 1 VP if you control an objective with a swept token on it. It's a little hard to parse this change at first, but once you’re able to puzzle it together its clear how this makes Sweep and Clear a much easier Tac Op to score.

Fortunately, the Dominate buff is much easier to understand. Enemy operatives with 12 wounds or more now award 2 Dominate tokens when incapacitated, turning this tac op from a non-starter against elite teams into a potentially spicy pick. CYRAC: The Sweep and Clear change is very nice. The issue is you’ll still never run it now as Dominate just went from amazing to one of the best Tac Ops in the game.

Team Updates

Battleclade. Credit: 40khamslam

Battleclade

Some big changes to Battleclade this time around, which is very welcome on a team that’s struggled to find its footing since release. 
  • The Servitor Underseer can use their Network Override ability twice in a turning point, and it can also be used for a free dash.
  • Incantation of the Iron Soul now reduces damage on a 4+ instead of a 5+.
  • Concealed Apparatus now also works on your big Gun Servitors.
  • The Neurocyclic Reserve Cells equipment now works once per turning point, instead of d3 times per game.
Great changes overall, especially for the Underseer and Incantation of the Iron Soul. Giving the Underseer an extra chance to use Network Override as well as more flexibility on how it can be used opens up that model to some great plays, and Incantation working on a 4+ suddenly makes Battleclade a pretty sturdy team. It’s still probably one of the more difficult teams to play, but now there's more bang for your buck. 

Blades of Khaine

Just one change here, bumping up Striking Scorpion operatives to a 3+ armor save, making them almost on par with Exarchs in durability against shooting. While also bringing them inline with their 40k statline. 

HappyRaccoon: This will be a pretty sizable buff in the sense that Aeldari teams tend to get shot up, so making their sneaky front line more durable will be felt.

CYRAC: This is a victory for all Aeldari players everywhere. Will it massively improve the kill team? No. Will it boost morale? 100%!

Blooded

The Glory Kill ploy now no longer requires the selected enemy operative to be visible, opening up some gnarly strategies for Blooded players to ambush and incapacitate key models.

Canoptek Circle

Credit: Dan "Swiftblade" Richardson

Canoptek Circle continues to suffer for its sins of dominating the metagame at the end of 2025 with a nerf to Tomb Crawlers, taking them down from 21 wounds to 18 wounds. That old wound stat was brutal for most teams' ability to take out a Crawler before it could heal up, and Canoptek players are going to need to be much more careful with their big robots with this new wound total.

CYRAC: It’s a good and needed reduction but I would have liked another change to help Canoptek interact with the opponent more in regards to the Kill Op due to how powerful Expendable is now as a rule.

Death Korps

The Spot action from the Spotter now applies until the end of the turning point, rather than just one shooting action, so a few gunners hanging out near the Spotter can really light up an enemy trying to hide behind some light cover.

Elucidian Starstriders

The Stake Claim marker is removed from the killzone at the end of the turning point instead of at the start of the ready step of the following strategy phase. A bit of a nothingburger change here, just making it extra clear that this marker doesn’t last between turning points.

Hearthkyn Salvagers

A few changes here to note for the team.
  • The Ancestors are Watching now lets you ignore injury when making the free shoot or fight action as a part of the ploy.
  • Jump Pack Warrior now moves 8”, up from 7”.
  • The Lugger operative now gives an extra CP at the start of turning point 1, as well as an extra equipment option.
None of these changes are necessarily groundbreaking for the team, but they’re all pretty solid. The Jump Pack Warrior in particular will love the extra movement, as now it can move even faster than elves and its flying charge range is the same as a 6” move operative.

Hierotek Circle

Rejoice, Hierotek players, the Kill Team design team has walked back the change to how Reanimation interacted with the Kill Op. Hierotek Operatives once again reduce your opponent's Crit Op when they stand back up, rather than potentially scoring for your opponent's Kill Op multiple times each time they reanimate. This rule was extremely punishing, and probably one of the biggest things keeping this team in the gutter. Reversing the Kill Op change likely means we will start seeing Hierotek back on tables, though probably still not as dominant as they were earlier this edition.

CYRAC: It’s funny as it feels like GW has given up with trying to balance Hierotek. It’s pretty concerning that they walked back the Kill Op change as now Hierotek are basically back at full power again. I think kill grade 8 but reanimated operatives not reverting the kill grade would have been the best compromise but we’ll need to see how it plays out over the next 3 months.

Kasrkin

Kasrkin. Credit: SRM

A clarification here to Veteran Leadership here; you cannot use the Veteran Leadership rule form the Sergeant to double up on the same Skill at Arms ability on an operative. So no double reducing damage from stacking Ice in your Veins or stacking movement boosts from Strike Fast.

HappyRaccoon: One of the more obvious eye rolls as far as rulings go. I’m honestly surprised people were trying to do this.

CYRAC: Oh no, my double Severe Kasrkin!

Kommandos

The Burna Boy’s deluge profile on the Burna picks up full seek instead of seek light, making it very useful on maps like Volkus with lots of heavy terrain.

Murderwing

The spikey boosty bozos are getting a second tap following their first round of nerfs at the end of March.
  • The Vox-Casters equipment range has been reduced from 3” down to 2” for stun tests. 
  • When the Warp Talon enters the battlefield via the Slice the Veil rule, you can't perform more than 2 APL worth of actions rather than being limited to only 2 actions, opening up the potential for a cheeky 0 AP boost action. More significantly though is that the Warp Talon no longer counts as obscured the turn it arrives, making it much more vulnerable.
Both of these changes are good changes that’ll make counterplay into Murderwing much easier for most players. The Warp Talon losing obscuring hurts pretty badly for MW here, you’ll need to be much more careful the turn he arrives or risk him getting turned into swiss cheese.

NotThatHenryC: Maybe now the Warp Talon isn’t a 100% auto-take. Not to worry, it’s still very strong and  Murderwing have many other options. 

Nemesis Claw 

Credit: Robert “TheChirurgeon” Jones

I think someone on the rules team just has a forever grudge against this team; they’ve gotten nerfs every balance dataslate. But hey! It's not all doom and gloom this time, as most of these nerfs are mostly clarifications and Nemesis Claw also received a buff! 
  • Rather than just preventing operatives from using Light Terrain as cover within 6” of an active Nemesis Claw operative, Preysight now gives you the Seek Light and Range 6” rule until the end of an action. More of a rewording than an actual nerf here, as the ploy still functions the same. 
  • You cannot use heavy terrain more than 1” below you for purposes of In Midnight Clad. Most people already played the rule this way, but its nice to see it made explicit.
  • Terrorchem now procs off of devastating wounds when using the Terrorchem grenade.
The nerfs here really don’t move the needle for Nemesis claw, it’s mostly just funny to see the team continue to get changes every dataslate. But the Terrorchem change is a big boon for the Fearmonger. With previous wording, you could potentially prevent an operative from being poisoned from the Terrorchem Vial if they blocked your crit, even though it did 0 damage. Now, landing a crit makes the poison unavoidable thanks to Devastating 2, making the Fearmonger’s Terrorchem Vial crazy good on any close quarters map or inside a Volkus stronghold.

NotThatHenryC: It’s been a bit painful seeing the nerfs rain down on my Nemesis Claw so it’s nice that these changes make the team better overall, for once. The first two points are really just clarifications, not nerfs. I have known people to block 0-damage crits from the terrorchem grenade rather than get poisoned and it’s nice that won’t happen any more.

CYRAC: Nemesis Claw continues to catch strays for no reason. I think In Midnight Clad is now probably the worst faction mechanic? Just so confused it keeps getting nerfed when it originally was just okay months ago.

Novitiates

Defenders of the Faith can work on critical damage again, instead of only being able to half an instance of normal damage. The changes to healing on the team probably still prevent them from returning to being nigh-unkillable like at the start of this edition, but reducing crit damage is still a huge buff to their overall durability.

Plague Marines

Both the Heavy Gunner and Fighter operatives can now take bolt pistols. This seems very minor, but considering how the Fighter had zero native shooting options and the Heavy Gunner only had its heavy weapon, having access to a pistol gives both of these operatives offensive options during their activation that were totally impossible before. A great buff to an already solid team.

CYRAC: A great quality of life improvement here for Plague Marines. Now more ways to engage and spread poison with your faction equipment.

Ratlings

The Spotter’s Spot action now works until the end of the turning point, rather than just once, bringing it in line with the Death Korp Spotter change. Additionally, the mobile profile of the Sniper Rifle and Suppressed Sniper Rifle on the team has been changed to a 3+ from a 4+, adding some mobility and flexibility to the team.

Scout Squad

Long suffering Scout Squad players see a few more buffs here to help these novice Space Marines out.
  • The Heavy Weapon Bipod equipment has been removed, and now just comes built in with the Heavy Gunner operative.
  • Replacing the Bipod equipment is the Tactical Vox Link, which lets you use either the Astartes Training or Emboldened Aspirant firefight ploy for free once per turning point as long your Sergeant is alive.
  • Speaking of the Sergeant, the Guidance and Experience ability no longer requires line of sight, letting you hand out an extra APL to any Scout operative on the Killzone.
Tactical Vox link is a pretty substantial boon for the team, as both the Astartes Training and Emboldened Aspirant ploys are excellent and you’ll want to make frequent use of them. The other two changes are nothing to sneeze at either, as Ceaseless on a Missile Launcher or Heavy Bolter is pretty solid and not needing visibility to your leader to hand out extra APL gives Scout Squad players great flexibility for making big plays. It’s tough to say if this is enough to push Scouts out of the doldrums, but its exciting nonetheless. 

Warpcoven

2+ saves are back for Rubricae, which is nice but not really enough to make up for having only five operatives.

Wolf Scouts

Wolf Scouts Kill Team. Credit: Rich Nutter

Finally, GW have come for the Wolf Scouts with a set of pretty significant nerfs. Whether it’s enough remains to be seen, but this is definitely a big hit.
  • Operatives can now only charge on Conceal if they end their move in the storm, which creates a tension between placing the storm where the Frosteye and Gunner need it and where you want to charge to. Unless you have the Skjald move it.
  • Frost Weapons, Runic Charms and Talismanic Trophies now only work on operatives wholly in the Storm. Additionally, the Pack Leader doesn’t get Lethal 4+ any more.
  • Speaking of Pack Leaders, they only get 13 Wounds now.
  • The Frosteye has caught another nerf as the Heavy profile of their gun is entirely gone now. Those Silent Guard shots will hit a bit less hard now.
  • Finally, the Fangbearer doesn’t prevent APL changes any more, though they do still stop other stats being changed.
These changes should significantly affect the dominance Wolf Scouts have had recently. They may not have shrunk the storm (so the stick that comes in the box is still valid!) but Wolf Scouts will be forced to stay in it more of the time.

CYRAC: GW continues to double down on a 12” bubble that can move twice per Turning Point being totally fine. The good thing is Wolf Scout players actually have to think about staging and where they put their models now. No longer the best kill team but Wolf Scouts remain very powerful. Really would have liked the Frost Eye to have to go on engage for guard, GEQ teams will still struggle into it.

Wrecka Crew

A change and a clarification here. The change is that you can only spend up to two Wrecka points at a time, meaning unsurvivable 6-hit shots should be a lot less common.

It’s clarified that Wreckas gain their +1APL immediately when they start a Charge action. This probably doesn’t change too much but it’s useful to be certain.

HappyRaccoon: Wreckas were stealthily making elites pretty weak, and these changes mean stray rokkit fire does not fully remove a 14-wound marine from usability. In fact I’m very pro this change and it should go a long way to widening out the pool of playable teams.

CYRAC: A nice tap here to make Wreckas solid. Melee accuracy is still great but, as HR said, they won’t be auto-deleting enemy operatives at range.

Stealth Suits

Received a couple of quite significant buffs. First, the Designator has a new action: Focused Markerlight. This lets it pick a visible enemy and light it up. Then once during the TP when an operative shoots at that target, they get +1 to hit for that action. This will make Stealths significantly more dangerous, especially if the target is in the enemy half of the board where Accurate doesn’t kick in. It’s just a shame the Marker Drone can’t also do this.

The Neutraliser also gets a nice buff, which is that they don’t need visibility to trigger their reactive Multispectrum Sensor Package rule. Big help against things like Wreckas bashing their way out of Strongholds, where they couldn’t be seen.

Finally Counter-network Jammers actually do something now. They’ve become a Strategic Gambit, where you pick an objective and reduce the total APL of enemies on it if one of your team contests. So Suits can steal objectives from elites and a buffed Drone can steal one from a 2APL enemy. This might have gone from never-to-auto-take, given the difference it makes to both the Crit Op and Retrieval.

HappyRaccoon: As the resident stealth suit player around here, these buffs are short, tasteful, and impactful. While little will truly help against the manacle’s-style effects the Neutraliser’s buff might budge that needle. Other changes will help when you send someone on a suicide mission and when you’re injured both things that happen often enough to be helpful. So I’m looking forward to playing these.

NotThatHenryC: Hey I play Stealths too! I’m very happy with this. I’ve been able to wrestle a few wins out of the team but the Neutraliser has always felt unnecessarily hard to make work and 4+ to hit has a horrible tendency to whiff a shot you need to not mess up. I want to play the team more now.

Winners

Credit: Dan "Swiftblade" Richardson

Fellgor Ravagers and Angels of Death win simply by not being addressed as Wolf Scouts take the hits for them. Changes to Seek and Destroy is also a huge win for the many elite teams that use it. The biggest winner as far as direct buffs is easily Battleclade, although it is hard to say how much this boost will translate to wins on the table top. They’re more suited than ever to take on Elite teams, but still seem to be vulnerable to shooting resistance melee teams like Goremonger and Fellgor. 

CYRAC: Fellgor Ravager and Chaos Cult not being addressed at all is absolutely criminal but sadly on-brand for GW when it comes to Kill Team. AoD may be a problem but I think it’ll be fine with them in the end, although they are likely the best Imperial Space Marine faction now. Salvagers were already good and got mega-buffed so are winners as well as Hierotek who are effectively back at full power. Mandrakes also dodged nerfs too so they are loving life as well.

Swiftblade: Angels of Death makes it out of this one like a bandit, but overall I think were going to see a shift away from elite teams in the metagame in the immediate future. We've been seeing a surge in the midrange teams on top tables for a while now, and following these changes I think the age of elites is finally over. I'd expect to see some really strong performances by Celestians and Goremongers, alongside teams that were already doing really well like Fellgor and Chaos Cults.

Losers

Wolf Scouts are suffering the most, but that hardly makes the team unplayable; it more brings them in line with their elite counterparts, while toning down some of their more obnoxious aspects. Wrecka Krew have been very popular as of late, and have stood as a strong counter to elite teams; the changes to their core ability might give those teams a bit of room to breathe, while making Wrecka players more nervous when up against people employing smoke grenades. 

CYRAC: Deathwatch are in pain right now, they have been under-performing for months now so it is strange they got nothing. Hernkyn Yaegirs who have been doing worse than Salvagers also got nothing, so I guess there is some internal bias between the vertically challenged teams? Murderwing caught strays too but are still very good, same with Wolf Scouts, but obviously both are going to be at least slightly less dominant than before.

Final Thoughts

HappyRaccoon: On the Just Another Kill Team stats show, we’ve been of the opinion that the meta game has been reasonably good for the last few months. Especially with the emergency nerfs to murderwing, and the now storm-centric gameplay of the Wolf Scouts. We are heading into one of the most stable chunks of rules in the recent past. While I’ve still got my eye on Fellgor and other skew teams, I’m excited to pick up my Kasrkin, Stealth Suits, and Battleclade!

CYRAC: From monitoring of the monthly and quarterly stats, Kill Team has not been in a good place. While this dataslate is generally good, there are a lot of confusing changes and lack of changes. Personally I feel it’s due to GW internally focusing on the new edition for next year but it’s becoming quite unpredictable with what changes are being implemented. I find it concerning that GW effectively gave up on trying to reign in Hierotek, and then teams like Yaegirs, Gellerpox, and other consistently under-performing factions getting nothing is very puzzling. Coupled with the lack of nerfs to Chaos Cult and Fellgor but then Nemsis Claw randomly getting nerfs, it’s all just quite confusing to me. I do think the meta will be in a better place now but internal balance between factions is also all over the place, although I think none of that will be addressed till Kill Team 2027 (next edition). Tournament play should be more stable, but there are now a lot of rock/paper/scissors match-ups out there.

TheArmorOfContempt: Everything here is good, but I have to repeat a common complaint I’ve had with past balance updates, and that’s it still doesn’t go far enough. Due to how minute some of the changes are you are given the impression the rules team really did look at everything, which in part is why this is so annoying. There are still lots of things in the game that could be addressed, many teams still have ploys and equipment options that are straight unusable, or are simply so vastly inferior to other options that players never use them. This is a problem that simply goes beyond the overall strength of a team. It is perfectly possible for a C tier team to have an equipment option that is an always take vs. an S tier team with a ploy that is simply never used. 

NotThatHenryC: I tend to like gradual change rather than huge swings so I’m pretty happy with this slate. As HappyRaccoon says, things aren’t all that bad at the moment. It’s true there are some things out there that don’t really work but I think most teams now have a decent amount of play. We’re about to start a new league in my area and I’ve got a difficult choice of which team to bring - which indicates a game in a reasonably good place.

Swiftblade: I think this an excellent Dataslate, and I think that the Kill Team community is in for an exciting couple of months. I think there are a few top dogs in the metagame coming out of this dataslate, like Angels of Death, Goremongers, and Celestians, but no team really stands just head and shoulders above the rest in terms of raw power. I think we’re going to see a wide variety of different teams winning at events, which means an even wider pool of teams that we’re likely to see at events. There are always some fixes here we could make to internal balance, sure, but taking just a few steps back and looking at the big picture here and Kill Team might just be one of the best balance spots it’s ever been in.
