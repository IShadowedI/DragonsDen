[SW Legion] Competitive Innovations in A Galaxy Far Far Away: Post April Update

May 14th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome to Competitive Innovations, our serialized feature for a number of game lines across the tabletop wargame space. Our army list and tactics experts canvass the top tournaments for their game of specialty, looking at the week’s top match-ups and how they played out on the tabletop to let you know what’s hot, what’s not, and what might be coming next as the competitive meta evolves year-round.

If you’re already deeply entrenched into the Star Wars Legion competitive scene you will no doubt have seen the discussion about the new sniper teams and heavy weapon changes for all four factions original special forces units but have the fears of triple auto wounding strike teams really come to fruition? Have support commanders like Thrawn and Yoda met their match? Have rebels completely overtaken everything with TaunHan and his double activating shenanigans?

Over the course of the past couple of weeks since the update we have seen a number of events including the Rocky Mountain Open GT, 2 LVOI events, and a smattering of local events all over the world. With all this said, let's get into the stats.

Thoughts

Both players had Recover the Research in their battle decks but it looks like Jason was able to squeeze in Retrieve the Data when they were building the mission. Neither list seems to particularly dislike this primary or this secondary. Dustin has multiple speedy frontline units who want to get into range 2 like the Sleeper Cell and the dynamic tauntaun duo but is still packing some backline holders like the Party Bus and the Sniper. Jason running triple Clone Commandos would mean he can put some durable bodies on the points while still allowing units that want to get up close and personal like Rex and Bad Batch to do what they do best, shoving handfuls of dice down the opposing armies throat. Snipers seem like prime units to pick off a model or two of the Clone Commandos or potentially popping off Katarn Armor in one hit if they don’t go for the automatic 1 damage from Sniper Team. If the Rebel player is able to crash into this clone list early enough without facing major attrition turn 1 and 2 they can easily clear out the DP-23 Clone Infantry squads with the sheer dice output of all these range 2-3 units they are packing in the list. A lack of impact on the Clone armies part would make dealing with the bus and the Land Speeder very difficult.

Thoughts

This list is running some really aggressive units that want to get in fast and hit their opponent hard. Luke and Han on Tauntaun are both speedy and hard hitting units with an array of command cards that can mess up your opponents plans or provide support to your army. Sleeper Cell are and have always been scary units for a reason, throwing 14 black dice surging to hit with the newly reduced Marksmen upgrade at range 1-2 and always generating at least one aim token with Tactical 1. The new Commandos have gone from a filler activation to a seriously deadly threat with the new Proton Charge Saboteur giving the unit Blast, Impact 6, and upgrading the dicepool to 5 red 1 black and 1 white with surge to hit if they get to range 1. The usual suspects are also here, Cassian leading a max Fleet squad, Din Djarin albeit without his jetpack but still rocking his flamethrower and Amban Rifle, a party bus to help deliver a unit or two, and finally the X-34 landspeeder bringing all of its usual guns. This list with good positioning can really hit hard and try to lessen the crackback, but can really suffer from attrition if those key units are dealt with.

Jason Baird (2nd Place)

Republic Clone Troopers, in the colours of the 501st Legion and 212th Attack Battalion. Credit: Rich Nutter

Thoughts

A clone list through and through; no space wizards allowed. Rex comes with a suite of decent command cards to help support his battle brothers while not being a slouch himself which is why he is so commonly seen. Bad Batch even post nerfs are still putting in work with their 2 red 5 black 5 white Pierce 1 (if hunter isn’t split firing) and a bucket of aims and surge tokens to support it all. The Fives shotgun Clone Infantry squad and all the clone infantry squads for that matter with their shotguns are a major threat at range 1-2 but not being max squads and medics being left out of the equation means they don’t have the same durability the usual clone squads we see on the tabletop usually have. Triple Commandos is a personal favorite of mine, laying down 4 red dice high velocity and having insane durability with shielded 1, recover 1, and Katarn armor giving you a get out of jail free card. This list seems like it wants to hit you hard but survive a bit better than the white save factions over a longer game.

Congrats to both players in the finals, you both have done incredibly well to make it through all 6 rounds.

Thoughts

As expected from the X and 1 Cast member who loves their mercs, and might be enjoying the newly buffed Maul and Pyke max squads. This list is moving fast and very reminiscent of the pre 2.6 “Mad Max” lists where you max busses and speeders so you can get in your opponents face quickly and hit hard. Unlike those old lists this list has no Black Sun at all, opting to go with max units of Pykes for a very versatile unit who is throwing buckets of dice whether it is at range 3 or in melee. Shockingly durable with that many wounds behind Danger Sense 2 and Independent Dodge 1 that can dodge crits. Chopping foes to bits with Maul or even utilizing Gar Saxon’s reasonable amounts of dice on his weapons and Compel to make sure you are always moving you can handle most missions that want you and your opponent to be fighting over mid board objectives.

Thoughts

Thrawn Tarkin lists seem to be on the rise, with the ability to be generating more aims than the emperor ever dreamed of and giving any unit of yours an extra out of activation attack to utilize them there is no question why this combo is very popular. Operative Vader being able to go toe to toe with most everything in the game and never shying away from having an order every turn makes him a melee threat that can wipe even clone squads off the map. Backed up by max squads of Shores and Snows and a single regular Stormtrooper squad, this list is putting major firepower down range, but it might suffer if an opponent can get close enough through all the dice, aims, and suppression.

Thoughts

CIS now needing to think about AI as well as losing the LOS blocking Aqua Droids has made multiple players look at the list of units and find that Crab Droids and Spider Droids pack a massive punch in close range. Patrick has forgone the meat bags (aside from a naked unit of Geonosians) and is relying on the supportive nature of Kalani and the T-series. This list definitely puts down massive firepower, cracking armor with the AAT and Snail tank while being able to utilize other vehicles to get in close and mop up what the guns can’t beat. This list is very susceptible to ion and impact and those characters are a sniper's dream come true.
