What We Know About 11th Edition 40k So Far: Recapping the New Rules Reveals Through May 9th

May 9th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome back to our weekly recap of the reveals of 11th Edition Warhammer 40k. Since last week's recap, Games Workshop has delivered a slew of faction-specific rules for the armies of the 41st Millennium, with the debut of 11th Edition's Faction Focus articles on Warhammer Community.

If you missed any of our coverage so far, you can find the older articles here:
  • Adepticon First Look
  • Reveals Through April 11 - Detachments, List Building, Force Dispositions, Cover, Objectives, Towering, Plunging Fire, Terrain
  • Reveals Through May 1 - Engagement Range, Charging, Reserves, Pile-ins and Consolidation, Fights First
At their current rate, we'll have a rough idea of what's arriving for each faction in the new edition by early June, which means these Focus articles should last until the release of Armageddon in just over a month. The goal with this recap series is to condense & extrapolate using the information that's been officially revealed, and boil it down to exactly what you need to know. For as much as things are staying the same, there's a bevy of rewrites for Detachments arriving in 11th, and that means both army construction, and some of the most popular ways to play, are likely to change drastically. With that prelude out of the way, what's new this week?

Note: We're dropping the "11 on 11th" naming this week as well; there are more than eleven things to cover.

Battle-shock Gets a Shocking Buff

Okay, okay, you can stop booing us.

Throughout 10th Edition, Battle-shock has been an anemic part of the game, being fairly easy to shake by way of high Leadership and special rules. In particular effects which cause a unit to Battle-shock on your turn have always had limited utility, as the effect just clears at the start of your opponent's turn. This was a substantial problem for Chaos Knights and Detachments like the Nightmare Hunt. 

Well, no longer! The horrors of the galaxy now persist when failed, and require a successful check to regroup. We only learned about this officially by way of the video version of Faction Focus: Space Marines, piecemeal to the article which leaves this unmentioned. It seems that units now have to take a Battle-shock test if they were already Battle-shocked, rather than having the condition clear on its own.

That's a massive boon for armies which can inflict effects like this across the board—Drukhari, Marines, Admech, Chaos Knights, and especially Tyranids seem significantly better at denying points with these alterations, and debuffs to Leadership can have cascading effects on a unit's ability to score. The actual effects of Battle-shock appear to be unchanged, so what we're used to (OC 0, no Stratagems, Desperate Escape) remain in 11th. Still, this is a huge liability for armies with LD 7+ or worse. It seems obvious that Enhancements or unit rules which can directly un-shock a frightened squad will be much more useful for those with a Force Disposition that requires sitting on objectives or performing actions, and I'd expect to see Chaplain and Dark Apostle stocks rise in the coming months.

Rangers. Credit: Rockfish

Stealth and Cover Don't Stack

Something we mused on when the new Cadian Recon Squad Datasheet released earlier this week was whether their Stealth ability would stack with the new benefit of cover, to effectively make them -2 to hit. Well we didn't have to wait long for an answer: In 11th edition Stealth merely provides the benefit of cover while out in the open, so long as every model in the unit possess the rule. In literal terms, this isn't much of a change at all, still keeping units harder to hit, but in practical terms this is likely a sizeable nerf. From the official layouts provided by Games Workshop, cover seems very easy to acquire, so you'd have to be especially out of position in order to need Stealth to provide it for you. Moreover, unless we see changes to how rules like Ignore Cover are phrased, that now also functionally ignores Stealth as well.

There's a triple threat of problems here, though, because no longer are most objectives out in the open, sitting in firing lanes. Infantry toeing onto these circles with some of their squad behind a wall loved having access to Stealth, because it provided an added layer of survivability to an easily-threatened position. In 11th, that's gone, with Terrain Areas being the new objectives...and providing cover to those inside, in basically all cases. To put it bluntly, Stealth has been given a similar treatment to Fights First in 11th, with a minor change in wording that will have massive impacts down the line, often leading to nerfs.

HEAVY Is Back

The Heavy ability/special rule is back with some tweaks. Now you can move up to 3" and still get +1 to your hit roll, but you have to be unengaged and you can't have been set up on the battlefield this turn. That means Heavy won't stack with weapons like Pistols to help you shoot better at targets in Engagement Range. Speaking of which...

PISTOL Is Now CLOSE-QUARTERS

Another housekeeping change, the new datasheets for the 11th edition boxed set show weapons like the Heavy bolt Pistol on the Captain now have the [CLOSE-QUARTERS] ability and have lost PISTOL, so we're pretty confident this is the new replacement, which has a name that is far less tied to the idea of a pistol.

The New MOBILE Ability

Revealed in Friday's Chaos update was the new MOBILE keyword, which Mounted Daemons units can be given via a Stratagem. Per WarCom, this rule allows you to move through dense terrain features like ruins and walls why you otherwise wouldn't be able to, making it likely to replace the host of rules that let you move through ruin walls as if they didn't exist.

Core Stratagems

The unboxing review on Friday showed a brief glimpse at the Core Stratagems in the rulebook, giving us a much clearer picture of what we'll have available and how those Stratagems will work in 11th edition. We received some additional information on these during the Space Marines Faction Focus as well, clearing up how Heroic Intervention will work. Here's what we know so far:
  • Command Re-roll is back, for 1 CP. It still has a list of things you can re-roll with it, and that list is the same - Advances, Charges, Damage, Hazard, To Hit, To Wound, and save rolls. If you re-roll a Charge, you re-roll both dice.
  • Explosives seems like the replacement for Grenades, adding a new keyword for the Stratagem to key off off. Seems unchanged otherwise, costing 1 CP and used in the Shooting phase to throw mortal wounds at an enemy unit within 8". It's still six D6 with mortals happening on a 4+.
  • Crushing Impact seems like the replacement for Tank Shock, allowing MONSTER units to get in on the fun.
  • Fire Overwatch returns, still at 1 CP and still not something you can do with TITANIC units, but with some big changes. The first is that Overwatch is now done at the end of your opponent's Movement phase, meaning you can shoot something which did not move, and your opponent can get out of sight without worrying about being shot. You also can't save Overwatch for the charge. The second change is that Overwatch shooting is now done with Snap Shooting, shots which only hit on an unmodified hit roll of 6 and for which To Hit rolls cannot be re-rolled. This makes non-Torrent Overwatch much, much worse.
  • Rapid Ingress returns and now has you make an Ingress move - the standard name for moves where you arrive from off the table now. You also can't use the Stratagem at all in the first Battle round.
  • Smoke Screen returns as well but with tweaks. Now it's used at the start of your opponent's Shooting phase, gives your unit the Benefit of Cover, and gives the Benefit of Cover to units behind your smoke unit if they block line of sight for an enemy unit. So your smoke guys can put down a wall of cover for other units, which is neat.
  • Heroic Intervention returns and gets a major facelift. You can now target any enemy unit within 12" using the Stratagem, and you roll 2D6 to charge. If you spent 1 CP on the Stratagem, you can move up to your dice roll and can only pick an enemy unit that charged - that's Leap to Defend mode. But if you spend 2 CP you can pick Into the Fray as your mode, and you max out your distance at 6" but can charge anything within that range, even if it didn't charge. This is a huge buff to melee units and really makes it hard to move block with weak units that otherwise just want to sit outside engagement range and stop units from moving. One additional note: Vehicles can now do an Intervention if they are CHARACTERS and not just WALKERS.

CLEAVE Gives Us Melee BLAST

There's a new keyword rule on some units, shown off in the unboxing video. The new Warboss' axe has the CLEAVE 1 rule, which gives him 1 additional attack for every five models in his target unit. This is something we've wanted to see for a long time, as it makes more sense than just adding a sweep mode to every weapon. This one feels like it was made for knights.

Ranges Change for Slippery Units

There's a small set of changes we've seen to both Lone Operative and example reactive move Stratagems, both of which make sense in the context of 11th's previous clarifications. Lone Operative has changed from not just being unable to be shot at outside of 12" to not being visible to those units, period. This comes in tow with an added line about being similarly illegal to target with Indirect Fire outside of 12", which is to be expected, but this carves out a number of rules which care about visibility (often those which inflict mortal wounds).

This can of course be mitigated by simply getting up close to a Lone Op, and it's a bit easier to do so now. Just like the change to Ingress Moves that we covered in last week's recap, reactive moves appear to check for 8" range now, not 9", letting models get slightly closer before opponents are able to shift away. In practical terms, this doesn't do much for charging, because you now measure base-to-base, but for those rare 18" Rapid Fire ranged weapons and multi-meltas, rejoice. This seems to be a sweeping change of most reactions, deployments, etc to check for 8" rather than 9", though the depth of those rewrites is yet to be seen. Getting used to measuring differently is going to be important, however, even if calculating the distance itself is easier.

Our First Look at New Detachments

There was much discussion had about what the new, splashable Detachments would look like in 11th Edition. Previous experimentation with things like mixing subfactions or formations was divisive and giving players that degree of freedom is always tightrope act for the designers. Make things too free and you end up with soup everywhere, all the time - we're looking at you, Callidus Assassin in every Imperium list. Make things too restrictive and players feel like they've lost key flavor - and now we're looking at the Daemon detachments in the Cult Marine Codexes.

That said, in their daily Faction Focus articles on Warhammer Community, we've seen a host of new Detachment options on top of the rewrites, and via a post on their socials there's been a critical bit of info revealed: All of them, so far, are 1 DP. Furthermore, it was said in passing that you could take three different 1 DP Detachments in a list, as 2,000 point games will afford you 3 Detachment Points to work with. They've also said that many of the older Detachments will be 2 DP, though some of the better ones may cost 3.

Some of these examples are things like Rollin' Deff, cordoned off to specifically buff Battlewagons & Rigs, as well as the units within them. They're hyperspecific, and across the board use a new form of 'batching', providing a new keyword to existing units in order to tie them together. Here, the Battlewagon, Kill Rig, & Hunta Rig all gain 'Wagon', and also clarify you can only take 1 'Wagons' Detachment, as to avoid overlap in the rules. This all seems fantastic, both as a way to provide the players with clear shorthand with which to reference appropriate units, and to prevent unintended synergies. A similar case is with Astra Militarum's Designation Force, which has the 'Recon' tag, almost certainly not being allowed alongside Recon Element. The function seems clear & concise, and the flavor gives players a chance to devote small portions of their army to specific roles. Just fantastic.

Digital Detachment Rewrites

Alongside new Detachments, Faction Focus articles are including 1-2 examples of what rewritten options look like, going into 11th. Detachments like Bridgehead Strike, More Dakka, & Taktikal Brigade all see tweaks to suit the new Edition's systems...but the selection has not gone unnoticed. At time of writing, all returning Detachments that have been shown to have changes are digital-only, with none from the factions' codices. What does this mean?

Emphasis was placed on 11th not being an 'Index Edition' like 10th, and that army books would carry over. It can be assumed with that promise front and center in the initial rules reveal, the designers have more leeway in changing digitally-released content rather than what exists in printed material. All that is to say we would not expect sweeping changes to codex Detachments unless we hear otherwise, with their balance likely being determined more in the form of different DP costs, rather than full-scale rules rewrites.

Detachment Utility Is King

It's worth highlighting a few of the more unique, niche Detachments that have been revealed this week to highlight the benefits of 11th's Detachment Point system. There were always skew lists in 10th, sure, but broadly speaking there's a reason you saw Gladius at every GT—generalist, high utility tools are rarely bad. Now though, with the ability to specialize a chunk of your force into one tiny role, and leave the rest to a broader skillset, we can see things like Taktikal Brigade take on a new identity. It provides your relevant units (Boyz, Kommandos, & Stormboyz) to Advance or Fall Back and still perform an action in the same turn.

While unconfirmed, this likely comes alongside a Force Disposition that rewards less of a lethality-oriented gameplan; you can play your Orks as a brainier, more subtle army and still chuck some Battlewagons in something like Rollin' Deff to crash into the midboard. Designation Force, meanwhile, synergizes with the Astra Militarum's propensity for long-range firepower, adding +3" to the Detection Range of certain units, and allowing you to pick them off from further away. This is an amazing thing to pair with, say, Siege Regiment or another 2DP Detachment which incentivizes artillery. The good news is that Games Workshop has confirmed "most" codex Detachments are 2DP, meaning most armies will pull on something you're familiar with, and one of these niche specialist 1DP options. Knowing what holes in your army you need to fill, and having the extremely specific tools to do so, seems to be the mechanical thrust of 11th's army design.

Orks Seem Ded Kunnin'

Now that we're through covering the broad changes, let's take a look at each army, in the order they've received their Faction Focus. The Ork Detachments revealed showed a new Detachment - Rolling Def' - which focuses on Wagon units, giving the WAGON tag to your Kill Rig/Hunta Rig/Battlewagon vehicles and gives them the ability to re-roll Charge rolls and auto-advance 6". The other two Detachments shown were replacements for the Taktikal Brigade, which now makes Storm Boyz, Boyz, and Kommandos units eligible to do an action after Advancing or Falling Back, and More Dakka!, which gives your Infantry units [ASSAULT], plus [SUSTAINED HITS 1] when your Waaagh! is active.

Something to note is that Rolling Def' is a WAGON Detachment, and specifies in its rules that you cannot take multiple WAGON Detachments. Keep that in mind as we see new Detachments which seem to have rules which would make for great "doubling up" with other, existing Detachments.

Marines Make Use of New Rule Changes

The Space Marines got a massive preview, with Detachment rules for not just the Adeptus Astartes Codex but also the four non-Codex Chapters (Blood Angels, Dark Angels, Black Templars, and Space Wolves). Nothing was shown for the Deathwatch, but maybe they'll show up with Imperial Agents.
  • The new Space Marines Detachment is the Fulguris Task Force, which focuses on your SPEEDER units. It lets all of them make an ingress move in your first movement phase. That seems bonkers, given Speeders tend to have Deep Strike. There's also a Enhancement to let a Speeder re-roll Damage rolls and shot counts, which will be insanely good on multi-meltas that now drop down within 9".
  • Getting replaced is the Librarius Conclave, with a new replacement that lets you pick a psychic discipline each battle round for your Librarians to have. There's also an enhancement to let a unit go back into reserves and Deep Strike.
  • Another new Detachment is Subversion Assets, which helps Scout Squads and Phobos marines, letting them increase the detection range on an enemy unit within 12". That is, you can use a Scout Squad to spot a hidden unit within 12", and then it will be visible from 18" away instead of 15". You can also play around with visibility for these units, making them harder to detect and see.
  • The Black Templars get Living Miracle, a Detachment which only buffs hte Emperor's Champion, making him an insane beast in melee.
  • The Blood Angels get Wrath of the Doomed, which focuses on the Death Company, and seems like a potential replacement for Rage-Cursed Onslaught? This is a DOOMED Detachment and presumably can't be paired with the other Death Company Detachments. It gives your Death Company units the ability to Advance and Charge provided they take some mortal wounds when they Advance.
  • The Dark Angels get Dark Age Arsenal, which buffs their plasma weapons with +1S. There are additional buffs for plasma weapons in the detachment.
  • The Space Wolves replace the Champions of Fenris Detachment, and the new one gives you an extra Heroic Intervention every phase on every Infantry Character unit. That seems neat, especially if you have Captains to reduce the cost of intervention. The other buffs here are all for Character units.
The big question slash Sword of Damocles hanging over all of this is "how much will Gladius cost?" If Gladius is 2 DP then well, we'll be playing that and one of these. If it's (hopefully) 3, then these likely fall by the wayside, outside of some odd combos that may become more attractive once we've seen all of the rules.

Ratling Kill Team. Credit: NotThatHenryC

Astra Militarum Gets Weird

Games Workshop showed off three Astra Militarum Detachments; one replacement and two all new. The Abhuman Auxiliaries Detachment buffs your Ogryn/Bullgryn and Ratling units, giving them the Abhuman tag and letting Commissar units issue the Take Aim! order to friendly Abhuman units. Bridgehead Strike returns as a Detachment that gives your Tempestus units BATTLELINE and +1 OC and +1 to hit the turn they're set up. There's an enhancement to give Tempestus Officer units a turn 1 ingress move. And finally the Designation Force is all about your scout units - Scout Sentinels and Infantry SMOKE units. Similar to the Scout Detachment for Space Marines, your scouts here can designate enemy units, giving them +3" to detection range.

Tyranids Are Flexible

The Tyranids get their own trio of Detachments as well. Ambush Predators is for Lictors, giving them Deep Strike and the ability to re-roll hit rolls of 1 against enemy CHARACTER units. The Enhancement here gives its bearer - which can include Von Ryan's Leapers - a reactive move of D3+3". Talons of the Norn Queen is a Detachment for your Norn Emissary/Assimilator units, and gives them the ability to once per battle make a new pick for its Singular Purpose ability. so they can have a do-over if you need it. And we get a replacement for the Warrior Bioform Onslaught Detachment, which makes your Warriors Battleline and gives them a 5+ invulnerable save, both of which are a pretty big deal. The Enhancement here can give a Tyranid Prime +1 AP and the ability to re-roll wound rolls in melee, which is also solid, and the Alien Physiology Stratagem can give incoming attacks on a Warriors unit -1 to wound if the attack's Strength is bigger than the unit's Toughness.

Credit: Robert "TheChirurgeon" Jones

The Chaos Mega-Preview

Similar to marines, Chaos had a five-part Preview, with coverage of the Chaos Space Marines and Chaos Daemons.

First up, a replacement for the terrible Cabal of Chaos Detachment, which saw very little use as an add-on Detachment introduced last year for the faction during a Dataslate update. The new version gives us the Empyric Wellspring rule, which gives non-Daemon Psyker units +1 to the Strength of their ranged attacks when they make a Dark Pact and in the Fight Phase gives non-Khorne Daemon Princes +2S and +1 AP when they perform a Dark Pact. There's also an enhancement to give a model the PSYKER keyword. Second up is Devotees of Destruction, which gives HEAVY to your Havocs and Obliterator units, which can be helpful for giving them a much-needed boost in accuracy once they've set up and gotten comfortable, though for my money this one's kind of a dud - I need those units moving around to get value most games. The Enhancement gives a Warpsmith the ability to re-roll Leadership rolls for Dark Pacts for his unit and the ability to heal wounds after destroying an enemy unit with Dark Pact attacks (more on this below). Finally there's the Murdertalon Raiders Detachment, which gives INFANTRLY FLY units re-rolls of 1 to hit against battle-shocked or below half-strength units, and -1 to be hit when those units attack them. This feels a lot like Nightmare Hunt, and likely can't be taken with that one. The Enhancement gives a Jump Lord's unit a 5+ Invulnerable save - very good on Raptors - and the Stratagem gives an charging unit with FLY the [LANCE] ability. Lance is really good on Warp Talons, especially with Twin-Linked on their claws. Wounding T9 targets on a 4+ with full re-rolls helps ensure you can kill bigger targets before popping back into the warp.

There three Daemons Detachments, and rather than focus on gods, they focus on unit types. Cavalcade of Chaos buffs your MOUNTED units, so Drones, Bloodcrushers, Seekers, and Fateskimmer can fall back and still shoot and charge. Plus there's a Stratagem here to give a unit Mobile for a phase. Lords of the Warp on the other hand is for your Daemon non-MONSTER CHARACTER units, giving them +1 Ld and OC, making them more effective leaders for your Battleline daemons and helping them regain models with the Shadow of Chaos rule. You can give them extra wounds with the Swollen with Power Enhancement or give them Fights First with the Carnival of Excess Stratagem, which is like a cheaper Counter-Attack. Finally the Warptide Detachment is alla bout your Battleline units, who gain ASSAULT and can Advance and Charge. The enhancement here is wild, giving a Battleline unit +1S, and Daemonic Infestation lets a unit heal 3 wounds.

That last bit suggests healing works differently to how it does in 10th edition, since healing 3 wounds on models who do not have 3 wounds otherwise seems pointless. Either way, ti's good to see Daemons get some love, as they were the only army to not get a Codex in 10th edition and we're hoping they get real love this time around. What we haven't seen yet is how Daemon allies were work, something we're eager to see addressed.

Speaking of Chaos, if you want to see a test game played with the rules revealed so far, Rob's already been playing with the known changes in his Road Through 2026! You can read his thoughts surrounding that very first test game here, with more to come.

Final Thoughts

We learned a ton about 11th edition this week, and as more factions come into view, a lot of the ideas behind the edition's design are coming into focus. There's a lot of cool stuff here, and we can't want to be back next week to recap it all again. So check back Saturday for another update of everything revealed during the week. There are a ton of sources to scour for these, from WarCom to videos to socials, and we're happy to put it in one place for you.
