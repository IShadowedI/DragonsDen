[AoS] Competitive Innovations in the Mortal Realms: 2026-05-13

May 13th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome to Competitive Innovations, our weekly feature for a number of game lines across the tabletop wargame space. Each week our army list and tactics experts canvass the top tournaments for their game of specialty, looking at the week’s top match-ups and how they played out on the tabletop to let you know what’s hot, what’s not, and what might be coming next as the competitive meta evolves year-round.

This week is a tour of Europe...well ok 2 countries in Europe. We got a Warhammer GT at Warhammer World, and then 2 smaller events in France. This week we'll be covering:
  • Age of Sigmar Grand Tournament - United Kingdom
  • GT solo AoS Clermont Ferrand - France
  • TOAST 3ème édition - France

Age of Sigmar Grand Tournament

62-player, 5-round Grand Tournament in England, GB on May 09 2026. All the lists for this event can be found in Best Coast Pairings.

Thoughts

The Lumineth list here wants to play defensive, which isn't always ideal for Sylvaneth. They can charge and leave but they mostly like to try and cover the board in Overgrowth and invite the opponent to charge them. Lumineth set up a lot early, and with Wardens, aren't really compelled to charge here. They will set up on the objective first and then just...stay there. The Sentinels can clear out anything that happens to be outside of Obscuring range, or Counter-Fire as needed. If they do see an opening to charge, Light of Eltharion is here.

Archetype

Vanari

Thoughts

This is a lot more straight forward than most lists. Vanari are the all-rounders of Lumineth. Not quite as mobile as Hurakan but they have tricks, especially with Lyrior to give them a headstart with a Strength Rune, which adds extra movement with the Wind Rune out of the gate. Not as resilient as Alarith, but the Anti-Charge with the Mountain rune on Wardens is pretty nice.

So the goal here is use the Sentinels to pepper opponents from afar while holding objectives with the sentinels. Delaying opponents and begging them to charge your Wardens. Should you need to force the issue and charge them, Light of Eltharion has got your back to slice and dice through most things in the game quite handily.

Thoughts

This is a surprising outcome, but a very close one so I have to imagine it was a hard fought fight. Since the Stormcast mostly lack a decent ranged option (ignoring the Longstrikes, which will be a bit of a problem for the two Megas, to be fair) the Gargants likely had to play long range ball for quite a bit before finally getting to charge in, but somehow they pulled ahead, likely by clogging up the board with enough units that the Stormcast couldn't hope to deal with all of them.

Archetype

Mancrushers

Thoughts

This is...well, very surprising! Don't think I've ever seen a list like this before, but the main thrust is to make the most use of the Manskittles Mob faction ability, which doubles the attack characteristic of ranged units for everything in this list, and there are a lot of Mancrushers here so that's a lot of thrown rocks!

Once the enemy is successfully softened up, send in the hordes to pick apart whatever is leftover.

Thoughts

As the Daughters of Khaine book continues to be put through its paces we're seeing some positive results. FEC have been one of the more consistent armies of 4th edition, especially since they got their tome. Even hitting the Terrorgheist list didn't really slow them down a lot. The infantry versions of the list have a ton of ways to tarpit units and rip them apart if there isn't a clever way to get around them.

DOK do have a lot of maneuverability, including teleports and movement shenanigans, that give them an edge on avoiding fighting Ushoran and his chaff directly. If the Shadow Queen can fight him face to face, without the Beastflayers getting involved, she can have a real edge in beating him and his Terrorgheist. The Blood Sisters also present a really strong strike last option against these larger threats, to counter Ushoran's own strike last.

The List

Archetype

Morathi

Thoughts

This is more or less how the book seems to be coalescing and its easy to see why, its pretty straightforward to play but has just enough tricks to keep the opponent guessing.

The Blood Sisters, alongside Shadow Queen, are the real hammers of the army. Hags function well both as a decent screen but can also clear out chaff to generate blood rites early. Khinerai are mobile objective grabbers, able to swoop toward an enemy unit and then leave, while the Hag Queen stays in the back on her throne, to give armor bonuses to everyone around her.
