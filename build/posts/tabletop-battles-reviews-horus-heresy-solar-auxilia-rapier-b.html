Tabletop Battles Reviews: Horus Heresy Solar Auxilia Rapier Batteries

May 2nd, 2026 via Goonhammer
Thank you to Games Workshop for providing a copy of this kit for review.

Two new kits drop for Horus Heresy today. Read about the Ogryns in Max’s article, but I’m here to talk about the two new Rapier kits. These trusty artillery units are gameplay staples across the Horus Heresy across a few different factions. The Legions Astartes kit came out last year, and has kitbashers’ mainstay: featuring its own unique armour pattern and a wide variety of arm options. This pair of new Solar Auxilia kit is similarly a banger.

The Rapiers were previously available in resin, but have now arrived in hard plastic. These two new kits include options for five different Solar Auxilia guns (all with solid gameplay applications). They are easily magnetisable with free-drilled holes for swapping out the guns, and the crew have much needed crew options for cross-pollination kitbashing with the existing Solar Auxilia Infantry.

If you play Solar, this is a good kit and you should buy it. Its worth mentioning it won’t do Imperialis Militia very well, but we’ll talk more about this later.

The Bits and Design

A Rapier is a self-propelled tracked artillery piece that can be taken as a Tactical Support Choice in the Solar Auxilia army list. Each gun has two crew: one on his own 25mm base (either standing up or kneeling down) and another built on a la-z-boy esque chair.

The first kit is the Fire Support Battery, which contains the two indirect fire weapons: the rapid-fire quad launcher and the subterranean mole-mortar. All well as the normal parts,

The second kit is the Direct Fire Battery, which contains three options: gravis heavy bolter battery, a gravis multi-laser array, or a laser destroyer.

There’s three sprues across the two kits:
  1. The base Rapier sprue, with the tracked gun chassis, the chair-sitting crewman, and the bits for the extra crewman.
  2. The Fire Support sprue, with its two guns (quad- and mole- mortars), as well as a special set of arm options with specific shells for either gun.
  3. The Direct Fire sprue, with its three guns (multi-laser, bolter and laser destroyer). There’s no weapon specific arms on this box, since there’s an extra gun.
Loading...


Each kit comes with 2 of the base Rapier sprue and 2 of the specific one, for 4 crew and 2 guns total. This mean you can split the box with a friend.

The body of the chair gunner is very Auxilia coded and quite difficult to remove. If you want to make Militia Rapiers, I’d recommend starting with the Legions Astartes box instead, as you’ll need 3 mortal crewmen per gun and not 2. De-Solar-ing the chair gunner would be a lot of work, and if you leave the chair off completely there’s no easy spot to put the controls for the gun.

The Build

This felt like a standard specialist games build, requiring roughly 2 hours light work per Rapier and its crew. Unlike Max’s reported woes with Ogyrn legs, the work felt commensurate with what I got out of it.

Each gunmount has a pre-drilled nub for a small magnet, so you can freely and easily swap out the guns between the different variations inside the same kit.  For Direct Fire I swapped out part 60 for my two mags, and for the Fire Support Kit it was between parts 41 and 46. You cannot, however, swap the guns out between the two kits, as they have different mounting points for direct and indirect fire. I didn't have the exact right sizes of madnet, so I used ones that were a touch too small with a bit of green-stuff as gap-filler.

One caveat: the quad-mortar points up like a normal gun, and the mole-mortar points down (it shoots rounds into the ground after all). This means there’s a little front bit of armour plating meant for the quad-mortar that you have to take out so the mole-mortar doesn’t have to shoot through its own carriage. I found I was easily able to dry-fit this armour plate without a magnet, but if you’re not a friction-fit Frank like me, you might have to investigate a different solution.

Special note goes to the tracks assembly. This is normally my least favourite bit of building a Warhammer tank. Normally these have to be carefully removed from the sprue one at a time and cleaned up one by one, before gluing on in precise order you have to get from an instruction booklet and remember what each part is numbered before you glue it in. This kit has specifically shaped and unique nubs for each track bit and track housing that line up like a peg and hole childs’ toy. This means I could batch out all of the tracks across all the guns at once, clean them up together, and glue them on using the nubs without looking at the instructions at all which I LOVED.

Loading...


The options on the crewmen are indeed delightful. The Solar Auxilia were missing a kneeling pose before, and the arm options include a series of binoculars, pointing fingers, iPads, empty hands, and papers, orders or maps, as well as various rifles slung over shoulders and under arms. These will really widen out the kitbashes available for the Auxilia, and add some more varied options for Officers, Command Squads and Artillery Section Command Squads specifically. It is a little short of head options, with just two for the kit, and no real options for pouches or other gubbinz, but last of heads is not a shortcoming of the range right now, whereas lack of flavourful arms is.

You can see Andrew's build here, with the back left off for sub-assembly painting, compared to the old-style resin rapiers.

New plastic Solar Auxilia Rapier (right) compared to resin Rapier (left). Credit: Andrew_N

The Paintjob

I painted my force in the scheme of the 17th Oeste-Albion Smogborn, part of the Dragoons companies of the Old 100 Regiment from the Hives of Wolvreham and Dudleigh.

I use a quasi-slapchop speedpaint method for my mortal army, beginning with a basecoat from brown spraycan from a hardware store. I then overbrushed the entire mini with a mix of Skavenblight Dinge and Adeptus Battlegrey, leaving brown in the deepest recesses, before drybrushing with Dawnstone Grey. I painted the red detail elements in Khorne Red, before a sponged highlight with Mephiston Red. I hit the leather parts with Black Templar, the metals with Leadbelcher and Balthasar Gold, and then the entire model with a homemade heavy dark wash. I then painted the lenses and screens with Warpstone Glow, with some messy highlights applied all over with the basecoat colours and a bit of gloss varnish where it made sense.

Nothing fancy! Pace, utility efficiency, as has been done with the hundreds of other 30k mortals I own.

Final Thoughts

Great kit. Five stars. Buy more.
