Tabletop Battles Reviews: Spectre Squad Kill Team Models

May 9th, 2026 via Goonhammer
With the release of Kill Team: Terror on Devlan, we have just a single new Kill Team: Spectre Squad. In this article we’ll take a look at the new kit, covering everything from its parts and assembly to how to paint it. It’s quite a flexible kit with lots of options, which the team have taken in some different directions.

We’d like to thank Games Workshop for providing us with a preview copy of Terror on Devlan for review purposes.

If you'd prefer a video review, check out this one from Can You Roll a Crit?

Assembly

Loading...


NotThatHenryC: This is a set of ten Guardspeople and a satellite dish across one big and one medium sprue. There are over 180 parts including multiple weapon and equipment options for each figure. The instructions take up 15 pages. This is a lot.

I found them perfectly ok; neither great or terrible. The pieces could have been a lot easier to find but there weren’t many mould lines. Each model doesn’t actually use a vast number of bits because you end up leaving close to half the pieces on the sprue. Everything fits together very nicely.

I decided to make all of my squad with the shorter carbines, which required one or two arm swaps. The kit isn’t designed to allow this, with arms intended for specific bodies, but I found I could do it with a moderate amount of cutting and no filling needed. I made sure I’d built all the models I didn’t want to swap anything with before using spare parts to do this kind of thing.

I also built all the models before sticking most of the heads on. There are a few that are specific to a particular model, but not many.

My radar dish thing arrived with broken legs, annoyingly. I bent it back into shape, stuck it to a skull to hide the damage and filled in the missing bit of leg with green stuff. So now it can claim to have a Termagant kill, somehow.

Cadian Spectre Squad WIP. Credit: SRM

SRM: The first thing you may think is "Wow, this is a lot of pieces for a squad of Guardsmen" at which point you would be absolutely correct. Usually landing at 10-14 pieces per model, this can be a difficult sprue to even parse, let alone begin to assemble. Models typically consist of left and right legs, two halves of a torso (with a flowing cape), and then 2 or 3 build options with different combinations of heads, weapons, and backpacks, all standing on a 28mm base. Those backpacks do largely only work on prescribed bodies and could be left off, but you'd have a weird strap on their shoulders where something should probably go. I had the notion of leaving the cloaks off for ease of painting, but as said cloaks are structural, often supporting the back of the neck and sometimes even making up part of the boots, it'll be hard to do much subassembling here. 

Building this kit was a pretty frictionless process, save for the odd fiddly half-arm bit here and there. The mold lines were maybe a smidge rougher than I'm used to in modern kits, but nothing too egregious. The arms all have these goopy, amorphous putty joins that I was skeptical of, but actually locked into place rather nicely. A few of those arms also had bits of cloak on them to attach to the models proper, all of which actually lined up extremely well. Gluing halves of cloaks is typically something I dread, often leading to large gaps or obvious seams, but due to the segmented panels of the cloaks as they're sculpted, it actually blends in perfectly. I found it to be a clever way to hide that join while still allowing for the three dimensionality of a multipart cloak.

If one wanted to further expand on this squad, the ball and socket neck joins are compatible with the rest of the Cadian range. That said, with 30 heads at my count, you're not exactly hurting for options, even over multiple squads. Some of those heads are only matched with certain poses in the highly prescriptive instructions, but you can absolutely swap heads to make these models your own. The sole exception are the hooded heads or those with face coverings, who will only work on certain bodies and poses. I still was able to use some of those on other models though, it just took some test fitting first.

CYRAC: Once figuring out the sprue locations, assembly was pretty good. Minimal mold lines and every miniature basically has two different Lasguns to pick from in addition to specialist weapons. Head-swapping was super easy too, found basically no difficulty in using Skitari Ranger heads on my models.

Conversion Potential

NotThatHenryC: I did a couple of head and arm swaps, as I’ve mentioned, but mostly built the kit out of the box. However, I found myself with a load of spare parts including a standing left leg from the Sharpshooter’s alternative build. For Kill Team I thought it might be useful to have a Trooper, so I set about using a load of spare bits (arms, backpack, chest, head and bits of cloak) to make a whole ‘nother little man.

Loading...


Since the model didn’t have the central part of its cloak it didn’t have the lowered bit of hood on. This meant I could sculpt this guy using one of the hooded heads and sculpt it to merge in with his camo cloak. I’m pretty happy with how this guy has come out.

The other thing is that you can magnetise the melta and plasma gun if you want. I did this using 2x1mm magnets, which hold the guns firmly in place. I took my time on this, letting the plastic glue where the hands are holding the guns set firmly, as I didn’t want them to set in the wrong shape.

Loading...


SRM: As I mentioned, there's a buttload of extra heads in this kit, most of which will work just fine on any Cadian bodies. There's a great variety of expressions and headwear on them, so whether you want a gruff, watch-cap wearing trooper or an eyepatch and moustache combo worthy of Old Snake, you've got it here. Unfortunately, the arms aren't really going to mix super easily with regular Cadian Shock Troops. The aforementioned putty joins won't quite fit without some shaving down, and the loose poncho sleeves won't really look right either. It's a shame since I really would love to give those tacticool lasguns a home, but with some carving I might find a spot for one or two in my tank crews. 

Painting

 

Loading...


CYRAC: I painted my Spectre Squad kill team as Cadian 122nd. I basically went with a shadow grey base and zenithal highlight by airbrush, then did urban tiger camo with Corvus Black and Pro Acryl Ivory. Once this was all done I did an all over thinned oil wash of Paynes Grey from Abteilung. When that was dry, I then did an edge highlight of the lighter shadow grey. Skin was painted in an experimental blend of Kislev Flesh and African Shadow and the metallics were all done with Vallejo’s new true metallics, which were all really great. For the basing I went with a grey earth, washed Nuln Oil, then with a light grey drybrush highlight. Finally I added some burnt grass tufts for a little colour variation.

Cadian Spectre. Credit: NotThatHenryC

NotThatHenryC: I decided to paint my Spectres in a camouflage scheme, dramatically adding to the time it takes to paint them. I find camo can sometimes look boring but I found a scheme used by the definitely real army in the documentary film Street Fighter. I’ve finished a handful of them and have loads more to do.

Spectre Squad. Credit: NotThatHenryC

The scheme has mostly been done in contrast. I started with Frostheart and used various different colours. For the camo itself I found it was better to use “proper” paint, as you don’t get very defined edges with contrast. A pretty strong wash of nuln oil over the camo tied it all together and dulled down the frostheart a bit, before I did a small amount of highlighting in raised areas.

WIP Spectre Squad Grenadier. Credit: SRM

SRM: I unfortunately did not have time to finish painting this squad in time for the review, but they do offer an opportunity to invert or otherwise change up your color scheme. The problem is that they do present a genuine problem to paint. While cloaks on Space Marines and Stormcast usually billow in the most heroic fashion possible, these camo cloaks are largely draped over their wearers, making the prospect of jamming a paintbrush behind them pretty challenging. The kneeling trio of troopers represent the worst of this. For that reason, I'd really recommend painting the cloaks early in your process, so you don't end up slathering paint on the boots or BDUs while angling your brush into the right nooks and crannies. They're also absolutely festooned with little doodads you'll need to think about. Everyone's got straps and pouches worthy of a Rob Liefeld character, and their backpacks all have different collections of ropes, knives, and hiking gear. I'm tied between the camping mug and grappling hook for my favorite backpack decor in the set. 

Cadian Spectre Squad. Credit: Andrew Corban

Andrew: I decided to keep my Spectres pretty basic as they’re still ultimately just 10 guardsmen who happen to be a little more cunning. I stick to the same base armor and fatigue scheme that the rest of my force wears and go for a simple black on the camo cloak. And like the rest of my army I gave them the gas mask heads from the Cadian upgrade kit to keep them tied in to the rest of their less sneaky and elite friends. I prime with Chaos Black then hit the armor plates with Eshin Grey and the fatigues with Sandstone from Two Thin Coats. The camo cloak I base with Corvus Black and the leather/gun casing in abaddon black. I give the fatigues a shade from Agrax earthshade and nuln oil for the camo recesses and their weapons/any metal bits. For highlights I edge the armor using Dawnstone and mechanicus grey for the camo cloaks and anything black, as well as Temple Stone/Karak Stone for raised parts of the fatigues. For the gas mask eyes and any lenses I do a spot of white scar then apply blood angels red contrast over it. I do a base of Martian Ironcrust then a drybrush of Temple Stone after it dries. The scheme paints pretty quickly on these guys and it avoids getting too bogged down in the details, but there’s certainly a lot of room to go hog wild on the camo if it suits you.
