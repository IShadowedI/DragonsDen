Tabletop Battles Historicals: French Resistance Women

May 7th, 2026 via Goonhammer
It's time to listen very carefully, as I will say this only once - the French Resistance Women are here, providing a very handy historicals kitbashing box, as well as a solid addition to partisan, resistance and home defence forces!

Thanks to Wargames Atlantic for sending this out for review.

It's been a while coming, but the Wargames Atlantic Partisans (2) French Resistance Women, which, as a bit of a mouthful, I'm going to abbreviate to Resistance Women, is finally out. Graduating from the Wargames Atlantic Digital range into full multi-part plastic, this is - I think - the first and only plastic set of 1940s themed women out there at the moment, and as such, fills a huge and yawning gap in the range.

This is very much a companion set to the Partisans (1) Box, which we reviewed here, and is another half-sprue setup, with five bodies, 13 heads and 10 arm sets per sprue, making for a chunky box of 30 bodies and - importantly - 78 female heads that will be widely useful above and beyond French Resistance models. While I think there's a good argument to be made for combining the two boxes into one French Resistance box, the kit as a whole provides you will everything you'll need for French-themed WW2 women and a massive chunk of excellent spares for other projects.

French Resistance Women, Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

While this is a companion set, it's also one made with several years of experience in plastics casting under the belt, so it's also a huge upgrade on the Resistance Men. Everything is at least a bit better. Sprue layout is denser, making for more kit on the same size of sprue, sprue gate placement is cleverer, making the very finely scaled M3 Grease Guns usable (because you can remove them from the sprue without breaking the stock), and arms now come with on-sprue labelling, making pairing up a breeze. This is all nice to see and it's a clear sign of how much Wargames Atlantic kits have come on in the last few years.

French Resistance Women, Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

Just as with the Resistance Men, the half sprue goes a long way. There's a variety of bodies in long coats, summer dresses and jackets, including one kneeling sculpt that works well despite the sculpting and casting issues that a kneeling skirt pose presents. Heads are largely a slightly romanticised version of the classic image of the French Resistance, mostly long luscious locks and berets, but with enough variety in style to present some shorter styles too. There's a selection of light weapons to choose from with a revolver, luger, M1911, British, German and American SMGs and a couple of rifles. There's even, because this is French themed and the opportunity is too good to pass up, one cigarette holding hand.

Simone Seguin in 1944, By Unknown author or not provided - U.S. National Archives and Records Administration, Public Domain, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=16065058

That variety and Frenchness of the kit is both a strength and a weakness. It makes it perfect for what it's supposed to be - French Women in the Resistance - but there's hints here of a slightly wider scope that leave it a little bit of a might have been kit. There's a Titovka/Side Cap head in here for Spanish Civil War or Yugoslavian Partisans (or to make Simone Segouin) and a Breton/Lenin cap that brings to mind Soviet Partisans, but no soviet weapons or bodies in slightly more central/eastern European clothing. That means these are nice additions, but require kitbashing to really work into more theatre appropriate clothing. Overall, the set zeroes in on some very particular images from the Second World War - see above - that keeps it to a very particular theme, of urban Resistance fighters in France in the late war. That doesn't mean that I'm not a fan of the set, or that it isn't more widely useful, and 20th century female plastic sculpts are hugely useful for a lot of projects - but it does mean that out of five bodies, two are in just-above-the-knee skirts, two are in knee length skirts and one in a mid-calf skirt, with no trousers, pilfered uniforms, boiler/jump suits or similar in sight. This makes for a very particular look at what women in the resistance wore that appears to be very, very influenced by a few famous pictures and quite a lot of movies, leaving (hopefully!) other styles of partisan clothing to other sets.

Scale Shot L-R - WA, Victrix German, WA, Bad Squiddo Metal, WA Male Partisans, WA Female Partisans Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

They scale perfectly well with most metal and plastic ranges, provided they're on the more realistic end of 28mm. As you'll see below, there's a substantial difference between these and heroic 28mm, but some components will still work, if you're looking for a lot of heads!

What Else Can You Do with the Set?

Having said that, there's a lot of other stuff you can do with this set, because 1930s-40s era women's civilian clothing is (as far as I'm aware) not available in any other plastic form. That mean's there's a ton of projects, kitbashes and conversions that can come out of this set. I've used parts from the set to kitbash a Yugoslav partisan in old surplus British uniform using the Wargames Atlantic BEF set, and mixed in components from the Wargames Atlantic Villagers set to make a pretty terrifying Dinner Lady for a small horror project I'm tinkering with.

French Resistance Women + BEF = Yugoslav partisan! Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

Unsurprisingly, the kit works well with the WA Female Survivors kit should you wish to mix in some more modern weaponry (perhaps to do 1950s covert agents in Europe?), or, as I've done, a double barrelled shotgun for a very angry villager for A Very British Civil War. Head connections work across both kits, and the scale of the arms works well enough that you wouldn't notice the difference.

An Angry Farmer, Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

You can keep this going if you like, and I've added in one zombified land girl for my weird war zombie force with bits from four different WA plastic kits. On this note it's worth saying that I'm really looking forward to Wargames Atlantic's forthcoming standardisation of scale and connections across their range - as a committed kitbasher, this opens up a ludicrous range of extremely easy conversions!

Possibly a Zombie WAC Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

Outside of the WA kits, kitbashing female models is often held up by the massive chunkiness of some male ranges. It'll be hard to kitbash in Warlord games components, for example. However, should you wish to up arm your Resistance Women, the Perry 8th Army kit scales very well and can provide more British rifles, SMGs and support weapons with very little effort.

Warlord Soviets (old) do not scale well. With anything, TBH, Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

Perry Arms scale perfectly. French Resistance Women, Wargames Atlantic. Credit: Lenoon

Why Get This Kit?

The direct comparator to this one is the Bad Squiddo range of metal Resistance fighters. You should absolutely pick them up, as they're great! I think of the two ranges as complements for each other. The Squiddo metals are particular individuals, who might be used as leaders for squads, Partisan heroes for games like 0200 hours, or for adding additional theming to a resistance force. This plastic set has the flexibility and convertabiltiy (is that a word?) of multi-part plastics, so fits for making everything else beyond specific historical personages. The smart advice is to pick up both, so that's what I'm going tell you to do!
