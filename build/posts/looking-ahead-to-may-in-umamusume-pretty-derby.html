Looking Ahead to May in Umamusume Pretty Derby

May 4th, 2026 via Goonhammer
May has arrived, the Kentucky Derby has been run, and Trackblazer has been with us for over a month now, which means it is time once again to visit the calendar and see what is coming up in Umamusume: Pretty Derby this month and talk about some of the upcoming banners, events, and specifically the reruns of the Champions Meetings and what that will mean for you! You may be surprised to learn that there are some changes coming to that format and some new things to know, as well as noting that just became the name is the same, doesn’t mean the race is going to be!

Golden Tempo (19) ridden by Jose L. Ortiz wins the 152nd running of the Kentucky Derby horse race at Churchill Downs, Saturday, May 2, 2026, in Louisville, Ky. (AP Photo/Jeff Roberson)

Before we get into that, did you watch the Kentucky Derby this last weekend? If not, you missed a pretty solid set of races with a few history making milestones in it. Japanese jockey Ryusei Sakai ran T O Elvis to victory in the Churchill Down Stakes, netting a G1 victory. Meanwhile in the Derby Itself, Golden Tempo ran an incredibly impressive race from the literal back of the pack to victory, netting the first ever Derby win for a female trainer in Cheri DeVaux, and Puerto Rican Jockey Jose Ortiz snagged his lifelong dream of wanting to win the Derby up against his own brother, Irad Ortiz Jr. on Renegade (one of the odds on favorites to boot). Congrats to T O Elvis and Golden Tempo!

Trainees for May

May only has 4 new trainees showing up this time around, and as with almost all cases, these trainees are going to mostly depend on if you like them or not in terms of whether you should pull for anyone. One of the staunchest tips I can provide for any month or any new banner in Umamusume is that you should only pull for a trainee if you can commit to pitying for them (30,000 carats), and that no trainees are currently limited; those won’t show up for quite a while yet and even still are very few in number in Japan. So if you feel like you can live without any of these trainees, you probably can, but if you feel like you absolutely must get one of them or they’re your favorite horse gal, then by all means make sure you can secure the bag!

Who drink Arnorl Palmer?

First up is Mejiro Palmer, who has the slightly unfortunate historical story of being a forgotten about and frankly uncared about horse from the Mejiro stable. Palmer’s wins were barely even noticed by the Mejiro company as they had the horse run in races that they either couldn’t run someone else in, or just did it to fill their card. Still, Palmer is a solid Medium to Long trainee, who during the current scenario suffers from the fact that Milers are needed in some capacity to be trained effectively. Still, you may want to consider Palmer if you’re a fan of the Escape style of running from Silence Suzuka, as Palmer is the second trainee to be able to gain the skill, and is also far better at utilizing it than Suzuka is. He unique skill actually restores her stamina as long as she’s been in the lead for the first half of the race, which can help immensely with the Stamina required to run Escape style. She isn’t exactly a meta pick but if you like the idea of an uncatchable front runner and the gamble of Escape style, Palmer is a solid choice.



The Wedding Event returns with Fine Motion and Curren Chan getting Wedding Alts, and like the first batch, these are mostly slight upgrades to the originals; Curren Chan’s Wedding form has a lot of great Debuffer skills and still succeeds very well at Sprints, while Fine Motion is a pretty good all-rounder that you could snag for Trackblazer era training and competition as she can easily run Miles, Mediums, and Longs with some sparks. She’s also geared towards Guts/Wit deck builds with her stat growths. Of the two of them, Fine Motion is pretty solid and will serve well if you need a Pace Chaser for Miles/Mediums and an overall reliable trainee, but also you can feel free to skip them as well as neither is going to set the world on fire.

Last of the Heisei Trio, Inari One, on the scene!

The last new trainee this month is Inari One, the last of the Heisei Trio of Oguri Cap, Super Creek, and herself. Inari One may not seem like an extremely stellar trainee at first glance since she lacks Speed growth, but take a second look at her aptitudes: Turf A, Dirt A, Mile B, Medium A, Long A, meaning that she can run any track and almost any length. She’s an ideal Trackblazer trainee but also just an extremely easy competitor to put into just about any situation that comes up, and she can easily be sparked into Pace Chaser or Late Surger if you don’t want to run her as End Closer (but that’s really her speciality). Her innate skill relies on being in the back, as she wants to be behind at the first half and then begin dueling with other trainees towards the end for a big speed burst. Of all of May’s trainees, Inari One is the one with the most versatility, and if you’re still lacking a reliable Dirt runner, consider grabbing her later this month.

New Support Cards

While there are some good trainees coming this month, the Support Card outlook is… pretty grim. There really are no good cards worth pulling for this month, which is great if you’re trying to save, but if you’re a newer player looking to build your portfolio of cards for your decks, this month is a real bust. The “best” banner is the one that arrives with Palmer that includes SSR Power Rice Shower and SSR Speed Matikanefukukitaru, but as we are moving into the “end” of Trackblazer’s lifespan, both of these cards are about to become functionally outdated and useless. Frankly even if you are a new player, neither of these cards are worth going for to help you ‘now’, and you’d be better off saving up, which isn’t a fun answer but is the correct one.

The wedding cards include Ikuno Dictus and Haru Urara, are both pretty bad. For an SSR, Haru’s card is especially terrible, having 2 skills and neither of them good, and her card’s support bonuses just are not good enough to make it worth it to get her. Dictus is just a pretty mediocre Power card, and as one of the more frustrating stats to raise and use cards from, that makes her pretty awful. The free one you can get, SSR Rice Shower, is worth mentioning because she’s a decent free SSR and events are a lot easier to clear now to get you an MLB from, so don’t skip out on getting her.

The final set, New Years Vega and Sakura Chiyono O, are just reruns and neither of them are that spectacular that you’d want to go out of your way in order to obtain. May is a pretty lean month for new, stellar content, and you will really be better off gathering up your Carats for upcoming cards like Speed Maruzensky and preparing for the new scenario than you are spending this month. Of course, there is slightly the boredom of saving all of your pulls and never using them in a game like this, but that’s the best strategy you can have for Umamusume; economy management in terms of your most valuable resource, Carats, is very very important to making the game successful in the long run.

Champions Meetings: The Return, Kind of?

May sees the return of Taurus and Gemini Cup, although both of them are returning in slightly different ways and in a new setting in terms of how Champions Meetings work. That may be the most important thing to note, so we’ll get that out of the way first. Since this is now the ‘reset’ of the Champions Meetings, the Open league will bump up eligible trainees to A+, instead of B, and Graded will remain the same as before (in that it is totally open, but you should expect trainees to be S or above from now on). Although Open now means that you could try submitting some of your previous A+ trainees, you’ll likely find that it is just as cutthroat as the original Open was. The second change that was announced is that races may not be exactly the same as they were the first time around, and we can see that in the second of this month’s upcoming Champions Meetings.

Remember Me, Eddie?

The first one is the rerun of the Taurus Cup, functionally unchanged from the first time it ran. We’ll talk about it more next week in a full updated guide for Taurus Cup, and now that A+ is included in Open, we’ll even talk about how you could try and compete in that too if you’d prefer, or at least what might cut it there at least. Like last year, Taurus Cup is a 2400m (Medium) race run from the Left during Spring in Sunny weather on Firm Conditions in Tokyo, and frankly much of the strategy from the first time around will do well to help you out with Taurus Cup the second time around. Of course, a lot has changed since then, but you should expect to see many of the same competitors and some new faces, along with some new skills and strategies in the mix, but don’t expect the world to get set on fire with something you’ve never seen.

Twinning means being totally different from your other version, right?

The second Champions Meeting of the month, Gemini Cup, is where things get interesting. Those of you who competed the first time around may remember Gemini Cup as a brutal stamina based slog that tested the Global community’s early trainees, but this time around, the new Gemini Cup is… a Mile race! That’s right, Gemini Cup’s rerun is actually a 1600m (Mile) race from the Left during Spring in Sunny weather on Firm Conditions in Tokyo, which means that ironically none of your previous Gemini trainees are likely even useful for Open league! Instead, you’ll likely be looking at having to train up a brand new set of trainees for this one, but since Trackblazer requires so many Mile ready trainees, you can probably make one fairly easily to carry you.

Looking Towards June

June is going to see some more slight downtime as the next scenario looms in the distance, likely appearing in July. That means that there’s a pretty good chance that you’re going to spend the next two months of Umamusume doing nothing but saving up your Carats, so if you’re the type who wants to pick up lots of new trainees and support cards, I sadly must preach temperance and that you will be rewarded with some great upcoming releases if you do so. Until then, we’ll be back next week with a look at Taurus Cup II, and if you have any questions, please leave us comments down below, drop by the Tabletop Battles Discord if you’re a Patron, or even email me at marcy@goonhammer.com! Until then, see you out on the track!
