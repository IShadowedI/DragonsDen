Necromunday: Adepticon 2026 Recap

May 4th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Another Adepticon has come and went, so let’s take a look at the various Necro-happenings now that the plastic dust has settled!

What Is Adepticon?

Adepticon started as a 100ish-person Warhammer tournament in Chicago back in 2003. This past year, there were 12,000+ attendees at the Baird Center in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, making it possibly the largest miniature-centric event in the US (not every event tracks general attendance). While plenty shy of the biggest gaming conventions, such as Gen Con with a whopping 70,000+ attendees, it is a huge gathering of and highlight for those painting, building, playing with, and buying models. I am one of those people.

There are heaps of hobbying classes and gaming events for all manner of systems in addition to a bustling vendor hall, but I am a Necromunda-centric person and so I’ll be moving on to that corner of the convention. We had five whole days of Necromunda happenings at Adepticon this year, so let’s get to recapping!

 

Table by @flansterous. Credit: Hive Market Minis

Panic at the Disco

Wednesday night featured the first of three sessions of narrative Necromunda from Joe Stromei and Nick Nguyan. This year’s “Panic at the Disco” served as a prequel to last year’s “Jailbreak” event, where the legendary Mike Jaghoff and Louie Jabronee had already landed themselves in Enforcer custody. I was fortunate enough to play in one of the 2025 “Jailbreak” games after spotting one of the 2024 events unfolding and thinking “I want some of whatever that is” to myself. So here we are!

This year’s scenario took place at a busting club in one of Necromunda’s hive cities. The board had two main floors with some stray platforms and rafters above the main areas, with thumping lights and classic club music selling the scene. Players were able to select from a handful of available factions or bring their own crew. I brought a Jokaero and his entourage, with a cybernetic raven standing in for a Techmite Oculi, a Squat with an Ironhead Autogun, and an insanity-prone Iron Automata. My crew was assigned the task of sneaking into the club, tracking down some chem runners, and taking their illicit goods for themselves.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

My plan of sneaking in through the back and scouting out the club was instantly thwarted by a player who had selected a Cawdor crew immediately using their vehicle to plow into the hive civilians waiting to get into the club and turning their templates on anyone within reach. My Iron Automata unloaded into the Redemptionists and pinned some of the models, buying the fleshier elements of my crew some time to make a break for the side entrance.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Numerous groups clashed in the club entrance, as the Cawdor group came into contact with some Escher chemists as well as the “house” gang running the club. My cyber bird was able unlock the side entrance with a strong Intelligence stat of 5+ and make it into the club’s office undetected. The rest of the gang was less lucky, as a Palanite patrol quickly rolled in on account of the escalating ultraviolence. My Squat and Automata traded shots for two rounds, but both were ultimately apprehended outside of the club.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Inside, the Redemptionists did what they do best and kept at setting any and everything ablaze. This made my objective much more difficult, as all of the civilians began to scatter before I had a chance to hone in on who exactly my targets were. In the end, my cyber raven was able to hack a computer and crack a safe while the Jokaero was able to make a deal with the house group to fill the recently vacated position of lead chemist. The Enforcers took charge of the scene outside, while the Redemptionists torched damn near everyone in the club. A fun, if blaze heavy, time for all!

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Community Day

Thursday was Community Day in the Necromunda room, hosted by the wonderful John Shaffer of @flansterous fame. John had numerous classic Ash Wastes, Zone Mortalis, and Sector Mechanicus boards set up for open Necromunda gaming, partnering with my pals Jay and Dale of JDin the Sump Sea who brought along the legendary Sump Wall board in addition to a few other sump-based offerings. It was a pleasure seeing folks drop in to appreciate the boards, get some casual games in, and chat with John, Dale, and Jay about all things event and Necromunda.

Scumathon Kickoff. Credit: Hive Market Minis

I myself hung around the space all day in preparation and anticipation for my own Scumathon Kickoff event, which you can read more about over here. It was a joy getting to offer a space for folks to enjoy and even discover kitbashing for the first time, and I’m looking forward to building on what I learned from this trial run of the event to give it another go in 2027 with more confidence. 

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Joe and Nick also ran “Panic” again as the night settled in, with different crews and a vastly different outcome! Seeing the story unfold through player choice makes dropping in to observe some narrative Necromunda a fun time.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Patron’s Claim

Friday and Saturday were filled with John’s “Patron’s Claim” events. These are wall-to-wall Necromunda days, with numerous objectives and goals for each player to work towards across numerous games. John has been running the major ‘munda event at Adepticon for years, and his experience with it shows in the depth of the single-day campaigns. The aforementioned Jay and Dale of JD in the Sump Sea were also around this year supporting John and ran some games on their Sump boards throughout the weekend.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Players joined up with one of three patrons, which determined their specific sub plot objectives and which specific assets they were after. My Ogryn-led Venators sided with Lady Credo, as they have some shared history (we've got unfinished business, Lady Haera!) and I'm all for a good rebellion. On top of this, players were also tasked with taking down various NPC Roaming Horrors throughout their games, from the meager Sump Spider all the way up to a Malstrain Patriarch or Sump Horror. Fowler’s Sump Whale even made a cameo appearance!

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Lastly, John's Gladiatorial Arena made a spectacular comeback, which made for some of my favorite memories during last year's event. Foraging for random high-powered equipment is always fun, but the shifting battlefield with spurting plumes of smoke keeps players on their toes and without reliable cover as they fight to stay alive.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

My first game on Saturday was the “Sump Wall Loot Run” against a band of high-powered Goliaths. My Iron Automata successfully tangled in melee with a few spiders before failing his insanity roll and taking potshots at my own crew. The rest of the gang spent the game navigating the board in search of loot while avoiding bolter fire.

My favorite element of the board were the six access tunnels that you could duck, getting spit out at a random one on a d6 roll at the end of the turn. This kept things lively and made for multiple fun intercepts and failed getaways. I ultimately managed to outlast my opponent as his gangers took the occasional tumble from up high into the sump. A fun vertical game on an excellent board!

Credit: Hive Market Minis

I played a few other rounds that day, culminating in a six-player spider hunt including community heroes @oscarlars, @wellywoodwargaming, and @tempestterrain. Dale, our GM and Arbitrator for this scenario, was clear this was a team effort to survive and escape. On the second activation of the game, Damon Wellywood decided he was having none of it and promptly started blasting into the amassed crowd of rival gangers. That's Necromunda, baby! A frantic scramble around the spiders (and each other) ensued, and a memorable game as had by all.

I made sure to play through more scenarios on Sunday, culminating in a four-player land train heist against none other than Kal Jerico himself run by Joe and Jay. This was another tenuous alliance situation, with four players vying for control of three cargo trailers while under fire from a hoard of NPCs. My Squat ganger survived a mighty leap from an elevated walkway onto one of the trailers, while the Automata immediately went insane and ran out of scarce ammo. Kal was ultimately brought down, which is the moral victory we can always hope for.

Credit: HIve Market Minis

The variety of scenarios and objectives kept each game fun and fresh, and everyone I matched up against was a pleasure to roll dice with. I'm no longer surprised by the positive and fun-focused spirit of our community, but it is something I'm constantly grateful for.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Each day also had a gang showcase, which is a treat for anyone who loves the painting and modeling aspects of the hobby. This year had a strong showing of beautiful and inventive crews all around, and I'm always glad to see models I've ogled from online in person. Speaking of models, we should probably touch on Golden Demon.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

Golden Demon

GW’s premier painting competition has its US leg at Adepticon, and it is a treat. It is also, at times, intense and divisive, which plenty of other folks have already said plenty about. For my part, I'm happy to see the Necromunda cabinet grow in both quality and volume of entries each year, with some fantastic painting on display.

My entry. Credit: Hive Market Minis

I myself entered this year with a group of my Ironfist Marauders aiming for my second “notable” pin after snagging one with my Lobo Gargant last year. I did come up short in that quest, but remain pleased to have been able to show my best work to date and proud of the models I submitted.

At the novice level I hover in, the challenge to put together my best work each year is a great motivator to continue to stretch and grow my skills, and seeing so many gorgeous models leaves me full of inspiration to use towards next year's entry. Wherever you are in your own painting journey, it's a great opportunity to put forward something showcasing the best you're currently capable of with intentionality and that extra level of attention to detail a formal competition elicits.

The Necromunda category awarded a number of deserving pieces with pins, though I felt there were a few more that lived up to that same quality. You can find plenty of coverage of the excellent trophy winners online, and so I'm sticking to sharing a few of my personal favorites.

Credit: Particulate_X







Should You Go Next Year?

Well, yes! I've already typed about the more tangible elements, but the absolute best part every year is the community. Whether you're getting to hang out with folks you've only previously yapped with online, rolling dice with old buddies, or making new friends on the fly, getting to socialize about the games and hobbies we love most around folks who really get it is simply amazing. I have a bit of social anxiety myself, but that's easy to breeze past when you're chatting hobby over dice, drinks, or dinner.

A number of my pals made some great recap videos that touch on this, including @wellywoodwargaming, @iPaintSmallThings, and one from both Dale and Jay of @JDinthesumpsea that are all worth a watch.

Credit: Hive Market Minis

There's certainly a summer camp vibe to events like this in numerous ways. I did have a tricky time the first day of my first Adepticon, struggling to solidify some of those online connections with folks eager to catch up with more established friends. It was a bit wild feeling the first-day-at-a-new-school thing in the pit of my stomach as a grown adult! I stuck it out, and connected with folks I’ve built meaningful relationships with in the years since. Now, attending the con feels like seeing my hobby camp buddies for a few days to make some more fun memories while welcoming new folks into the fold. All while getting to play Necromunda!
