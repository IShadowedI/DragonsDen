Tabletop Battles Reviews: Infinity N5 Operation Mazebreaker OSS Rules and Units

May 12th, 2026 via Goonhammer


Aleph is Mother, Big Sister and maybe even a little bit of HAL 9000 rolled into one. We built Aleph, she’s smarter than we are. She sees all and she knows what’s best for us. Why reject her wisdom where she has already done the calculations and optimised our shared path forwards? Those of us who close their minds to Aleph’s wisdom are mistaken, children who should be loved for their fierce independence. Corrected, of course, but loved. Always.

Where Steel Phalanx is Aleph’s highly marketable Hammer, blunt and yet bombastic, the Operations Subsection is a vastly more subtle tool in Aleph’s arsenal. Sometimes it’s diplomacy and sometimes it’s covert operations. Either way, the Operations Subsection is ready to do whatever Aleph needs done without harming the reputation of our great and noble protector. The last time OSS got featured in a Two-Player Operations box was Operation Coldfront back in 2018 or so. Funnily enough, the contents of that box very closely match what you’re getting here (trade a Deva for an Asura, a Yadu for a Dog-Robot, change some weapons on your Naga and Shukra and it’s a match). So, here we are, Aleph is back to capture our hearts and minds with Operation: Mazebreaker.

New (and Updated) OSS Units

Dikpala Tacbots

If it looks like a Dakini, has the same stats as a Dakini, and has the same costs as a Dakini Tacbot…it’s probably a Dakini Tacbot. Aside from the removal of the Grenade Launcher profile, which is replaced by an SMG-Repeater (Oh, wow!) and a Specialist Comm-link profile, there is fundamentally no difference between Dikpala and Dakini Tacbots. This is fine, Dikpala/Dakini were always a solid profile. On the more expensive side for a Line Trooper type piece but you get Mimetism -3, Move 6-4, as well as some niche abilities such as Remote Presence, Courage and ECM: Hacker (-3) for the price of admission. Let’s talk about that Repeater profile though, for just 13 points you get to drag a Repeater around behind whatever active piece you’ve got leading an Operations Fireteam. When you consider the sort of Hacker firepower OSS is bringing to the table, that’s a pretty amazing capability to just have following a Fireteam around the table.

Sharvara HoundBots



Musterkrux: Normally, when we see a canine-themed model here on Tabletop Battles we would be inclined towards replacing genuine consideration of their visual design and rules with inane commentary about Goodest Boi’s, Heckin’ Woofers and whatnot…and today will be no different.

HoundBots are incredibly mobile pieces, with Move 6-6, Superjump and then dodging +2” on a 15 or less. Players trying to defend against these models are going to have a challenging time trying to predict their angles of attack and ensure that they’re protecting their keypieces against a surgical strike. The ability to fork someone between dodging your Riotstopper or E/Marat template or putting the Hound down before it jumps into melee is going to be a sublime experience for OSS players. That’s not even accounting for the shenanigans you can get up between Super Jumping 8” and then using a 75% chance to Dodge 4” in the Resolution stage (which your opponent cannot declare ARO against if that Dodge would otherwise trigger LOF or ZOC awareness). That said, the HoundBot isn’t actually a CC Monster (but it’s solid enough for the price) and doesn’t have Stealth.

Genghis: this is maybe the standout unit of the box for me. Having such a defensive unit at 15-17pts is a big deal for the faction. Nothing in the game can risk walking into a Heavy Riotstopper or an E/Marat. They can contribute to Fireteams when serving that guard dog, picket duty role, and when they pivot to assault, they're moving 12-14” an Order. These things are highly optimised to punch up. I've played a practice game against two of them, and they were a real pain to deal with. 

Nagas



The core Naga chassis/profile hasn’t changed (same stats, same special rules) but there are a few notable adjustments here and there for Nagas (who haven’t been seeing a lot of table time recently). First of all, we’ve got a +1SD MULTI Sniper Rifle profile for 29/1.5 (just 2 points more than the old Sniper profile). That’s very OK. I’m not sure that it’s enough to make the Naga Sniper a reliable ARO piece (not for 29 points) but you’re going to have games where you need to roll those dice, as well as leverage Dogged to keep your opponent’s premiere attack piece from reaching your DZ and tearing your army apart in Round 1. On the offence, that +1SD is going to help the Naga more reliably pull off those alpha strikes you need to clear the ramparts. The old Monofilament Mine profile has traded in their Combi-Rifle for an AP-SMG as well as the ability to Minelayer, which can stop your opponent from going ham with a TAG or other premium piece without testing the waters first (read: tossing a WarCor around the corner to trigger the mine).

Dasyus



The more elite of the vintage Aleph skirmishers received a substantial set of changes. They lose NWI - a big deal - but gain mobility, Infiltration (Reroll) and the always-handy BS Attack (+1SD). These changes make them slightly more of a finesse piece. Being able to reliably tank a single chain rifle (or similar cheap template) hit is a major strength for any midfield unit, and Dasyus now look fragile for their cost. But the boost to their shooting capability is massive, particularly for that sniper. The other standout profile is a Minelayer (2) with Viral Mines - a very scary piece of defensive tech, provided your opponent isn't heavy on STR units. 

Genghis: I’m not sure how widely Infiltration (ReRoll) will be used on these guys. 9s, re-rolling, is just under 70% success. Tempting but not exactly reliable. It's not the aggressive Turn 1 fork that the Bandit Minelayer Deployable Repeater is, because mines cannot be deployed using Minelayer with enemy models in their Trigger Area, a restriction which doesn't apply to Repeaters. I think the +1SD sniper is the big news here. I've played one game against it, and it wasn't as hard to deal with as a Swiss Guard Missile, but then again it costs far less. 

Kinnara ScoutBots



The Kinnara is an interesting piece, at first glance you might think: ‘If it has Martial Arts L2 like a Dawon…’ but it’s more than just a Kung-Fu-Bot. The Infiltrator looks like an excellent mid-field skirmisher, with Sensor to reveal enemy camo markers as well as a variety of weapons to fork AROs with the Kinnara is going to dominate the mid-field and give you the breathing room to bring up your specialists or other active pieces up. With Stealth it’s also going to be very good at hunting down the Hackers or Repeaters that would otherwise prevent your key hackable pieces from advancing up the table. The Parachutists, aside from being Specialists (TBF, the Infiltrator is, as well) also bring Eclipse Grenades with them, which will give you a huge amount of vision control. This is pretty important when you consider that all it takes for the Kinnara to walk up to your TAG, obvious Lt or other critical piece and Monofilament them to death in close combat is a clean run without any inconvenient AROs.

Genghis: Like the Dasyus, these strike me as finesse pieces given the medium-high cost, 1STR and assault role. But Decoy(2) and Eclipse Grenades, at least on the Parachutists, give them some tools to penetrate enemy defenses and reach the important enemies they want to assassinate.

Asura



The Asura profiles of old get a very welcome tune up. The Spitfire is now AP (lovely, now your 60+ point shooter can break armour on HI and TAGs in Cover, making it a viable choice now) and the Hacking Device Plus profile swaps PS=4 on Trinity for +1SD. That’s amazing. Abso-lutely-freakin’ Amazing. At this stage, we’re getting pretty close to the point of saying that the Asura is now genuinely competitive with pieces such as the Anathematic for the high water mark of Hacking in Infinity. Musterkrux: As ever, please remember that while your Asura Hacker may have access to White Noise via their Hacking Device Plus, they also have an MSV-3. We beg of you, write in and tell us if you ever accidentally White Noise yourself into getting ARO’ed by accident.

Genghis: I think these changes elevate the Asura from a unique, enjoyable unit to a true bruiser. I expect to face it a lot in the future. 

Who Is Johnny Kao?

Musterkrux: The updated OSS sectorial list includes several named characters that we have limited information on thus far: Dr Claire Lazhari, Kyra Sharma and someone who I can only assume is an absolute Gigachad just from their name: Johnny Kao. The only thing I know about Johnny Kao is that I want to know more about Johnny Kao. I just can’t keep thinking about or saying Johnny Kao. However, that’s a ‘Me Problem’, I’m sure our well-adjusted readership can avoid the brain-rot memetic virus that is Johnny Kao. We have always enjoyed CB’s take on named characters in the setting, there’s enough of them to add flavour to their respective factions without crowding out the ‘generic’ profiles. They also give faction’s the ability to pipe in very specific capabilities with strictly controlled costs and abilities.

Keen to see what CB can do with all of these new named characters!

Other New Units

A few Thorakitai have migrated over from Steel Phalanx for some reason (they are, of course, not unwelcome here though). Maximus has decided to grade OSS with both his TAG-mode and Dude-mode forms (where before we only had access to TAG-Max).We also have the Pilot-X Team, unsure what they do but considering that both Next Wave and CJC have recently introduced some interesting mechanics around ancillary Remotes (ie. Cyberplugged Sartroids and Bamdadroids) we’re willing to put our money where our mouth is and bet that Pilot-X Team will involve both Remotes and being Bad Enough Dudes to Rescue the President (I mean, with a name as radical as Pilot-X Team how could they not?) 

Who Was Removed from the OSS Army List?

The sectorial has lost a few named characters: Trisha (from Defiance), Andromeda (from Steel Phalanx), Parvati (from Aristeia) and Armand Le Muet (from whatever artichoke hell the Tohaa come from). Getting ahead of the ITS Season adjustments, we don’t see Sacha or Kazuraba Ruby Monday featured in the new Army List but that’s a reasonable and expected change.

Musterkrux: Do you know who wasn’t removed from OSS?

Netrods and …Post-Humans.

CB had their chance to remove Post-Humans from the game with this update and they didn’t. And so, I’ve decided to make that your problem now. [Laughs in Binary] 

Genghis: Post-Humans have been an auto-take in OSS ever since their introduction. It's fascinating to think, with all this new stuff, that they might actually sit out a game or two. 

Personally, I will wait out to see which of the units missing from this roster have actually been removed. Ruby Monday, in particular, we expected to be usable in OSS, given her inclusion in the box!

OSS Fireteam Structure

This art slaps (Corvus Belli)

There have been some significant changes to the way OSS builds Fireteams with the arrival of Operation: Mazebreaker. Just about any Fireteam you could build before remains viable but now we have so many more options to experiment with. Exciting times!

Operations Fireteams

These remain broadly similar to their previous iteration. However, we get to add Sharvara HoundBots (counts as Dikpala), as well as Artalis (who can bring an Engineer profile if you’re keen for baked-in force reconstitution). Dikpala Duos involving a HMG or Sniper paired up with either a Repeater or Paramedic (cheap specialist) feel like our default choice but there’s a variety of options to explore here. Happy days.

Genghis: The addition of Artalis is cute - it's an engineer on the spot with your Dikpala, although I'm not certain it's necessarily better than the other excellent engineers in-faction. Houndbots are also a great piece of security. Large Dakini teams were always a staple of OSS for their repairability and budget firepower. Dikpala teams look substantially better. 

Asura Fireteams

The old Yadu Fireteam has now been commandeered by the Asura and renamed in her honour. If you partner up an Asura AP-spitfire with a Yadu Forward Observer, you’re getting +1SD on the shooter and dragging a Specialist around but that’s also an incredibly expensive module consisting of two models. Deva and Shakti remain optional inclusions but I’m not sure the juice is worth the squeeze for either because Shakti is very expensive and duplicative of the Asura’s capabilities while the Deva doesn’t ‘Count-As’, so wouldn’t be giving you fireteam bonuses. Claire Lazhari is also a possible member now but until we know more about them there’s not much we can say about that.

Samarthan Fireteams

The Samarthan Fireteam is a bit of a catch-all Fireteam. There are a lot of options to unpack in this list but at least the foundations of Samarthan teams remains relatively similar: Riksha, Dawon and the option to slide in an Artalis. On top of that, you can now build around Yadu as your ‘required’ choice. Additional options include: Sharvara HoundBots, Kyra Sharma, Shakti, Claire Lezhari, Deva, Satrah and Apsaras. Seems fine, even if building a fireteam with decent spread of capabilities won’t be cheap.

Bounty Hunters Fireteams

Duo or Haris Fireteams that start with either an Authorised Bounty Hunter (Regular and Recommended) or a Motorised Bounty Hunter (Irregular and …Irrecommended [Ed: Unrecommended, you illiterate oaf!]?). Fundamentally unchanged from before and there’s always been something special about spending 12 points to give Miranda Ashcroft (love that E/Mitter) or Hawkwood an extra +1SD on their BS attacks. Noting, of course, that this turns Hawkwood in particular into a veritable Special Dice Machine. Musterkrux: I have a personal favorite module of taking a pair of Duos: Miranda and an ABH, as well as Hawkwood and an ABH and running an incredibly Duo-heavy OSS list. I don’t see this changing too much in this new iteration of the sectorial.

Genghis: I have almost always played the Miranda/ABH Duo in Tunguska and Imperial Service. It's equally good here, and there are a lot of other combos. Miranda or Hawkwood are fearsome hard AROs, and the threat of a Dasyu sniper hidden behind them doesn't make them look any softer. 

Max-Op Fireteam

Pick a TAG (Max or Marut), add a friend. Simple. It has the option of running as a Haris but I’m not sure there’s a strong argument for doing so. If you’re really keen, Maximus and either Claire Lazhari or Johnny Kao (that name again!) will get you a pure Duo but, again, until we see their stats and point costs it’s a bit of a guessing game as to how effective that would be.

Genghis: This is the team we know least about, but the one I think might be the biggest new thing overall. 

Wildcards

Compared to the classic Wildcards, new OSS has actually lost a few (Trisha and Parvati), otherwise kept: Rudras GunBot, Arjuna FTO, Samekh Rebot FTO (ie. the un-Guided Missile Launcher remote), Maximus (we assume they mean Max-mode and not the TAG-form) and Hawkwood. The Arjuna remains a solid option for adding a Tactically Aware specialist, who is a decent (but not great) gunfighter, to a fireteam. Otherwise, the rest of the Wildcards are a smidge too expensive to drop into the Fireteams of this sectorial, which are already pretty good without Wildcards.

Genghis: This is another chunk of information we need confirmation on. As written here, TAG form Maximus is a true Wildcard, and that's just nuts. He's a divisive unit - some players despise him for having less firepower output than a conventional TAG, and some adore him for being incredibly tough to kill. But I think we can all agree he's an extreme unit. Letting him freely join cheap, efficient Fireteams, with +1SD bonuses, Firewalls, engineer support, and whatever other combos haven't occurred to us, would surely be madness. 

Example Post-Mazebreaker List



Building an effective OSS list and using as much of the Mazebreaker kit as possible isn’t terribly hard. The only model in the kit that we wouldn’t consider an easy include would be the Naga BSG (which is a shame, as that is one baller sculpt). Sure, it’ll fit into the right list when you build it but we’re going to call in our one veto for the box and use it on the Naga. Otherwise, your first Post-Mazebreaker list might look something like this (allowing for some proxy on profile loadouts, of course): Group 1: 9 Regular, 2 Lt Orders
  • Asura Lt (+1 Order) with AP Spitfire
  • Sharvara HoundBot with ADHL, Heavy Riotstopper and E/Marat
  • Duo: Hawkwood (K1 Sniper Rifle) and ABH
  • Duo: Miranda Ashcroft (E/Mitter) and ABH
  • 2x Netrods
  • Post-Human: Mk1 Doctor (or Engineer) with 2x Yudbots and 1x Mk5 Forward Observer
Group 2: 5 Regular, 1 Irregular
  • Duo: Dikpala x2 (HMG and Paramedic) 
  • Duo: Dikpala x2 (MULTI Sniper Rifle and Repeater/Panzerfaust)
  • Shukra Consultant Boarding Shotgun and CoC
  • WarCor
Total: 15 Trooper slots (noting the Post-Humans and Peripherals), approximately 300 points and 4.5 SWC.

The only thing this list isn’t giving you that is a core feature of the OSS-experience is going to be hacking your opponent into oblivion (but not necessarily Oblivion). If you swap out the Asura AP-Spitfire for an Asura Hacker and tweak a few costs here and there, I think you can make that happen, as well.

Genghis: That list has a lot of ranged firepower! I don't know if I would go for more Houndbots and skirmishers or Scoutbots, myself. But hey, I'll be playing against OSS, not for them!

Conclusion: What Are we Excited for in Operational Sub-Section?

Hello Ruby Monday, who could hang a name on you? (Corvus Belli)

The new units, even just those we've seen so far, have substantially changed OSS. They've got some much more esoteric new surprises, like those +1SD sniper options, and the Houndbots have a massive impact by providing cheap defence and assault. Their Fireteam flexibility has been expanded, and the traditional strengths of useful big Core teams, and widely repairable STR units with good engineer support, have only been bolstered. Musterkrux: I really like what I see. Everything that I enjoyed about classic OSS remains available and viable but some of the less commonly seen profiles, such as Nagas and Dasyus, have been given a very solid Quality of Life sweep and we’ve even got some new and exciting toys to play with that help shore up some capacities that OSS wasn’t necessarily strong in.

Genghis: I'm honestly a bit scared of the new OSS. A prediction about their meta standing would be premature - our preview info doesn't show all the new units being added, existing units could always change, and of course no preview information is necessarily final. But they've got a hell of a lot of cool stuff.
