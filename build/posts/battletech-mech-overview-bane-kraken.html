BattleTech Mech Overview: Bane (Kraken)

May 13th, 2026 via Goonhammer
MechWarriors, today we have a question to solve: How can you make a 100-ton Clan ‘Mech bad?

One possible answer can be found in the Bane. The Bane, or Kraken if you’re a Spheroid, claims its origins as a Star League prototype that went nowhere. One might draw some quick conclusions about why when reviewing its base loadout, but the Clans did not. Nonetheless, the Bane has enjoyed a long career as part of many toumans second-line forces, acting as either a long-range support platform or short-range ambusher in most configurations.

Liberty: I for one disagree with the assertion that the Bane is bad. I fuckin' love most of these things, they're silly as shit and surprisingly cost effective for my burgeoning love of fully armored 100 tonners with SFEs, LFEs, and Clan XLs.

Clan Jade Falcon Epsilon Galaxy Bane (Kraken) 3. Credit: VAlk.

The Chassis

Banes, despite being standard BattleMechs, have a few things in common across all models:
  • A Clan 300 XL engine, giving it a 3/5 move profile
  • 304 pips of armor, stopping just short of the maximum
  • No lower arm actuators (i.e., every Bane has flippy arms)
Normally, this would be fine; most Clan Assault ‘Mechs aren’t designed as punchbots and are instead meant to carry as much of their exceptional ranged weapons as physically possible (Phoenix Hawk IIC notwithstanding). The standard Bane chassis, stripped of its weapons, has a full 55.5 tons of available space to exploit. The Bane’s primary issue is how it spends that tonnage. Rather than making good use of its available tonnage to mount a mix of energy, missile, and ballistic weapons with good synergy, the Bane often goes all-in on ammunition-based weapons – specifically, autocannons. While this does make it cheaper than you might expect a 100-ton Clan Assault ‘Mech to be, it’s certainly not optimal.

Well, usually.

Variants

These mechs have all been reviewed based on a standard F through S scale, which you can find described on our landing page here (along with all of our other ‘Mech reviews, the name of the box you can buy to get any of the mechs we have covered, and our general methodology).

Standard

First things first: the base-model Bane was not designed for BattleTech as we know it. This might feel a bit confusing, but it’s an important factoid to keep in mind. We’ll get to it.

Clan Jade Falcon Gamma Galaxy Bane (Kraken) Standard. Credit: Valk.

At first glance, the Bane Standard appears to have been designed by Infantry Guy’s long-lost cousin, Autocannon Guy. The ‘Mech’s 55.5 tons of space have been allocated entirely to mounting a frankly-ridiculous 10 Ultra AC/2s (also 4 machine guns). The AC/2s are fed from 4 tons of CASE-protected ammo in the side torsos, giving you a total of 180 rounds. Even knowing we’re on the cusp of a rules change that makes you want to double tap Ultra ACs by default, it’s still not great, or even good. The theoretical upper limit on damage from this guy outside of machine gun range is 40 damage in a turn, and that's assuming all of your Ultras land both hits. On a Clan Assault ‘Mech, that’s just not enough, period. 

But why is it like that? Well, as it turns out, the Bane was one of a few ‘Mechs originally designed for the original version of the ‘Mech Duel Rules written for Solaris VII: The Game World. Per the Bane’s original designer, Ashley Pollard, the Bane Standard was designed around the original iteration of these rules, which used a different time and map scale than the final product. In this iteration, AC/2s were able to fire with more frequency than most weapons, making the Bane into a buzzsaw that historically “good” ‘Mechs would have had a harder time dealing with.

Alas, that world never came to pass, either in the Solaris VII rules or BattleTech proper, and subsequently, the Bane is just kinda…lacking. Even as a “budget” anchor taken as a cheap brick of armor, it’s still 1,950 BV that you could spend on something much more lethal or similarly durable without being too much more expensive. 

Valk’s rating: C-. Very funny to park on a hill next to your DZ and annoy people with, though.

Liberty: C. Genuinely a fully armored 100 tonner with a Clan XL for sub 2K, even if its armament is the never ending UAC/2 tide, is cool and useful. Sight this thing up on someone and start hammering shells into them as you advance. If you're worried about heat drop a gun, if not go up movement for a couple turns then sink it all right back out. For average play the 9 salvos at full tilt that you get is fine. If one of the UACs locks up what does it matter? The bomb's not real, simply fire it all until the barrels melt and the inevitable hand of the law of averages will carry you through while one of two things happen:

1- It takes all the fire and saves the rest of your list from the punishment

2- It takes no fire because your opponent doesn't view it as a threat and then by the tail of the game you've got a full armor 100 tonner with more than enough crit seek to bore your way through anything they've still got standing.

Beat them to death, god's own hail machine demands it.

2

The Bane 2 is a bit more in line with what we’re expecting to see out of a Clan ‘Mech of this size. Rather than spending all of its tonnage on plinkers, each arm mounts twin Ultra AC/10s. The machine guns stay for some reason, but moving up the autocannon scale gives the Bane 2 some punch that has to be addressed. If you don’t, it’ll waddle downrange and start pasting anything that gets too close with a quartet of double-tapped 10-pointers, as Anthony Scroggins did to my Stealth at an Adepticon Grinder in order to bring my multi-hour reign of terror to an end. You’ll build 4 heat plus movement heat when in Full Send™ mode, but it’s very easy to just single tap one or two of the guns each turn and still do consistently solid damage while cooling off.

It has a little too much ammo though; 14 tons of AC/10 food means you’ve got 35 turns of single-taps and just under 18 rounds of fire at maximum ROF, making the ammo stores a bit of a liability outside of campaign contexts. Nonetheless, at 2,502 BV, it’s a perfectly serviceable alternative to machines like the Gargoyle D or Warhawk D if you’re prioritizing armor and raw damage potential to mobility and accuracy.

Valk’s rating: C+.

Liberty's Rating: C+. Fun fact, this is one of two 'Mechs in the game with quad UAC/10s. The other one is the Annihilator C. Secondary fun fact: the only place both of these crossover is the Raven Alliance.

This thing is positively cheap for what it can do, again being a near-full-armor 100 tonner that simply advances. If you've got some heat problems just ripple off your easy set until someone steps where they shouldn't with the wrong TMM.

Then unmake them.

3

This is, I would argue, “the good one.” 

Lots of people likely first encountered the Bane 3 in their Aces: Scouring Sands campaign, and it’s every bit as potent in BattleTech as it is in Alpha Strike. All of the autocannons and machine guns have been yanked out of this one to make room for 8 LRM 15s, a one-shot Streak SRM 4, and a bunch of extra heat sinks that don’t quite make the ‘Mech heat-neutral, but gets it close enough. Both sides are made almost entirely out of explodium, but you do kind of need all 16 tons of ammo, as each full salvo of 120 missiles(!) will instantly empty a bin, and the Bane 3 is sufficiently durable that it will probably live to dump most of it downrange. 

Jade Falcon Epsilon Galaxy Kraken (Bane) 3. Credit: Jack Hunter

In many ways, the Bane 3 is the Nova Cat B’s older brother - heavier, slower, less heat-efficient, but still utterly terrifying if allowed to sling its payload every turn. The flippy arms also help it a decent amount, as it can throw all of its missiles even point blank at a target directly behind it without too much concern. The main thing holding it back from being quite as good as the Nova Cat B is cost. You’ll pay a fairly steep 2,941 BV for this one, and the extra 100 armor or so doesn’t help as much as you’d hope it would, given that it’s exceptionally vulnerable to battle armor headhunting squads due to its slow speed and lack of lower arm and hand actuators.

Valk’s rating: B-. Be sure to bring a bin of Thunder LRMs and make those enemy Fire Moths work for it.

Liberty's Rating: C. I mean, this thing certainly fucks, but I could just have a Nova Cat B and near on 500 BV still in the bank...

4

My beloved Falcons dreamed this one up, and it’s not half-bad. The Bane 4 looks at the legacy of its predecessors and decides to split the difference. It’s the first Bane on the list to mount any kind of energy weapon at all (an ER Large Laser), and then uses the rest of its tonnage to mount a trio of ATM 6 launchers and double Ultra AC/20s. It’s a decently flexible design even if there’s not much range band synergy to speak of; being nearly-max armor on a 100-ton chassis means it can plink away with the ER Large and Standard/ER ATMs while waddling in close before switching to the Ultra 20s and letting rip. You’ll need to make some hard decisions about which weapons to fire and when though; while it has retained most of the extra heat sinks from the Bane 3, it’s not nearly enough to pop off the full payload, which will drive you up by 4 heat plus movement even while single-tapping the autocannons.

The main problem is once again its lack of speed. It needs to get to difficult terrain where it can punish anything that comes in close, and while the Clan Ultra AC/20 has decent range bands, it’s still going to be hard-pressed to answer its contemporaries outside of 8 hexes in a manner befitting a ‘Mech of its size. I really want to like this one, but at 2,709 BV, there’s more flexible options for general-purpose Assault ‘Mechs, and vastly cheaper ones for double-barrel Ultra AC/20 fun (the Longbow 10K is nearly a full 1,000 BV cheaper at the expense of some armor, range, and the ATMs).

Valk’s rating: C-. At the risk of being branded an ATM heretic, I really wish this thing just had LRMs instead.

Liberty's Rating: C. I continue to cope, seethe, mald, and rage that the funny 'Mechs I want to use all seem to belong to the damnable Falcons.

I'll like... have a Trial about it, or whatever.

5

Oh what the hell.

So, good news first: LRMs are back. Bad news: is so are the AC/2s. 

The Bane 5 is just kind of a mess. The 4’s Ultra AC/20s have been replaced with a quartet of Rotary AC/2s, and the remaining tonnage was spent swapping the ATMs for LRM 10s. The ER Large is back, so that’s a plus, and there’s also now an ECM suite for some reason. The bins are CASE II protected, which is nice, but it still goes up 6 heat plus movement if you throw all your guns downrange in the hopes of doing something in the neighborhood of 34 damage (assuming an average hit rate of 3 shots in a 5-round RAC salvo and both LRM 10s land 6 missiles each).

Make no mistake, this thing is a crit-fisher extraordinaire, and Clan RAC/2s mean it can do it from a long, long ways out. Unfortunately, you’re paying 2,554 BV for it. Pass.

Valk’s rating: D+

Liberty's Rating: C+. I am the RAC/MAN, I am the Walrus, Coo-Coo-Ca-Choo.

I love RACs. It's not a secret. In a world where I can have 4 RAC/2s and a pair of LRM-10s off the rail at someone on the run and be heat neutral I am very happy. On this frame for 2,554 BV I am very happy. It's another instance of 'I'd be quite pleased to let this waddle in once the big fight is fully over even if only to see the light fleet from my enemy's eyes as they realize they still have to chew through a 100 tonner.

Fuckin' hilarious. Wonder who gets it... GOD DAMMIT IT'S JUST THE FUCKING FALCONS AGAIN!

6

We’ve arrived at the “we need an ilClan version that’s visually identical to the OG” model. 

Bane (Kraken). Credit: Rockfish

The Bane 6 is basically a remix of the Standard model. We’ve got 10 Protomech AC/2s, 4 ER Medium Lasers, the ECM suite from the 5, and now a Targeting Computer as well. It’s marginally better than the Standard in a couple of ways, being able to actually deliver hits bigger than 2 pips at a time, and CASE II is again nice to have on such an ammo-dependent design. Unfortunately, the 8-ton Targeting Computer doesn’t feel like much of a value add, and damage output is still anemic for something that costs 2,339 BV. 

There is an upshot, however: the Playtest Gear packet included new rules for AP ammo that allow for Protomech AC/2s to utilize those special munitions. Assuming those rules are finalized as described in the playtest rules, the Bane 6 becomes a fair bit better, taking a more direct approach to the Standard's "just pelt people until you get a TAC" role and having enough bins to provide 14 turns of AP fire with all PAC/2s. The Standard maintains a range and BV advantage in either case, but the AP ammo change will make this a much more appealing option.

Valk’s rating: D+, C- if the AP ammo changes stick. 

Liberty's Rating: Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop-Pop

7

At some point, the Bane’s designers apparently heard me complaining about its dedication to lots of small, long-range ballistics. The Bane 7 is the answer to those complaints, and the result is somehow…worse?

Take the Bane 6. Rip out the Proto ACs. Add a Large Pulse Laser (good). Add an extra double heat sink (good). Pull the ECM suite (huh?). Now jam 2 HAG/40s in there and 12 tons of ammo (why).

Having double HAG/40s puts the Bane 7 in very exclusive company, as it’s one of only four ‘Mechs to do so (the others being the Cygnus 2, Marauder IIC 4, and Dire Wolf D). All of them are also at least 300 BV less expensive, with the Bane 7 clocking in at an eye-watering 3,740 BV. It’s just way, way too much for something that, despite the big number, is going to do the vast majority of its damage in 5-point clusters when it hits. If you must have double HAG/40s, get the Cygnus 2 instead – it’s nearly as well-armored, faster, and has nearly the same backup guns, losing only the pulse laser. If not, pick a big LRM boy like the Nova Cat B, Night Gyr D, Viking IIC, or even the Bane 3 and pocket the extra BV.

Valk’s rating: F.

Liberty's Rating: D. Having recently started using the Marauder IIC 4, essentially this but 300 BV cheaper to ditch the guns that aren't HAG/40s while going 4/6/3, I honestly sorta fuck with 'Mechs kind of like this. They often end up viewed as either the hell machine that needs to die that is going to refuse to do it because the explosives are out in the arms so the worst that happens is head hits while the enemy spends 5 turns trying to beat it apart; OR the weird backliner that's just capitalizing on what the heavy frontline is doing that slowly but surely grinds the enemy's armor away like a running belt sander being spun around the room on a big rope.

Never know what it's gonna hit but when it does it's gonna fuckin' hurt.

Kraken-XR

Technically speaking, this one’s not a Bane. The Kraken-XR is an experimental version of the Bane developed by the Jade Falcons that got the IS designation instead. One would assume that’d mean it’s good, but it’s actually just a prototype version of the Bane 5. It carries a very similar weapons load of 4 RAC/2s, ER Large Laser, and 2 Streak LRM 10s, but it uses laser heat sinks and carries dramatically less ammo, with 2 tons each of RAC/2 and Streak LRM ammo. Adding insult to injury, the ammo is protected by CASE II, but rather than keeping it all in the side torsos, the RAC/2 ammo is split off in the arms, meaning you’re wasting a half ton for CASE II there instead of just folding it into the existing storage for the LRMs. The Bane 5, thankfully, does this, which allows that variant to mount an extra heat sink.  

The worst crime this ‘Mech commits is in its armor. It maintains 99% armor coverage like its true Bane brethren, but for some reason, the Falcons chose to swap its standard armor for reflective armor, which is the equivalent of a "kick me" sign on something this slow. Sure, you’ll shrug off energy weapon hits from other Clan Assault ‘Mechs that are actually designed to kill things, but god help you if a punchbot ever rolls up on you, which it will, since you can’t really shoot down ‘Mechs big enough to threaten you with physical attacks when you’ve blown 34 tons of your payload on RAC/2s of all things. 

Oh, and it’s 3,055 BV. 

Valk’s rating: F. You can see why they only made one of these.

Final Thoughts

I want to like the Bane. I’ve always wanted to like the Bane. Unfortunately, there’s just rarely much to like here. 

Wanting to use most Bane variants means asking yourself a question during force building: "can I annoy my opponent with 2-pointers enough to make them chew through a 100-ton ‘Mech to make it stop rather than play the objective?" The answer is almost universally "no, especially not for that cost." It’s a big brick of guns and armor, but more often than not it doesn’t use it well, either spending too much tonnage on long-range, low-caliber weapons or short-range, high-caliber weapons it has trouble closing with.

There’s also some thematically-appropriate variations that could be done, but haven’t. Where’s the LB 2-X variant to annoy people from multiple map sheets away, or an Ultra AC/5 model to do decent damage at a decent price with ridiculous amounts of armor? God willing, they’ll break out of Arkham some day, but until then, the Bane will likely continue being a canvas for adventurous painters looking to practice freehand murals and little else.
