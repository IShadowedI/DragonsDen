Tabletop Battles Reviews: Kill Team Spectre Squad

May 6th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Greetings readers, TheArmorOfContempt, along with the Kill Team brain trust, here to give you another rundown on the latest for Kill Team. Today I’ll be going over the Spectre Squad Kill Team half of the Terror on Devlan box set! As always thank you to Games Workshop for providing the rules in advance!

Before we get into the nitty gritty, it's worth giving a quick heads up on what to expect from this team. The Spectres are a special forces Guardsman team in the vein of Kasrkin, trading in their hot shot weapons and carapace armor for abilities that are meant to showcase their role as a light scout force. Is this immediate loss of general durability and lethality for utility enough to let them compete?

If you'd prefer a video review, Can You Roll a Crit? has one for you!

Spectre Squad. Credit: Warcom

Faction Abilities

Elite Fieldcraft

Every round the Spectres generate up to 2 Fieldcraft points that can be used to Ready a friendly operative, not in control range, to perform either a free Shoot, Reposition, or Dash after an Engaged enemy performs an action during its ACTIVATION. There are some restrictions here, such as only being able to Shoot the enemy that performed the action, not being able to move closer to that enemy or other enemies or their Drop Zone, and not allowing operatives who use this ability to Reposition to do so again during that Turning Point. This ability cannot be used to interrupt an enemy activation twice; for example, if you already have another operative on Guard, you can use either ability to interrupt but not both. Last but not least, your Shoot action also can’t be used to go on Guard. So it comes with a lot of restrictions; however, none of them prevent this from being a very strong core team ability that amounts to essentially having your entire team on Guard at all times regardless of Kill Zone. Lastly, it's important to note that while you can’t use Elite Fieldcraft if you’re in Control Range you can still perform a point-blank shooting attack if an enemy charges you. The biggest weakness of this ability is that it does not proc off enemy counteractions. 

Camo-Cloaks

Surprising absolutely no one, the team comes with Camo-Cloaks, which do a lot to make up for their lack of carapace armor. 

Operatives

The Spectres are an 11 selection team, however one of those selections is a REQUIRED expendable choice that acts purely as a buffing mechanic, making it in reality a 10 operative team with 8 wounds as you’ve come to expect from elite humans, but with the 5+ save normally associated with non-elite soldiers like Brood Brothers or Veteran Guardsmen. Also, as mentioned in the intro, they lack hot-shot las weapons and instead can be equipped with either lascarbines or lasrifles, which are essentially regular lasguns that either have Lethal 5+ or Rending depending on your choice. Personally, I am a huge fan of this decision as lasguns and autoguns feel nearly useless in most games of Kill Team, so giving them a bit more without simply upping their damage to 3/4 is a nice compromise. Lastly, their melee is standard human gun butts across most of the operatives, and their shooting hits on a +3 as standard.

NotThatHenryC: The Lethal 5+ option also comes with Heavy (Reposition), which is annoying. Spectres have a couple of ways to get extra APL and can fall back for 1APL with a ploy, so you can do without this limitation. I prefer Rending anyway, as it gives your operatives the chance to spike and get damage through by landing multiple crits. A single crit for 3 damage just isn’t that great.

Spectre Squad Veteran Sergeant and Gunner. Credit: Warcom

Veteran Sergeant

Comes with the generic +1 wound, but instead of your usual pistol/melee setup, has a slightly better las weapon and a bionic fist that makes him 3/4 dmg. His main strength is he has the Issue Mission (0 AP) ability that allows him to select a visible friendly operative that has not used the Elite Fieldcraft ability, and that operative may use Elite Fieldcraft until the end of the Turning Point even if they aren’t Ready. This is a great ability that enhances the core team rule. Frankly, I love how they went with the Sergeant; while not giving him a power sword like his other Imperial counterparts is technically bad from a competitive aspect, it’s nice to see a leader being equipped with abilities and weapons that feed directly into the team’s playstyle. Additionally, they made the correct decision of making his special ability cost 0 AP, meaning he can still contribute normally while buffing his operatives instead of forcing you to pick one or the other.

Spectre Squad Grenadier and Field Medicae. Credit: Warcom

CYRAC: The leader is nice although I would have liked their Bionic Arm to at least have some keyword buffs to make it a little more threatening.

Field Medicae

A medic in all the normal ways, except his ability to heal is 0 AP, which automatically makes him the best medic in the game apart from the Battleclade medic. 

Spectre Squad Guide and Trooper. Credit: Warcom

Grenadier

A combination of a standard grenadier and the Kasrkin Demo-Trooper. Has access to both types of offensive grenades, AND smoke, but can also place a Melta Mine in exactly the same fashion as the demo-trooper. Does NOT come with a big satchel bomb like some grenadiers do, or stun grenades.

Guide

An interesting take on the Recon operative. All terrain features within your territory or within three inches of this operative count as having been Scouted. Once per activation, friendly operatives may either A) ignore two inches of their first climb or B) Perform Operative Hatch for one less AP on terrain features that have been scouted. This is awesome, and basically makes the Guide an auto-include as they provide incredible mobility within your own territory regardless of which type of Kill Zone you’re in. As a final little bonus the Guide also provides a fifth Equipment choice IF that choice is an ammo cache or terrain equipment. 

Trooper

Standard dude with las weapon, but he has the ability to perform Elite Fieldcraft without using one of your points, which might make him the first generic operative choice since Naval Armsman that you’re heavily incentivized to take over one of your specialists.

NotThatHenryC: I’ve found the Trooper disappointing. Yes, they get to do Elite Fieldcraft for free… but with a lasgun. More usefully, they can grab a Retrieval token as your last activation, reposition away with the Strat ploy and run off again with Elite Fieldcraft.

Spectre Squad Heavy Gunner and Loader. Credit: Warcom

Gunner and Heavy Gunner

The Gunner gets either a Plasma or Melta, and the Heavy is your Missile Launcher. Neither come with any special abilities, and the latter comes with Heavy and the extremely limited ability to become Heavy (Dash) if the Loader is within control range when they begin their activation. If this weapon was on any other team I’d immediately just call it straight trash, but thanks to Elite Fieldcraft, it can be used to really keep your opponent on their toes. 

Loader

Has the Load Weapon (1 AP) unique action that allows a friendly operative in control range, but outside of three inches of an enemy to immediately perform a free shoot action, if the loader didn’t Charge, Shoot, or Dash during its own activation. Additionally, friendly operatives in control range can re-roll a single attack die when shooting. Frankly, this guy is a bit lackluster; while double shooting your gunners is powerful, the range requirement means you’re opening yourself up for a blast retaliation or double charge. 

CYRAC: For some reason, Load Weapon works on any weapon instead of just the Missile Launcher. Despite clearly holding spare missiles. You know what that means. Double-firing Meltagun!

Spectre Squad Sharpshooter and Stub-gunner. Credit: Warcom

NotThatHenryC: Yep, I also like it for the Sharpshooter’s first Silent shot, preferably on TP1.

Sharpshooter

A standard sniper. Not much else to say.

Stub-Gunner

Technically your fourth specialist gunner option, they come armed with, unsurprisingly, a stubber which falls into the odd category of 5 atks, 3/4 damage, and Heavy (Dash), essentially an inferior version of what the Wyrmblade have from a lethality standpoint, but it does make up for that by coming with a Seek Light profile that does no damage and has Stun, called Suppressive. Oddly, this operative also has a three inch debuff aura ALSO called Suppressive Fire that reduces the attack profile of all enemy weapons by 1 if they’re also visible, and the Stub-Gunner is engaged and not in control range of a different enemy operative. I see what they wanted to do with this operative, but frankly this is just clunky. Why this isn’t simply part of the Seek Light shooting profile, I have no idea.

NotThatHenryC: This operative is bizarre, and sort of good-ish. 2APL teams can’t really charge the Stub-gunner, or even move to shoot at it. They’ll find their APL gets shot off and they aren’t able to do anything. It’s strange to machine gun people at point blank range for 0 damage though.

Spectre Squad Vox-operator and Vox-relay Beacon. Credit: Warcom

Vox Operator

Normal Vox guy, but has no range restriction; however they still need visibility because radio waves apparently immediately stop when they make contact with a solid surface?

Vox Relay Beacon

This is a strange one. This operative cannot move nor really negatively affect your opponent in any way, but is also expendable. Its only abilities are the standard boost of a friendly operatives APL much like the Operator, however now you don’t need visibility, but must be within 6 inches (wtf is going on here rules writers?), and it can be set up anywhere in your territory during operative setup provided it is 2 inches from markers or terrain equipment. In essence, a second Vox Operator, which is VERY GOOD, but also baffling in its inclusion as nothing else really works off the Relay Beacon, and given its fragility and inability to move, its ability to set up forward from your deployment is of questionable utility. 

Ploys

We have a mixed bag of ploys with this team. It is nice that I can say upfront that none of them feel broken, which is a welcome switch from Murderwing. 

Firefight Ploys

Dodge: Allows an operative to Fallback for 1 AP. This is rather standard ploy for this kind of team, which has its obvious uses, especially given how weak this team is in close combat.

Silent Killers: An operative who has a Conceal order and not a valid target for an enemy operative can perform a Charge from Conceal AND may deal an additional 3 damage on its first strike if you choose to not resolve any other successes. A useful but very situational ploy, as I continue to mention this team is weak in close combat, so having the ability to pop an opponent for 5-6 damage without inviting retaliatory shooting is good to have in your pocket. 

Sharp Reactions: Essentially allows you to use Fieldcraft against an enemy that has the Conceal order if they’re within 8 inches. Basically, this is your work around for when your opponent has the ability to Charge from conceal as normally that would prevent you from using Fieldcraft to point-blank shoot. 

Prepared Defense: Allows an operative that either has a Conceal order or is Ready and retaliating to do one of the following A) perform a pre-fight block or B) block two successes with a single success of your own. This can not be used in the same activation in which you have used Elite Fieldcraft. Again, not terrible, but niche. 

Strategic Ploys

Disappear: Allows an operative to perform a Reposition as long as they don’t get closer to enemy operatives or Drop Zone unless said operative is within your own Drop Zone. Additionally, if you use this ploy you may not perform Reposition again with that operative during the Turning Point. Strong competitor for the best ploy on the team. Out of activation movement is always strong, and this ploy is an excellent way to make up for losing Initiative, or simply trying to bait your opponent out.

Hidden Engagement: Friendly operatives gain Balance on their shooting attacks if they are in cover from the perspective of the target. It's important to note your opponent still gets to choose the cover line most suitable to them, however this will rarely matter unless your positioning is particularly bad. This ploy is your primary source of re-rolls, which is unfortunate because it falls firmly in the “ok” category. 

Ambush Volley: Shooting profiles of operatives who are more than 3 inches from the enemy and not a valid target when they begin their activation gain Devastating 1. Again, not terrible, between Lethal 5+ Lasrifles and an overcharged Plasma this team has decent access to critical hits. Provides additional chip damage against elites or is good for simply messing GEQ equivalent wound breakpoints. 

Patience: Tied with Disappear for the most powerful ploy on the team. When declared you can do one of the following during the Firefight Phase A) Skip an activation or B) Have Relentless on a Fight or Shoot action performed by your last operative to activate. The first ability means your team can bump up to 12 activations for a turn, putting you on par with Kroot or Brood Brothers, while the later is great to have against elites since not a single operative has re-rolls built into ANY of their weapon profiles. It's important to note you don’t have to declare which ability you wish to use when you spend the Command Point on this ploy. 

NotThatHenryC: I really like Disappear. A Spectre can do Retrieval on their 11th activation, particularly with APL-buffing shenanigans, then be gone again before the enemy even knew they were there.

Equipment

Sniper Overwatch:  Once per Turning Point one of your operatives can use a slightly weaker variant of the standard sniper profile, trading out a small reduction in accuracy and damage for Heavy Dash (as opposed to Heavy). I think this will be a near auto-pick for a team that frankly is a bit light on serious shooting threats. 

Starshell Flare: A Stun Grenade that can be used as a Strategic Gambit. It goes away once you have successfully stunned an opponent, making it a slightly more useful version of the actual stun grenade? Likely to only be used against opponents where stun is exceedingly powerful like Felgor, however this team will need a lot more than that to have a hope in that match up. 

TVID-Feed Triangulation: Once per Turning Point allows an operative to ignore Obscuring if the enemy in question is either a valid target to another friendly operative, OR within 6 inches of your Vox-Relay Beacon. This is sadly likely a must take against elites as you have no way to negate Smoke Grenades, which will very easily mute any shooting you have. This very much feels like an ability that should be on one of the operative’s datacard. 

Advanced Camouflage: Operatives may perform the Advanced Camouflage (1 AP) ability to gain super conceal. Again, a very niche choice, but may have its uses on Tomb World against teams that have Seek Light in abundance. 

Final Thoughts

Spectre Squad Loader and Heavy Gunner. Credit: Warcom

So…is the team good? Yes and no. From a rules design standpoint, this is a solid team. The core ability is powerful, and it along with all the other abilities feel in accordance with a team of stealth operatives. Additionally, everything in your repertoire has a use; there are no trashbin level ploys or equipment here.

Unfortunately, from a competitive standpoint this team is on the low end; we’re talking Stealth Suits territory here. You can win games -- this team in the hands of a great player will still be better than a S-Tier team piloted by a mediocre one -- but you aren’t likely to win any Major GTs, sorry. I’d be willing to move this team up to mid-tier in a world where Goremongers or Felgors don’t absolutely slam dunk it into the trash bin. To compound matters, the Tac Op archetypes available are Recon and Intelligence, neither of which gives this team a lot of strong options. Odds are this team will operate much in the same way that Stealth Suits do, trying to zone out the central objective while performing Retrieval, and punishing anyone who enters their territory, which is where this team will shine. Where it will struggle is when you have to go on the offensive against your opponent. 

CYRAC: Power creep fears are not needed here, which is amazing. Spectre Squad would have been amazing last edition, this edition makes them unfortunately too honest in a very strong meta with teams that constantly break the core rules. Elites are slightly manageable but once they take down your Meltagun, they don’t really have much else to fear. Goremongers and Fellgor will be unbeatable for them but those two factions are designed to skew against factions like Spectre Squad unfortunately. 

They’re oddly very powerful on Gallowdark/Tomb World due to all their free movement and action economy but Spectre Squad do struggle to put out damage. I would have preferred it if the Skull wasn’t an operative and you had the option to take 2 to 4 warriors or have free abilities instead like how Death Korp used to work. Once again, I wish more kill teams were like Spectre Squad and if future faction releases are like them, then it’s great news for the game. However, I do struggle to see how they will compete in the current competitive meta. There’s just only so much an 8 wound team with 5+ saves and effectively 10 operatives can do.

NotThatHenryC: I’m glad to see that this isn’t a busted team and that the designers continue to make interesting additions to the game. These guys do feel like a proper Kill Team made up of individual operatives who all have interesting abilities. So that’s all good.

I agree that these will ultimately play a bit like Stealths. They have the same Tac Ops, for a start. Like Stealths they are fragile, have decent shooting and very little melee ability. I’ve been quite enjoying playing my Stealths, so maybe that’s a good thing. There are several good Tac Ops for them and that provides a route to victory: Hide, score 9 points for Tac and Primary. Try to edge the Kill Op and not give too much away on the enemy's Crit or Tac Op. It's quite a negative approach though, which not everyone will enjoy playing with, or against.

This is not an easy team to run, and I don’t just mean competitively. The right order of activations isn’t always obvious, since you want Ready operatives available to react to stuff. In a test game my opponent and I spent quite a lot of time trying to work out all the potential reactions we might trigger if we did anything. We had a confusing stand-off with the melta Spectre on one side of a wall and some Celestians on the other. Nobody wants one of their operatives melted on its own activation, only for the meltagunner to then have their turn immediately afterwards. But the gunner didn’t want to activate either, as then he’d just get smashed afterwards.

It’s a fun team to use for Joint Ops. You can easily imagine them in a 1980s monster horror film, which is pretty much the set up in Terror on Devlan. They play their part as the humans up against unspeakable alien horrors. NPOs will carelessly blunder into their traps, which is satisfying until a bunch more of them spawn in, and then...well, you know how that ends.
