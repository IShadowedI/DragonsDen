Warmachine 3D Faction Overview

May 7th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome back, dear reader, for more Warmachine goodness here at Tabletop Battles! This week, continuing our series on Warmachine faction overviews by looking at the legacy factions currently available through Warmachine 3D.

What Is Warmachine 3D?

Warmachine 3D is a program that Steamforged launched in 2025 in collaboration with MyMiniFactory with a focus on providing Warmachine related items that 3D printing enthusiasts can purchase an STL for and print at home. Since Steamforged doesn’t want to step on retailers toes, these models don’t include the Prime faction stuff, but it does include other neat things for enthusiasts such as faction terrain, “extreme” alternate sculpts, and several out of production (OOP) legacy factions.

Credit: Steamforged Games

These factions, which we will refer to as the Warmachine 3D factions, have had their rulesets slightly tweaked in order to bring them more in line with the current prime factions. Several have even received new models or brand new sculpts through Warmachine 3D, such as Vorgoroth for the Infernals faction. Since these factions get a little more love than the regular legacy factions, its best to think of the Warmachine 3D factions as “Legacy Plus”, and are a much more common sight on tables thanks to their increased availability.

Its a nifty little program that breathes life into many of the game’s older factions, and according to Steamforged there’s plans to continue and add even more to Warmachine 3D in the future. We’ve got a basic overview for all these factions ready to go for you right here. So whether you’re someone with a 3D printer who wants to jump into the battlefields of the Iron Kingdoms yourself, or you want to know more about these factions in case you find yourself playing against them, we’ve got you covered!

The Warmachine 3D Factions

Crucible Guard

Credit: Steamforged Games

What’s Their Deal?

Crazy Combat Alchemists. In the Iron Kingdoms, the Order of the Golden Crucible held a monopoly on the production of blasting powder (basically gun powder), and made a fortune selling it to everyone. That monopoly fell apart with the Khadoran invasion. The Order of the Golden Crucible stuck around though, and the Crucible Guard is their militant wing. They operate using a wide variety of special effects on their weapons, while wearing their chemical safety gear at all times.

Playstyle

Every Crucible Guard caster has the Chemical Warfare rule, doubling the damage done by continuous effects, like fire and corrosion. There are a wide variety of units in the faction that are able to apply, or synergize with, continuous effects to create some nasty combos. They have two main classes of units: hard hitting slow units, in the form of Assault Troopers or Failed Experiments, and extremely light mobile units in the form of Crucible Guard Rocketmen and Rocketmen Gunners, all enabled by potent support units. 

Popular Units

Rocketmen are commonly played due to their high DEF of 15. Since the January ‘26 update RAT and MAT values have been dropped, making DEF 15 very hard to deal with for many factions. The Vulcan, Crucible Guard’s Colossal, is also popular due to the Unnatural Affliction rule, removing enemies immunities to fire/corrosion/cold/electricity, allowing Crucible Guards combos to affect them. 

Dark Operations

Credit: Steamforged Games

What’s Their Deal?

Dark Operations are a group known as the Cephalyx. These are mind controlling, body modifying, masking wearing monsters that live in the sewers. The Cephalyx mind control their victims to either use them as fodder, or mutate them into something far worse. 

Playstyle

Boxes, boxes, boxes! Or Dudes, dudes, dudes! Or somewhere in between.

Dark Operations field slow, low DEF monstrosities that have a ton of health, referred to as boxes. These monstrosities, while easy to hit and relatively low ARM, make up for it by having 36 boxes on each and every one. They also all have the hyper aggressive rule, allowing them to react and advance when taking damage. The flip side of the faction are the Drudges. These are mind controlled individuals from other factions that act as chaff for the Cephalyx. Drudges take a few different flavors, with units representing units stolen from Cygnar, Orgoth, and Khador, as well generic people that have been mind controlled. Drudges tend to be low MAT, easy to kill, but dirt cheap and bring a number of interesting effects. 

Popular Units

Thexus and Cyphon are the two more popular leaders. Thexus’s feat allows you to push enemies models 2” in any direction, bring enemies closer for an alpha strike, or break up formations of units that are synergizing amongst each other. Cyphon’s feat brings a +3 ARM swing. Mix that with a gaggle of Monstrosities, and it creates a tough brick to crack. 

Thornfall Alliance

Credit: Privateer Press

What’s Their Deal?

Pigs with mechanical upgrades and all the insanity that comes with. Part mad science experiment, part mayhem machines, the Thornfall Alliance pigs were one of the most popular legacy mercenary factions back in the day, and are the most recent of the Warmachine 3D factions. 

Playstyle

As of April 2026, pigs have not been released fully in Warmachine 3D, and are currently not being played often. There is not a large enough play history to say what units are popular or what play style they will settle on. In general though, the Thornfall Alliance tends to reward aggression and pushing your pigs to the limit. Many of the warbeasts have rules that reflect this ethos, boosting their damage output or speed at the cost of taking some damage themselves.

Popular Units

Again, it’s hard to say what’s going to end up on top of this pile of angry hogs, as the faction is still so new to the Warmachine 3D world. There is quite a bit of buzz around the new Chainbelchers though, which are battle pig centaurs with a gnarly harpoon gun arm. These are heavy cavalry that love to get stuck in, and can use their harpoons to close the gap with the enemy with frightening speed. The reverse Scorpion “Get over here!”, if you will. 

Convergence of Cyriss

Credit: Privateer Press/Steamforged Games

What’s Their Deal?

Convergence of Cyriss are worshipers of the god Cyriss, the mistress of numbers. They believe in the supremacy of numbers and the machine, and transfer peoples souls into machines to power them. All of their models have the construct rule, with a mechanical focus and clockwork visual motif. 

Playstyle:

Convergence’s Vectors (their Warjacks) are unique - they do not have a MAT or RAT value, instead taking the values of their warcaster. For example, Axis the Harmonic Enforcer has a MAT of 7, and a RAT of 4, any Vectors in his battle group will have the same. This allows you to emphasize different aspects of a Vector's capabilities based on the leader. Additionally, Vectors do not power up, instead, they have the special rule “Force Induction”. When a Vector spends a focus it may pass that focus off to another Vector within 9”. This allows a handful of focus to be passed around the army like a hot potato, adding value over and over. Careful management of the chain of power drives activation order, as well as impacting where Vectors will be on the battlefield, and leads Convergence to field more Warjacks than many other factions.

Popular Units:

The Corollary is a staple for Convergence. The Corollary allows for a little focus generation with the Accumulator rule. More importantly though, it has the focus battery rule, allowing focus to be held from one turn to the next. If a convergence player plans and executes their plan perfectly, they will take focus from the Corollary, pass it all throughout their army going from Vector to Vector, before finally returning that focus to the Corollary, allowing the focus to be used again the following turn. 

Grymkin

Credit: Steamforged Games

What’s Their Deal?

Grymkin are defiers of Menoth (the God of Law). They were punished and exiled to Urcaen (roughly the underworld) where they built their domain and punished the wicked. As the infernal invasion drew near, the Old Witch summoned them to the Iron Kingdoms to punish the wicked and hunt down infernal worshipers. It kind of worked. Kind of. Now that the infernal invasion has come and gone most of the Grymkin have retreated to Urcaen, but some still remain. As for how they look, the Grymkin is best described as “what if Spirit Halloween wanted to kill you?”, leaning hard into campy horror aesthetics from the scariest of stories you ever read in the dark. 

Playstyle

Grymkin rely heavily on corpses, collecting corpses from enemies, then passing them around their army, powering up various abilities. Additionally, Grymkin leaders do not have feats in the traditional sense, instead they have additional command cards that offer exceptionally potent abilities that function as minifeats. 

Popular Units

The current meta list is The Child with two Slaughterhouses. The Child brings (almost) free charges for cohort models, and a potent threat extender with Abuse. The Cage Rager, once it has a corpse, is able to shut down enemy spells, limiting potent enemy spells. The Grave Ghoul is able to dig up corpses with no outside interaction and power up key Grymkin abilities prior to any models being killed. 

Credit: Privateer Press/Steamforged Games

Swiftblade: I’d be remiss if I didn’t take a moment to highlight the Slaughterhouse here. It's just too fun. One, the design rips. It’s a gigantic house, a la Final Fantasy VII, and much like that iconic enemy it exists only to cause violence and mayhem.

Two, this house that leaves you in the dumpster has a very nasty ability with its gun, the Porch Light. When it hits an enemy with the Porch Light, it drags them base to base with the Slaughterhouse, ignoring all intervening models or terrain. If you’re not careful, the Slaughterhouse is prone to dragging even your best protected models out of position and right into its POW 21 front door. 

Infernals

Credit: Privateer Press

What’s Their Deal?

Infernals are an order of demons from beyond reality and the maniacs that follow them. Infernals arrived in the Iron Kingdoms to collect a debt of souls owed to them in exchange for humans' access to magic in the ancient past. In the current Prime lore, the Infernals have collected as many of the souls as they could easily collect and have retreated to whence they came (for now).

Playstyle

Infernals have multiple mechanics that focus on collecting and spending souls from the battlefield. Additionally, infernal masters do not automatically regenerate essence (their version of focus) every turn, and instead must sacrifice a model to replenish its essence. Those cultists do serve a purpose! Lastly, and most importantly, Infernals can summon horrors (their cohort models) onto the battlefield, allowing them to bring more and more hard hitting pieces over the course of the game. This power comes at a cost, as part of summoning a horror requires sacrificing a solo with the Marked Soul rule to the process, and Infernal solos tend to be very powerful in their own right. The need for extra marked souls means Infernals run an abnormal number of solos; don’t be surprised to see seven solos on the table to enable summons.

Popular Units

Agathon, The Voice in the Darkness is their most popular leader. He has a potent spell list, a terrifying assassination run, and Field Marshal [Sacred Ward], meaning models in his battle group can’t be targeted by your opponents spells. Infernals also have a handful of solos that pop up in almost every list. The Hermit brings an armor debuff. Eilish brings Puppet Master, allowing for rerolls. Lord Roger D’Vyaros provides spell cost reduction, and spell slave allowing for even more spells outside of activation. The Infernal colossal, the Guardian of Souls, brings a relatively cheap huge base and access to an armor buff against models without souls, buffing Infernals against soulless models and constructs. 

And that brings us to the end of the currently available Warmachine 3D factions! New Legacy factions are planned to make their Warmachine debut as time goes on, and if enough new releases come out we may do another article like this to cover them! See you then, dear reader.
