[40k] Competitive Innovations in 10th: Wind Down

May 13th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Welcome to Competitive Innovations, our weekly feature for a number of game lines across the tabletop wargame space. Each week our army list and tactics experts canvass the top tournaments for their game of specialty, looking at the week’s top match-ups and how they played out on the tabletop to let you know what’s hot, what’s not, and what might be coming next as the competitive meta evolves year-round.

After a few wild weeks in a row, we suddenly have a big dip in numbers this week. This suits me fine - we are in the process of preparing some cool end-of-edition retrospective content, and I was almost wondering about taking a full week off anyway. As it is, we'll have a quick rundown of the events over the 30+ player threshold (which is likely to become the permanent cutoff for full reviews going into 11th), and I'll see you next week for (hopefully) a few more events, plus the start of our big retrospective.

This Week's Events

  • The 2026 Central Arkansas Warhammer 40K Grand Tournament
  • 09 & 10 May 2026 - OWN's Breaking Point GT at Game HQ

Archetype

Ultra-heavy Votann

Thoughts

Good lord that's a chunky dwarf build, and in the current metagame you can't really fault it. Both big squads of Thunderkyn can seriously threaten the current metagame nemesis out of their transports, with the beamer unit able to pop off with Ancestral Sentence, and the Graviton unit plausibly able to smoke a Defiler without much help. Packing everything in lots of Forts makes this daunting to counterattack into as well, hopefully ensuring full delivery of the big guns. You're definitely gambling on not hitting something like Kroot, Recon Element or Hunting Pack, as I think all of those can just bog this down and outscore it, but if you run into all the popular crunchy crabs, you're laughing. The same applies if you hit a bunch of other Monster Mash/hull spam lists, which seems to have been what happened here as Hunter was dead-on about what metagame to target, and romped to victory as a result!

The Best of the Rest

There was 5 more players on 4.5-1.5+ records. They were:
  • 2nd (5-1) - Heath Shepherd - Necrons (Cursed Legion): Hexmarks, Lokhusts and Skorpekh, oh my, with not a C'tan in sight.
  • 3rd - Taylor Barger - Grey Knights (Sanctic Spearhead): Lots of extra-speedy Dreadknights, Interceptors to keep pace with them, and one Paladin unit as a troubleshooting threat.
  • 4th - Blake McCutcheon - Adepta Sororitas (Bringers of Flame): Hull spam Sororitas with many Castigators and Immolators.
  • 5th - Georde "Gator" Murray - Imperial Knights (Questoris Companions): Ultra hefty five bigs, including a Castellan.
  • 6th - Randall Robinson - Chaos Daemons (Scintillating Legion): Three Birds, Be'lakor and a Daemon Prince.

Archetype

Defiler-killing Elves

Thoughts

Aspect Host takes a surprise win at our other event, with Cam deploying a very metagame-targeted build that I honestly kind of love. As mentioned last week, Wraithlords are low-key excellent in Defiler and hull-spam land; sure, they die easy, but they're very good at popping out and dropping heavy damage on hulls. Fire Prisms are much the same, and the Avatar, while comically overcosted if you benchmark against other big stuff at this point, is enough of a counter-charge threat to discourage foes from pushing too hard. The build also manages to shore up its anti-horde credentials reasonably well with Asurmen and Lhykhis, both of whom rack up massive body counts, and even more if they team up. Asurmen is also your one remaining properly evasive threat, and he'll do plenty for you. I honestly adore this build and I'm suddenly having a crisis of whether I should audible into running exactly this for my (probably final) 10th Edition event this weekend instead of running out the C'tan again. List submission isn't till tonight and I'd only have to paint a couple of gun swaps...

Well done Cam for leading me into temptation, I guess.

The Best of the Rest

There were 4 more players on 4-1 records. They were:
  • 2nd - Hunter Boyd - Adeptus Custodes (Talons of the Emperor): Custodian and Land Raider spam.
  • 3rd - Marc Nuessen - Grey Knights (Warpbane Task Force): A sort of Warpbane take on the above with Land Raiders and various Terminator/Purifier units to fill them up.
  • 4th - Troy Rooney - Emperor's Children (Coterie of the Conceited): Double Defiler Coterie.
  • 5th - Brandon Whitehurst - Tyranids (Invasion Fleet): A mix of big gun bugs, big hormagaunt squads and a couple Hyperadapted Ravener units.
