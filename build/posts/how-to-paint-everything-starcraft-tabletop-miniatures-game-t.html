How to Paint Everything: StarCraft Tabletop Miniatures Game Terran Marines

May 12th, 2026 via Goonhammer
The not so humble Marine is the backbone of the armies of the Terrans of StarCraft. Whether the surly freedom fighters of Raynor's Raiders, the former convict hicks of the Terran Dominion, or the strangely similar marines of the United Earth Directorate, they all have a few things in common: They don't want to be here, they have a big gun, and they've got about ten minutes of life expectancy.

The Marine is probably the first unit you encounter while starting any StarCraft game. Armed with C-14 gauss rifles, they will bravely march to their doom, get dropped behind the enemy's mineral line to blast some Drones, or most preferably, get embarked into a bunker and forgotten about. Most millennials know their unit barks and distinctive rifle sound effects by heart.

How Were They Portrayed in the Video Game?

"Jacked up and good to go" - StarCraft OG Terran Marines

In the original StarCraft, the Marines were grey/steek human shaped blobs, with the team colors on their pauldrons, the tips of their guns, and their little booty flaps. Their visors are pitch black.

"Give me something to shoot." - StarCraft Remastered Terran Marines

The StarCraft: Remastered version of the Marine smooths out the eye-cutting pixels of the 1998 game, and unifies the look somewhat with the eventual StarCraft II version. The team color remains on the same places as the original sprites, but the metallic body is noticeably a darker steel than before, though it still highlights to a bright shine. This is also the introduction of the chest mounted lamps.

"This better be good..." StarCraft 2 Terran Marines

The 3D edition of the Marine sees the introduction of the mag-locked combat shield, presumably to give the Marine another 15 seconds of survivability. The team color is removed from the gun, but added to the top of the shoes, the helmet dome and the collar around it, and I'm just now noticing it around the exhausts on the top of the power armor. We also see heraldry for the first time, on the right shoulderpads and the fronts of the combat shields. The visor color was updated to a yellow/orange/gold, and it actually stands out here.

Some promotional art shows the marine with nearly full team color coverage on the armor, but I wasn't able to generate one like that in game. I'm not as familiar with SCII, though, so it might be more for campaign missions.

Painting Guide

They say you can paint your Marines any color, but I'm looking at my paint rack and I don't see any any color!

I've decided to go with a variation on the original StarCraft Marine here, with the brighter steel armor.

Steel Armor

Along with the StarCraft starter, I picked up a bunch of the Vallejo True Metallic Metal sets at Adepticon, and thought these miniatures would be a great place to test them out. I basecoated the Marines with Vallejo TMM Obsidian Black Airbrush, though there's no reason you could not just use a regular brush and their Base version/ The finish is pretty smooth, and I did not notice a speckling effect I've seen with other airbrush metallics.

I then did volumetric highlights with Vallejo TMM Sterling Silver Base, covering most of the basecoat outside of the recesses. I did not think the shadows were deep enough, so I applied the Vallejo TMM Sterling Silver Shade directly into the deepest recesses. I think a black oil wash might be faster here, as these guys have a lot of nooks and crannies.

Volumetric highlights were done with a mix of the Vallejo TMM Sterling Silver Base and Light paints, with final edge and reflex highlights being done with the Light.

If you only have access to the Warhammer range of paints, you could try substituting Leadblecher/Nuln Oil/Stormhost Silver for the Vallejo paints, though the Sterling Silver Light is quite a bit brighter than Stormhost.

If you want to go for a darker armor, similar to the StarCraft Remastered version, skip the volumetric highlights in favor of chunky edge highlights. You might even want to mix a little black paint into your basecoat to get it even darker.

Black Undersuit/Gun Casings



I went with my favorite way to do black. Here I targeted the weapon casings, the little ribbed parts on the front and back of the biceps, the area along the back and under the breastplate, the leg joints, and the knee joints. I later decided I'd also want the part of the shoes with the holes on it to be this color.
  1. Basecoat with Pro Acryl Coal Black. This is currently my favorite standard black paint, as I find it dries consistently matte and smooth. I also really like Warhammer Black Legion, but I would not recommend it here, as it will take more coats than you'd want to eliminate the shine of the metallic basecoat. Really, any opaque black you like would do well here.
  2. Chunky edge highlight with Warhammer Dark Reaper. Sometimes it is hard to see if you're applying too much or too little, as it's a very subtle highlight. I also glaze this color along the top of the rectangular barrel of the gauss rifle, going from about halfway down the gun to the end of the gun.
  3. Fine edge highlight with Warhammer Thunderhawk Blue. This desaturated blue is one of my favorite colors in the Warhammer range. You want to go sparingly here, only getting about 2/3rds the edges, as it will look stark if you do everything. I also do reflex highlighting with Warhammer Fenrisian Grey.
Exposed Skin/Hair



The only skin that shows on these models is on the faces of the ones you decide to leave the visor up on. It's not the easiest thing in the world to get your brush in there, so don't go too detailed on these if you don't want to.

There are many worlds in StarCraft, so you can really paint the skin of these models however you want to. For in game references, we only really get 3 versions of one really sweaty guy:

No, this isn't Kill Team aficionado and TTB author TheArmorOfContempt

Tabletop Battles has covered how to do do skin in many different ways, so pick your favorite, maybe from one of our articles like How to Paint Everything: Human Skin.

Here, I did the lighter skin with a Warhammer Bugmans Glow basecoat, washed with Warhammer Reikland Fleshshade, then highlighted up with Warhammers Cadian Fleshtone, Kislev Flesh, and Flayed One Flesh. The darker tones were with a Warhammer Catachan Flesh basecoat, washed with Warhammer Nuln Oil, then highlighted with a combination of the Warhammer Catachan Flesh with Warhammer Cadian Fleshtone, going all the way to pure Cadian.

Dirty Steel



One of the difficulties with mainly steel models is how to show what different metals look like. I usually like making the other metals look dirty and oily. Here, this was mainly the lower parts of the weapons, the front and backs of the rocket launchers, and the topmost exhaust stacks.
  1. Basecoat Warhammer Iron Warriors. If you don't have this color I highly recommend picking it up, it is great for a dingy and uncared for metal.
  2. Wash Warhammer Agrax Earthshade. The red and brown tones here help separate these parts from the neutral steal on the main body.
  3. Highlight with Vallejo TMM Sterling Silver Base. Any steel will work here, and it doesn't matter if it's the same that you used on the main steel.

Leather

If you decide to put the Slugthrowers on these models, this is where the main leather part will be applied. I also put leather on the belt on the AGG-12 Grenade Launcher, and on the foregrip underneath the rifles. I do not know why a robot hand would need a comfort grip, but I just think it looks neat.
  1. Basecoat Warhammer Mournfang Brown.
  2. Wash with Warhammer Agrax Earthshade. You can afford to be heavy with it here, the more variation you can get in the colors the more it looks like leather.
  3. Highlight with Warhammer Karak Stone, and then do extreme highlights with Warhammer Baneblade Brown. If you build the guy brandishing his pistol, the holster doesn't have any negative space, so I freehanded the space where the gun would go with Warhammer Dryad Bark, and washed that with Warhammer Nuln Oil.

Grenades



These are another optional bit for the Marines, but I like adding them as they break up the steel of the main body really well. I also painted the grenades in the AGG-12 the same way.
  1. Basecoat Warhammer Khorne Red. This is probably the best pale red on the market.
  2. Wash Pro Acryl Black Wash. I know I've mostly been doing Nuln this tutorial, but I usually just grab whichever is closer.
  3. Highlight with Warhammer Wazdakka Red, and do extreme highlights with Warhammer Squig Orange. This gives it kind of a dull highlight, and helps the grenades not stand out too much.
I went back and did the little mag clamp that holds the grenades on with the black recipe above. Painting it earlier would have just meant that I'd have to redo it again after errant brush strokes.

Team Color



This is the part I was most excited for, and the reason why I picked up the Vallejo True Metallic Metals sets. I decided on a nonstandard color here, mostly because I misremembered how green the SC1 team color was. Here I put the team color on the pauldrons, the visor dome cover and color, and their little butt triangles.
  1. Basecoat with Vallejo TMM Hydra Turquoise Base. This is already quite shiny, but I like it a whole lot more than most other colored metallics I've used.
  2. Wash all over with Vallejo TMM Hydra Turquoise Shade. This shade feels thicker than most washes, it is pretty unique. When it dries it imprints cool swirls of shade onto the base color, and gives the metallics a lot more depth than I was expecting. It is almost a candy coat effect.
  3. Re-highlight with the Base color, then mix in Vallejo TMM Hydra Turquoise Light, and finally do extreme highlights with that color alone. The light version of the colored metallics is extremely shiny, and you want to be sparing with it for sure.
If you're doing one of the canon schemes, then you could do the Confederacy/Terran Dominion with Vallejo's TMM Ruby Red triad, while if you want to do the Mar Sara Colonists/Raynor's Raiders, then look to their Sapphire Blue triad. If you're the sole Antiga Prime Colonial Militia stan on the planet, then pick up the Amethyst Purple set.

For those of you who want to do the Alpha Squadron, I wouldn't really recommend any of the white metallics on the market. I don't think they'd look distinct enough to the steel parts on the rest of the model. There I'd go with normal colors, probably a Warhammer Celestra Grey basecoat, a very thin Warhammer Coelia Greenshade recess wash, then highlight with Warhammer Uthulan Grey, and dot highlights of your favorite white paint.

Brass Metals



To add a tiny bit of interest, I picked out a few details in brass colors. These were the big circle vent things on the legs, the forearms, and the circle handle dealies on the lower portion of the chestplate.
  1. Basecoat with Warhammer Runelord Brass. I've tried many brasses, and I always come back to this one.
  2. Wash with Warhammer Devlan Mud.
  3. Edge highlight with Wahammer Canoptek Alloy.
I've also used the Pro Acryl Bronze and Light Bronze on similar models to good effect, and the Vallejo TMM Aged Metal set would produce a paler, yellower version of this color.

Visors

I didn't build any of the initial six models with visors, as the unvisored portrait from StarCraft 1 is always my mental image of the Terran Marine. However, each sculpt only gets one single head, so I'll eventually mix some in so I don't have an army of just six cloned dudes. Even without the visor bit glued on, there's a small portion of the visor visible on the model, the part under the part of the hood with the two triangle teeth.
  1. I basecoated the visor with Warhammer Mephiston Red.
  2. Instead of washing, I did all highlights here. I highlighted the whole edge of the visor with Warhammer Wild Rider Red.
  3. I then did the final highlights in the direction that the marine was facing. I think this will really help direct the eye for models with the visor down - you want to avoid the model looking silly if the face is locked looking forward while he's firing off to the left. The first highlight was with Warhammer Troll Slayer Orange, then I did smaller highlights of Warhammer Fire Dragon Bright and Warhammer Dorn Yellow.

Combat Shields



As you can tell from the lack of them on the WIP shots earlier, I left the combat shields off while painting. It would be an enormous pain in the ass to try to paint around them. Instead, I bluetacked them to my texture palette, and just painted them separately. I did the outer trim in the team color, the trim around the viewing slit in black, and the rest of it in the base steel armor. The fronts of the shields would be great for freehand, but i haven't yet decided what subfaction these guys belong to, so I left it blank.

Chest Lamps



Not strictly necessary, but there are two lamps on the front of the power armored chestplate. I couldn't decide between a yellow light to contrast the team color, or a blue one to complement it, so I did both. In either case, I basecoated the whole lamp with any white paint, here I used Pro Acryl Bold Titanium White. For the yellow lamp, I washed the whole area with Warhammer Casandora Yellow. The blue one instead got Warhammer Aethermatic Blue contrast paint. They both then got dots of white on the bulb inside of the recess.

With the exhausts I'm painting next, I thought it was enough yellow, so I went with blue here.

Exhausts



I strictly don't think that these suits should be getting quite this hot, but those confederate engineers can't be too great, and it just looks very striking. I basecoated the inside of the top of the exhausts with Warhammer Ulthuan Grey, and then painted the innermost with any white paint. I then painted the deepest area with Warhammer Phalanx Yellow, and while it was still wet, I wet blended some Warhammer Troll Slayer Orange then Warhammer Wild Rider Red along increasingly higher parts of the exhaust. After everything was dry, I dotted the deepest potion with Warhammer Dorn Yellow to make it look really hot.

Minerals

The Terran Marine kit comes with a load of mineral bits you can add to the bases across your army, so I tossed one on.

I basecoated the minerals with Warhammer Macragge Blue, then washed it with Warhammer Drakenhof Nightshade. I then drybrushed them with Warhammers Teclis Blue, Temple Guard Blue, and Blue Horror. This was a little stark, so I then washed all over with Warhammer Talassar Blue contrast paint. I then redid a light version of the Blue Horror drybrush, then dotted the tips with white paint.

Firing Effects

Everyone makes mistakes. There was one of these models that I got a little lazy on, and didn't score the barrel before using my pin vice to drill it out. You can see him up a bit there on the Team Color section.

Normally, to fix this you'd have to putty it then do the world's most careful job drilling it back out. Luckily, Archon provided cool clear plastic firing effects for nearly every gun in the range. I'm not a huge fan of clear plastic in general, but it takes primer just as well as any other plastic, and this is perfect to cover up such a horrific blunder.



To paint it, I primed the whole thing white. Then I painted Warhammer Yriel Yellow into the parts of the effect closest to the end of the barrel. I then basecoated most of the rest of it with Warhammer Troll Slayer Orange. A little further out, I painted a layer of Warhammer Evil Suns Scarlet. The tips of the explosion got Pro Acryl Coal Black. I then wet blended between all of the layers until the explosion looked mostly right. The bullet got a bit of Vallejo TMM Sterling Silver Base and Wash, and I painted a tiny bit of Warhammer Phalanx Yellow into the deepest part of the blast.

For the OSL on the gun and bayonet, I drybrushed those areas white first, then drybrushed them again with Warhammer Yriel Yellow. I probably could have gone a bit further, but I am really nervous about doing OSL still. I won't be doing too many of these firing effects, but they do certainly look cool, and it would be great to see a StarCraft battle pile with everyone shooting in random directions. Even the guy cradling the gun could be misfiring into the air.

You Wanna Piece of Me, Boy?

StarCraft Terran Marines by Craig "MasterSlowPoke" Sniffen

I had a great time painting these Terran Marines. These are the among the first models of Archon's that I've painted, and they've made a fan. I can't wait to paint more and throw them into range of a Hydralisk, where they will be reduced to a pile of goo.
