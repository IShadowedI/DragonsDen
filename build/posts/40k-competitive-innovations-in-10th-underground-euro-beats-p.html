Thoughts

The Chaos menace faces off against an interesting Ork build here, ready for us to find out whether three big horde bricks is enough to weather the storm the Defilers and friends can unleash.

The Ork list is quite interesting, looking to try and thread the needle between the strengths of Green Tide and War Horde by going Freebooter Crew. Access to an extra Waaagh Stratagem that you can (potentially) use to great effect as early as turn one gives the hordes the extra stickiness of Green Tide without forcing you to only use regular Boyz (so the better Snaggas are on the table), but proper management of the Loot objective in later turns can give you comparable krumpin' power to War Horde, making it harder for some lists to no-sell the army.

It's a pretty cool theory and I like the thought behind it, but this is a tough matchup for it, because that's so many excruciator cannon shots with re-rolls on the Chaos side. These are pretty much the perfect tool for carving through Beast Snaggas (as the D2 essentially neutralises the Feel No Pain), and adding the Huron/Chosen bomb to nuke a big squad as well is vicious. The one saving grace for the Orks is that they do have wagons for both big snagga units, so if they get the first turn and can use it to push out a bit and not get move blocked, they might have a game from there. The Chaos army has two biker units to block stuff out with, but if those aren't in position then the reach of the orks from the mid-baord is pretty huge (especially with Boardin' Rush), and in a Waaagh turn the Snagga units have a shot at pulling a Defiler down (as does Ghaz, obviously).

Essentially, the game for the Orks is whether they can stage for a Waaagh turn where they aren't move blocked to oblivion, and whether that then lands. I think that gives them an OK shot going first, because they can move the Wagons up the board and shouldn't get them opened too easily, but as soon as the CSM start dictating the pace of play, it's tough for the Freebooterz because of how easily the CSM pick them up. There's also a much higher failure chance for the Orks if they get to do their plan and whiff - all it's going to take is one Defiler tanking through what should be a lethal swing, and that's probably it. Uphill struggle, and not one they were able to overcome, but I do really like the list theory on the Ork side.

Thoughts
