Tabletop Battles Reviews: Cities of Sigmar Erasmus Zonn, Amethyst Knellmage, and Aqshian Pyrocaster Models

May 16th, 2026 via Goonhammer
What an amazing array of weirdos and warlocks released alongside the new Cities of Sigmar Battletome! Games Workshop was kind enough to send us some of the new hotness to review, and I was granted the cosmic and magickal boon of tackling the mighty WIZZARDS of the Cities of Sigmar’s mortal realms! What a treat. Three unique and interesting sorcerous sculpts to play with. So big thanks to Arch-Mage Gdubz and let's crack on.

Bored of cranking out amazing Chaos human weirdos, the sculptors for the Cities range seem to be doing their best to populate the entire mortal range with absolutely unique little guys. I’m here for it.

Boxes of WIZZARDS!!!

Amethyst Knellmage

Amethyst Knellmage-
Credit: @badusernametag

This very Mordheim weirdo is the first of two infantry Wizzards that pay homage to the Empire Battlemages of old. His helmet is stylised bell, with two cute articulated hammers atop it (that must give him a really bad headache?!). Option for a closed grill or gnarly, haggard face reveal. He holds a gloomy death candle and wields a scythe. Perched on a tall piece of fallen masonry, he is all tall narrow and gangle. Nailed it with this one. I’m not a fan of all the smoke and sculpted whisps that are fashionable in modern sculpts, but the candle flame/smoke is subtle here and it’s just right.

Note: The registration for the scythe hand is tricky to get right, took a lot of fiddling to it where it needed to be and it doesn’t register easily. 

Made mine into a Necromancer with a change of headwear and the addition of some rotten droogs. Just the part. You can never have enough Necromancers!

Empire Battlemages.
Credit: Gary Askham - Credit: Gary Askham - Instagram: @heroes_guild_e17

Aqshian Pyrocaster

The second of the new infantry mages, the Pyrocaster is all flame-boyance and bombast, in contrast to the grim stoicism of the Deathmage. Right arm held aloft in a gesture of power, the left sweeps his wand right out to the side, summoning a long sweep of flame. So much movement and energy in this sculpt, which is very cool. It all hinges on the interaction of the model, its outswept arm and the circle of flame that is sculpted to exactly fit around the front lip of the base. Hot! Blindfold and in a hat of keys, this striding Wizzard is guided by the magickal flames of Aqshian power.

The instructions for this one are great; there is a very nice plan view (looking down, from above) at the end, along with the front elevation view. These two together show you exactly how to register the model and the line of flame (comes in two parts) together on the base to get the interaction just right. Again, I’m not usually a fan of the ‘magical sculpt’, but here it’s worth it and this is an excellent model. 

Worth noting that as the model and ‘fire-circle’ have to bisect just so for the effect to work, you need to be basing this guy on a nice, flat base. Not that it could be elevated, but uneven sand and rocks are not the thing here. Something flat and solid.

To demonstrate my point here I chose to dedicate this guy to Tzeentch (sneaky, those cultists, getting into all the free cities somehow!?) and mounted him on a disk. Reckon it makes a really nice Magister in fact and the disk is also the correct circle and flatness to support the flame- wand interface. Flame on!

Aqshian Pyrocaster-
Credit: @badusernametag

Erasmus Zonn, the Enlightened One

Wow.

I am often of the opinion that whilst GW weaves a concise and well considered visual language in each of their ranges, they can also seem constricted by the established design methodology of any given army. That is to say, they seem to develop a design intent and concept and stick to it rigidly. So although well developed, it can sometimes seem predictable, or constrained. Then a model like this comes along and my preconceptions in this area are blown away. 

Erasmus Zonn is another nostalgia throwback in part, harkening to the mysterious majesty of the Gold Wizzard lord Balthasar Gelt (spoilers: He gets off'd by that douchebag Manfred Von Carstein in the Endtimes), faceless, brooding and cloaked in magical artifacts. But that is just the tip of the iceberg here. This model is quintessentially Age of Sigmar, the highest of fantasy and sporting the design markers of the Cities of Sigmar, but less obtrusively. The spokes of the wheel, comet etc are there, but subtlety woven into the design. This model is dripping with Arabesque intrigue, Zonn’s accoutrements being many, varied and detailed. Then you get to his mount, oh my. Truly a Chimera of mythical proportions, with two heads; one a goat and the other a bird, the feet of both, large, feathered wings and a cobra for a tail! The sculps on these animal features are really otherworldly and magickal and the whole ensemble comes together very, very well. It stalks a scenic base that sits neatly in a 90mm oval and leaves room around for some grass, sand, basing.

Erasmus Zonn, the Enlightened One
Credit: @badusernametag

It’s rare that a model like this comes along which fits in the range’s aesthetic whilst simultaneously being its own outstanding standalone model. This model would fit into so many other settings, games and scenarios. It stands out as one of the most interesting and compelling models I have seen in years and I cannot wait to paint it. 

Erasmus Zonn, the Enlightened One
Credit: @badusernametag

Buy this model.

Sadly I must say that the instructions are a little ill-conceived and one should be careful with a really intricate and fiddly model. I nearly lost one of the halves of the snake head on the floor and you’re not replacing those parts in a hurry.

The Instruction book weirdly jumps from 2a straight to 2e and 2b-2d are in fact overleaf on the backpage?! Couldn’t figure this out and it just seems to be a mistake. So read through it all before you start and proceed with care. 

Page 1- 1a- 2a 

Pretty straightforward and if followed in order and all goes together well, with excellent registration. 

Page 2 2b- 2d (Hidden on the Back)

This is trickier. They want you to put the Wizzard on first, but:
  • His seat upright assembly (2c) pops in behind him, and is tight
  • Then his Stuff (2d) not only has to pop in behind, but 
  • Also the ends of the scroll and the book have to line up with parts of the seat back in 2c.
Almost think he should be built with seat back, gear and all and THEN stuck on. If I had another go I’d probably tackle it that way. Rather than fight the scroll/book ends to try to get them to line up straight at the end.

Page 3 2e- 2f

Wings and base, easy.

This is not an easy model to assemble (for a plastic hero), but I guess the ritual of deciphering the backwards destructions and then the trial of assemblies is part of the road to mystical enlightenment. Or something?

This is my fave model of the year so far. Get it. Prosper.

Final Thoughts

Three magical weirdos of the mortal realms, three big wins for me. Age of Sigmar is all about it’s heroes and stand out character models and the free cities are really starting to shine as a winner in the epic character model stakes. These guys certainly outshine the Stormcast Eternals heroes, for me at least. Although perhaps that is because mortal, human heroes (even if they’re mysterious and powerful Wizzards) have a certain charm that more naturally dramatic armies might not.

The one thing that is missing in this group is some more representation, as all these models present as male. Arguably Zonn is ambiguous and shrouded in mysterious mystery, but they are all quite male. Having said that the centerpiece of the Cities range is a woman, as is the Pontifex of the Great Wheel herself! So the range already has some strong female heroes, as well as good representation in all the units we’ve seen in this release and previously.

I’m really impressed with these models; the infantry do a lot of heavy lifting for their size and Erasmus Zonn just blows me away with its presence, theme and composition.

A magickal release, indeed.

Wizzard Sprues!!
