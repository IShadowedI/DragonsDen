BattleTech Mech Overview: BattleAxe

May 6th, 2026 via Goonhammer
Back Breaker shook with a groaning creak as we slogged through the growing snow drifts that choked the pass towards Kite. The bobbing headlights of the rest of my Lance around us did little to cut through the howling snow storm that clawed and crept at the edges of Breaker’s viewscreen. The only solace I had was that at least the frigid air would keep the cockpit from finally managing to cook me.

If the Marshals didn’t do it first, of course.

None of us was sure what Major Josiah was up to. What his play was in all this. He was refusing to cough up what the job was, who pitted us on the target. Just handed off his old, decrepit, Thunderbolt to Jameson and eagerly hopped into a shiny new Coyotl; all the while chattering about how big a pay day we were all gonna net once we got back to the Broken Tooth.

I didn't like this one bit. The Major’s promised big payday or not. 

Breaker kept on trundling on though, scared or not I wasn’t going to leave the man out to dry. He’d been too good to me for too long to do that. Been through too many hard situations to give up on whatever plan he had. Even if he might’ve deserved it this time.

“Look alive, people.” The Major’s voice drifted over the ancient HICK-1 mounted to the cockpit’s roof, his Coyotl snapping to look up at the distant ridge to our east. “Looks like we’ve got Marshals ahead on the ridge, bearing 104. One Lance, got some heavy assets in there. Stay tight and knock them on their ass.”

“Aye-Aye, Major.” The old Thunderbolt on my right sped up, Jameson’s chirping voice full of eager excitement as he broke from my side to punch headlong towards the awaiting killers. “Deal with ‘em in no ti-”

“Jameson, you get your ass back in formation now.” I growled, shivering fingers reaching up to flick the conduit bypasses on Breaker’s gun systems to the ARM position. “Graduating from a Commando to a Thunderbolt don’t make you king of the mountain. Keep you fucking head.”

“Fine, just… pick it up…” The younger man muttered in begrudging agreement before slowing again, waiting for me to catch up as he never deviated his cockpit from the figures thundering down the mountainside towards us. 

“Besides, they’ve already spotted us and it’d be a shame not to start with something to put them on the backfoot.” A grin split my face as I hauled Breaker’s targeting reticle around past the Lance, laying her quartet of missile bins on the peak of the snow capped mountain. The computer in her guts hummed and clicked for a few long moments before screaming a defiant, joyous tone and letting the missiles fly free with a ripple of popping kicker motors. “Wonder how well these boys practice avalanche control.”
  • Sargent Kelvin Bingham, Dog Head  Mercenary Company Pirate Band, Battle Axe BKX-7NC Pilot. Rockwellawan, Fronc Reaches, April 4th 3131.  
Howdy Mechwarriors and welcome back to another Mech Overview! This week we once again delve deep into the archives of the world of ‘Mech development with one of the predecessors of the Warhammer, the BattleAxe! Yes, it is capitalized like that, no I do not know why. Its designation code is also BKX, for some fucking reason. There’s not even a K in that name, where’d it come from?

I’ll be honest, I don’t know where they dug this thing up out of for us to look at, I presume the Filtvelts must’ve had a few of them but still… Did I need to be shown God's own LRM weirdo mistake? The BattleAxe is one of the old OG Battle ‘Mechs, with the introduction of the standard tech variant coming to light in 2475, and its earlier prototype primitive build rolling off the line in 2459.

So, who built this thing? Well, simple: The Davions, namely First Prince Simon Davion cut a deal with the Lyrans to get intel on how to build a Battle ‘Mech and contracted Achernar BattleMechs… bit presumptive of a name considering it was their first shot but I’ll give it to ‘em, they certainly built something.

BattleAxe. Credit: Rockfish

Chassis

The BattleAxe, like the other ‘Mechs so far in the Proliferation Cycle, serves as a glimpse back in time at the design ethos of those who built her. A fat, kind of slow heavy, armed with too many guns and not enough heat sinking seems kind of like the Davion’s MO, at least in some ways.

The Battle Axe, at least 3 of the 4 variants, all share the same baseline layout so we can run through those for now.
  • 66% armor for a 70 Ton ‘Mech, meaning 144 Pips.
  • Two big energy guns, one in each arm.
  • Some missiles up on the shoulders.
  • 14 armor on the arms, 16 on the legs, 18 on the side torsos, 20 on the CT, and 6/7/6 on the rear.
Invigorating.

Variants

These mechs have all been reviewed based on a standard F through S scale, which you can find described on our landing page here (along with all of our other ‘mech reviews, the name of the box you can buy to get any of the mechs we have covered, and our general methodology).

BKX-1X

Starting off… well we’re certainly starting off.

The Primitive 1X is honestly the best of the early battle axes and it’s for exactly one reason: Heat sinking. The 1X packs in:
  • One(1) large laser in each arm
  • One(1) LRM-5 on each shoulder with a shared ton of ammo in the left torso
  • Two Machine Guns, one CT one head, with a half ton of ammo in the left torso.
  • An SRM-6 in the RIght torso.
  • 18 Single Heat Sinks
You can meander around the battlefield at 3/5 popping off both large lasers and an LRM-5 to be heat neutral, or toss the other one on and build movement heat and when you get close sub the LRMs for the SRM 6 and you’ve got a fine enough firing pattern. Also the machine guns are here, for fun and activities.

And because the infantry man tax must be paid.

For 1,079 BV I honestly don’t hate this, and I hate to say that this is, in fact, the most competent the Battle Axe will be for a little while.

Liberty’s Rating: C-. It’s a cheap heavy that does stuff at people, ineffective as it may be, until they make it stop. You can make it work, it’s just not gonna be a thrilling game while you do. Well, until it turns into a pyre and detonates from an ammo crit.

BKX-7K

Oh god it’s fuckin’ hot in here and the Jump Jets keep failing mid arc. Help.

The 7K takes the 1X and instead of continuing on with the actually kind of good and cool idea of being the red shifted Warhammer and using Large Lasers instead of PPCs it just… well it tries to harsh the Whammer 6R’s vibe by being it but worse. Same tonnage, same main guns, less armor, less heat sinking, and less secondary guns…

For 30 more BV. I can’t believe I’m saying this but if you have to choose some random Succ War era 70 ton heavy with two PPCs then take a Warhammer 6R instead of this. There’s a reason this is the predecessor.

The heat is just bad, both PPCs is up 5+movement so you can just do one of them, both LRMs and be Movement -1 but this is not an equivalent exchange and it means we’re paying for a second PPC for… vibes, I guess? For 1,329 BV there’s a lot of other things I’d rather be using than this thing. Also it still has the same dog shit armor as the 1X, the switch from Primitive to Standard tech, the 3 heat sinks that went missing, and the machine guns sent to the shadow realm were used explicitly to buy a 4/6/4 movement profile.

Cool, the bare minimum.

Liberty’s Rating: D. Holy shit this is comically bad. Ditch the Jump jets and buy some heat sinks and armor, I beg of you.

BKX-7NC

I… yea, sure man.

So the 7NC takes a 7K and drops the jump jets. Cool, good, this is what I asked for. Did they buy armor and heat sinks to make a vaguely functional ‘Mech?

No? Ohhh, how awful.

Two more LRM-5s, you say?

No extra armor or Heat Sinks, you say?

1,252 BV you say?

To shreds, you say?

Get this thing out of my fucking sight.

Liberty’s Rating: F+. Come on man, this is just ass. Why is this the way that it is? I understand it’s the transitional period of ‘Mech design where people were figuring things out but the basic arithmetic of fucking Thermo-dynamics and armor to tonnage rations are still the same. Just… Stop having it be that fucking hot and easy to stave in the head of.

BKX-8D

Wait, what the fuck? Is this… a real ‘Mech? A real modern ‘Mech?

Oh bitchin’.

So the 8D is a very funny path to take with the idea of the BattleAxe. With a Light fusion engine, a full load of Light Ferro Fibrous armor, and the same 15 heat sinks, but double now, we’ve got some space to spare here. So, what’d we buy in armament wise?
  • 4 IS ERMLs, 2 on each arm
  • A C3 Slave in the Head
  • 3 MML 7s, 2 RT 1 LT, with 4 tons of ammo in a CASE protected bay in the left torso.
  • 4 Jump Jets
All that for 1,592 BV? I dig it. An alpha strike will send you up 2+ movement which means you drop some ERMLs every now and again to let the ‘Mech burn off the excess and get right back to the SRM LRM beating this thing can dispense.

Fuckin’ Slick. This is great utility, a good brickish stature, and some real oddball shit that weirdo platforms like this from the dawn of time should be used for.

More like this please.

Oh… we’re out of BattleAxes? Uh… huh. Ok then.

Liberty’s Rating: B-. Honestly between price, standing utility, and the capacity present here to just unmake someone who’s had their armor blown I can’t really say anything bad about it. This thing fucks, hard.

Final Thoughts

The BattleAxe has two good variants, the Prototype, and the 8D. If you have a BattleAxe and want to run it then do those. The other two will just make you incredibly sad, I assure you.

As for what’s missing, I think I’d love to see some real oddball shit go on with this, like I said. Weirdo ‘Mechs that have been functionally relegated to just their OG faction, Mercs, and a random Periphery State, should get real weird and fucky Variants. I’m talkin’ test bed mixed tech fuck shit that just… shouldn’t be yet persists on to get off the production line. A Re Engineered Laser Platform that’s a straight refit package for old models found in storage. All the odd ‘oh we had a depot of these on a random world that we erroneously struck from the records’ ideas that pop into your head? Give me those.

Until next time! Fair weather, good seas and happy never-matching-up-to-the-Warhammer-ing!
